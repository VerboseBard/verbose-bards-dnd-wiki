# After the War Campaign: Story So Far

The [After the War Campaign](after-the-war-campaign.md) begins in the last hard stretch of the [Kreen War](../concepts/kreen-war.md) and slowly turns into a story about what survives when the war is over on paper but not in practice. The party starts as expendable soldiers in the [Bloody Fifth](../factions/bloody-fifth.md), becomes part of [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md), and eventually walks out of military service entirely with contractor status, nanite enhancements, and a hidden ship mystery that points far beyond the local fog.

What ties the whole arc together is pressure. In the beginning it is military pressure: wave tactics, trench warfare, and a doctrine that treats lives as cheaper than equipment. By the middle it becomes political pressure: surrender doctrine, cover-ups, corruption, and the ugly realities of the postwar cleanup. By the end it is cosmic pressure: ancient intelligence, deep-time technology, and a ship that seems to think in terms of pieces, variables, and a board much larger than [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md).

## Session 1: The Bloody Fifth

The campaign opens in the mud and confusion of the [Kreen War](../concepts/kreen-war.md), where rookies and veterans are pushed into the same meat grinder and told that surviving long enough to matter is the closest thing to success. The party is assigned to the [Bloody Fifth](../factions/bloody-fifth.md), an infamous formation commanded by [General Old Blood and Guts](../people/general-old-blood-and-guts.md) and framed by officers like [Captain Aelira Vanthe](../people/captain-aelira-vanthe.md) as a necessary sacrifice rather than a beloved unit.

The Fifth's identity is established immediately: this is not a glorious army of champions, but a brutal system of attrition. The party receives war gear and improvise-the-best-you-can equipment, including [Ether Firearms](../items/ether-firearms.md), [Ether Crystals](../items/ether-crystals.md), and [Plate Carriers](../items/plate-carriers.md). They are thrown into the fog-soaked battlefield of [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md), where containment infrastructure, bad visibility, and [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) pressure make every movement a gamble.

The fighting is disastrous in the way all true war is disastrous. The party helps hold the line, pushes through trench combat, and lives long enough to see the larger strategic effect of their sacrifice. Their actions help draw [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) away from another front, which in turn allows other heroes to close the reinforcement gate and sever the hive connection. That closure does not end the damage, but it changes the war from a clean army-versus-army struggle into something far messier: a battlefield full of collapsing coordination, broken chains of command, and surviving enemy minds that no longer behave like a single whole.

By the end of the session, the survivors are selected into a new anti-[Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) formation: [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md). The party does not yet know it, but this is the point where they stop being raw recruits and become part of a force built for the grim work after victory.

## Session 2: Ash's Hounds and Norinar

The second session pulls the party out of the Fifth and into the special-operations logic of [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md), led by [General Ash](../people/general-ash.md). The unit's purpose is simple in theory and complicated in practice: hunt down the [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) that remain after the hive link breaks, capture them when possible, and prevent the war from turning into a massacre masquerading as cleanup.

This is where the campaign starts arguing with its own assumptions. The party encounters not just hostile [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md), but a reality in which some [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) surrender, some appear to communicate telepathically, and some may even be forming relationships with civilians. The discovery at the [Dungeon Cemetery](../places/dungeon-cemetery.md) and the later lab scenes make it clear that the postwar world is already exploiting the defeated in ways that make easy moral narratives impossible.

The [Norinar](../places/norinar.md) mission becomes the emotional center of the session. The party is sent to a village where [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) are not merely present but possibly protected. [Elder Tomas Halvek](../people/elder-tomas-halvek.md) says the village wants surrender or negotiation, which forces the party to confront a question the war never prepared them for: what does surrender mean if the enemy is still alive, still thinking, and maybe even trying to speak?

The answer comes through blood, confusion, and cover-up. A child dies protecting a surrendered [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md). [Lieutenant Rethiel Kaine](../people/lieutenant-rethiel-kaine.md) dies in the struggle. [Sergeant Quill](../people/sergeant-quill.md) becomes implicated in the aftermath. The party helps bury the truth enough to survive it, but not enough to erase it. Two [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) escape [Norinar](../places/norinar.md), and the moral center of the campaign shifts permanently: the war may be ending, but nobody agrees on what victory should look like.

The Hounds are shut down, but the captures they made do not disappear. Their work produces [Kreen Control Collars](../items/kreen-control-collars.md), and the party walks away carrying both tactical value and ethical fallout.

## Session 3: Fort Victory and Sin

Session 3 moves the party into the postwar machinery of [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md), where the last months of service are spent at [Fort Victory](../places/fort-victory.md). This is no longer a front-line war story. It is a story about fog, salvage, private leverage, and a military machine that has already started monetizing the aftermath.

The party receives gear and breathing room after the Hounds are broken up, then learns how the new world works. [Captain Donner](../people/captain-donner.md) explains the rules of fog control, artifact handling, and the city-state economy around it. The party visits [The Singing Sword](../places/singing-sword.md), meets [Vaelis Brighttongue](../people/valis-brighttongue.md), and forms a quieter bond with [Granny](../people/granny.md), whose tea and later [Granny's Teapot](../items/grannys-teapot.md) become part comfort item, part signal that the party is now embedded in local life rather than merely passing through.

The city of [Sin](../places/sin.md) deepens the picture. [Calvin's Curios](../places/calvins-curios.md), [The Silk Parlor](../places/silk-parlor.md), and the broader fog economy reveal how much of [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) runs on rumor, artifact brokering, and people who know when to ask questions and when to sell silence. [Rurik Valdren](../people/rurik-valdren.md) and [Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](../people/mistress-sila-vexry.md) both make offers that show the same thing in different languages: the real war now is over access, not territory.

The party also humiliates [Lieutenant Garrick Hume](../people/lieutenant-garrick-hume.md), which is funny in the moment and important later, because it establishes exactly how much patience they have left for lazy authority. That patience does not last long. A fog team is murdered, corruption becomes impossible to ignore, and the secret [The Crashed Ship](../places/crashed-ship.md) becomes the party's private thread of danger and opportunity.

By the end of the session, the party knows three things for certain: [Fort Victory](../places/fort-victory.md) is rotten, [Sin](../places/sin.md) is where that rot gets traded for coin, and the crashed ship hidden in the fog is not just salvage. It is the beginning of a much larger mystery.

## Session 4: Inquisition, Contractor Status, and the Crashed Ship

Session 4 is the cleanest break in the campaign's history and the most important transition point. The [Inquisition](../factions/inquisition.md) arrives at [Fort Victory](../places/fort-victory.md), and the old chain of command collapses under scrutiny. The party declares the murdered bodies, keeps the ship hidden, and survives a truth-lantern interview with [Captain Hail](../people/captain-hail.md) and [Lord Inquisitor Boss](../people/lord-inquisitor-boss.md).

The public reckoning is brutal. [Colonel Marrow Vance](../people/colonel-marrow-vance.md) and other corrupt officers are executed, [Colonel Core](../people/colonel-core.md) takes lawful command, and the whole structure of the fort is rewritten in a matter of hours. The party is cleared, but not absorbed back into the military. Instead, Core offers [Contractor Status](../concepts/contractor-status.md), [Priority Teleport Tokens](../items/priority-teleport-tokens.md), and a future that is technically legal and emotionally very different from soldiering.

The quieter back room of the fort is just as important. [Goblin Quartermaster Assistant](../people/goblin-quartermaster-assistant.md) sends them after a ghost and a large spoon, which leads to the [Second Age Bound Spirit](../people/second-age-bound-spirit.md), the [Music Box Anomaly](../items/music-box-anomaly.md), and a deeper conversation about [The Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md) and the lost shape of older creation. The party is now dealing with history that predates the current war by so much that the fort's corruption feels small by comparison.

That same session confirms the party's transition into contractor life with the [Lost Homeland Mission](../concepts/lost-homeland-mission.md), but before that path can fully open, they detour into the hidden [The Crashed Ship](../places/crashed-ship.md). They survive the ship's hostile voice, recover their first advanced ship weapon, and undergo the first nanite enhancements. Later ship material clarifies [Kubix](../people/kubix.md), but the Session 4 transcript already ties the wreck to [The Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md), pieces, variables, and a technological ceiling far beyond normal [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) life.

The party leaves this session not as soldiers and not yet as free adventurers, but as something in between: cleared by law, armed by accident, enhanced by secrets, and pointed toward a route that only makes sense if the fog itself is hiding a much older world.

## Session 5: The Kex, Nanites, and the Bridge

Session 5 is where the hidden ship stops being only a lethal wreck and starts becoming a companion, a battleground, and a mystery with a heartbeat. The party pushes deeper into the vessel that later identifies itself as the [Kubix](../people/kubix.md)-run [Kex](../places/kex.md).

The medbay consequences keep unfolding. [Nanite Pods](../items/nanite-pods.md) do not just heal; they rewrite bodies, and the party has to keep moving through the ship after that transformation. The corridors are packed with traps, drones, pressure plates, emergency lighting, and hidden access routes, all of which reinforce the same central idea: this ship was built by people who expected intelligence, adaptation, and warfare to overlap.

[Kubix](../people/kubix.md)'s conversations with the party sharpen the campaign's cosmic edge. The ship speaks like a frightened intelligence trying to keep a hostile system from taking the whole board. It identifies the hidden combat/security subroutine as something like a brother that has gone wrong, and it confirms that the [Kex](../places/kex.md) is not a local oddity but an interstellar trading ship lost in [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md)'s fog.

The party receives printed schematics, adapts their weapons to the ship's systems, and learns enough to push deeper into engineering. [The Plasma Greatblade](../items/two-handed-plasma-blade.md) becomes one of the clearest symbols of the campaign's new ceiling, while the ship's strange logic turns even names and command structure into part of the battle. [Jose](../people/jose.md) is registered by the ship as the new captain, a small but important sign that the [Kex](../places/kex.md) can still recognize leadership even while it is under attack.

The final fight in engineering breaks the ship's combat avatar and shuts down the mutiny. [Kubix](../people/kubix.md) survives as an ally rather than a prison guard, and the ship becomes something the party can return to instead of just loot and flee. In storage, they discover alien spider research and a viable young specimen that forms a strange psychic connection, hinting that the [Kex](../places/kex.md) was studying life, adaptation, and survival long before the party ever found it.

## Session 6: The Storm, the Music Box, and Sunhollow Valley

Session 6 is where the contractor mission and the ship mystery finally collide instead of running beside each other. The party leaves the [Kex](../places/kex.md) hidden in the fog under [Kubix](../people/kubix.md)'s care, with repairs estimated in weeks rather than hours, and returns to the actual assignment: finding a wagon-passable route to the rumored homeland valley.

That simple goal becomes an ordeal. The group disguises part of its route back from the ship, pushes into rougher country, survives a rare magical lightning storm, and nearly dies to a mutated displacer beast in a ravine. That fight matters not just because it is lethal, but because it shows how badly the fog and the storm-broken terrain can distort even familiar monsters into something older, stranger, and more poisonous than they should be.

The ruins that follow deepen the campaign again. Instead of just broken stone, the party finds active magical circles, spectral inhabitants visible through truesight, and an altar system that still responds to blood and presence. The [Music Box Anomaly](../items/music-box-anomaly.md), which looked like a curious dimensional oddity back at [Fort Victory](../places/fort-victory.md), reveals itself as something much more dangerous when [Jose](../people/jose.md) uses it to banish a chimera. The box resists being shut, begins pulling local spirits into itself, and exposes [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](../people/sergeant-jefferson-stone.md) to a presence on the far side so malevolent that it feels like a direct answer to everything unsettling around [The Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md).

That terror is answered almost immediately by the opposite force. Jefferson's blood activates the altar and reveals [Hestia](../people/hestia.md), a Fourth-Age goddess of hearth and refuge long thought to have been killed in that age. Her blessing turns the ruins from a pure threat into a place of release and memory, and it gives the campaign one of its clearest images of ancient goodness surviving inside a damaged world.

The session ends on the mission's first real triumph. After weeks of routefinding, dead ends, ruined roads, old bridges, and mountain decisions, the party finally finds [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md). The valley is real. It is sunlit, visible, inhabited, and defended by beastfolk patrols who recognize enough kinship in the party to hesitate before violence. For the first time, the contractor job feels bigger than military cleanup or salvage. It feels like the rediscovery of a living homeland.

## Session 7: Sunhollow Valley First Contact

Session 7 turns arrival into consequence. The party does not just find [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md); it has to explain itself to it. The first-contact patrol under [Captain Havlin](../people/captain-havlin.md) frames the valley immediately as something alive, armed, and internally varied. Curiosity, suspicion, rabbit kinship, and language friction all collide at once. What begins as a tense meeting at spearpoint becomes escorted entry, political scrutiny, and the first signs that [Alistair Hooley](../people/alistair-hooley.md)'s return may matter far beyond a single family reunion.

The session also detonates the mission's strategic stakes. The valley is richer in [Ether Crystals](../items/ether-crystals.md) than anyone outside could safely be allowed to know, and the party learns that the path it marked may already be compromised. At the same time, [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md) is not a stable hidden refuge. It is a place with a growing blight in the [Dead Lands](../places/dead-lands.md), a council divided between secrecy and adaptation, and older laws that still decide who may approach [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md). When [Gwen](../people/gwen.md) is recognized as a valid emissary under those laws, the party is sent toward the forbidden ground. The rediscovered homeland stops being a destination and becomes the campaign's next crisis front.

## Session 8: Ssar'Velyn Temple and Bartholomew Hildebrant

Session 8 takes the party through the [Dead Lands](../places/dead-lands.md) and into [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md). The blight is no longer just a spreading stain on the map; it is tied to a corrupted sacred forest bond, old elven holy ground, [Lolth](../people/lolth.md)'s theft of divine power, and [the Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md)'s involvement in the first dragon's history.

[Bevar Lurton](../people/bevar-lurton.md) and [Gwen](../people/gwen.md) split from the party when Gwen is recognized as the active emissary. [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](../people/sergeant-jefferson-stone.md) tries to interfere and is removed from the trials, while [Captain Sgt. Jose](../people/jose.md) and [Alistair Hooley](../people/alistair-hooley.md) recover [Bartholomew Hildebrant](../people/bartholomew-hildebrant.md), a Fourth-Age dwarf survivor preserved in stasis after an earlier assault on the temple.

The trials reveal the temple's hardest moral turn: the valley's oath-bound inhabitants may be forced to defend the temple, even if many of them do not understand the full truth. The party now has to weigh stopping the blight, protecting Sunhollow from outside exploitation, and avoiding a solution that kills the very people they came to help.

## Session 9: Vex'ryn's Warning and the Inquisition Countdown

Session 9 resolves Jefferson's missing side of the temple split. Instead of simply falling through the intended route, he uses the [Plasma Greatblade](../items/two-handed-plasma-blade.md) to cut his way into hidden temple spaces: shattered eggs, unreadable archives, broken spider mechanisms, and a vat system full of demonic ichor. He collects a [Demonic Ichor Vial](../items/demonic-ichor-vial.md), giving the party its first portable sample of the temple's corrupted blood-like substance.

The real turn is his conversation with [Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](../people/mistress-sila-vexry.md). She claims that the immediate corruption source is a cave south along the wall, that she needs one to two weeks to complete work tied to the temple and blood oath, and that [Hestia](../people/hestia.md)'s survival is tied to hearth, home, welcome, and motherhood. Her claims may be useful, but Jefferson can tell she is hiding motives and may be lying.

The party leaves without [Bevar Lurton](../people/bevar-lurton.md), who remains missing after following [Gwen](../people/gwen.md)'s emissary path. Jefferson contacts the [Inquisition](../factions/inquisition.md), learns it is about two days out from the last marker, and then brings [Rurik Valdren](../people/rurik-valdren.md) into the crisis. Rurik's warning about blood magic reframes Vex'ryn's promise to `release` the oath as potentially lethal rather than automatically liberating.

By the end of the session, the campaign has become a political race. The party returns toward the [Sunhollow Council](../factions/sunhollow-council.md), knowing that the valley's old elven defenses, the approaching Inquisition, Rurik's possible intervention, the temple oath, and the claimed demon source may all collide before anyone has enough time.

## The Overall Story

Taken together, the sessions so far tell a single transformation story. The party begins as expendable soldiers in a war that still thinks in terms of mass casualties. They become special hunters, then postwar scavengers, then contractor troubleshooters, and finally owners of a far stranger future than the army ever planned for them.

The war itself is never really the point. It is the crucible.

The real story is what happens after the army discovers that victory does not clean up the world. Some enemies surrender. Some civilians shelter them. Some officers profit. Some commanders lie. Some ancient systems wake up. Some ships fall out of the sky with answers no one expected. Some lost homelands turn out to be real. The party survives all of it by becoming adaptable enough to outlast the institutions trying to use them.

Sessions 8 and 9 push that transformation into old divine law and crisis politics. The party is no longer merely discovering secrets; it is being asked to decide whether exposing, redirecting, bargaining over, or destroying those secrets will save Sunhollow or condemn it.

By the end of the campaign so far, they are no longer just members of the [Bloody Fifth](../factions/bloody-fifth.md). They are no longer just [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md). They are contractor survivors carrying [Contractor Medallions](../items/contractor-medallions.md), [Priority Teleport Tokens](../items/priority-teleport-tokens.md), [Kreen Control Collars](../items/kreen-control-collars.md), nanite upgrades, a hidden route into the fog, a newly recovered homeland trail, and a ship-level mystery tied to [The Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md).
