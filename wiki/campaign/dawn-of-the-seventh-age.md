# Dawn of the Seventh Age

The Dawn of the Seventh Age is an early [Seventh Age](../concepts/seventh-age.md) campaign arc for the post-[Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md) era.

## Opening Monologue

The people of the linked worlds call this era the [Age of Heroes](../concepts/age-of-heroes.md).

At the end of the [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md), the Great Deceiver, the Destroyer of Worlds, was finally defeated. But his ruinous legacy could not be undone. The realm was nearly shattered, worlds collided, the skies were torn asunder, and life as it once was ended.

From that ending came a new beginning. Guided by the Architect of Worlds, the greatest minds from across the sundered worlds came together. Like a stained-glass mosaic or a patchwork quilt, they pieced together what remained, forging a single fragile world from the remnants of many.

To keep the new reality from unraveling, they bound it with ley lines. At each nexus point, the weave grew unstable. To protect those junctions, they created pocket dimensions where excess mana manifested as guardians, elemental defenses, and unknowable beasts. Those unintended sealed spaces became dungeons.

While the [Conclave of Churches](../factions/conclave-of-churches.md) debated how to divide and rule the newborn world, others acted: explorers, outcasts, adventurers, and refugees. They began mapping the unknown. They founded camps, towns, and trade routes. When one returned alive from a leyline nexus bearing treasure, the world changed overnight.

Where once there was fear, now there was purpose. Where once there was chaos, now there was profit.

Thus rose the [Adventurer's Guild](../factions/adventurers-guild.md), a banner not of kings but of earned renown. Scholars call it the Age of [Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md). The people call it the [Age of Heroes](../concepts/age-of-heroes.md).

## Campaign Identity

This arc covers the earliest organized dungeon culture of the [Seventh Age](../concepts/seventh-age.md):

- guild members
- independent rescuers
- unaffiliated divers
- explorers of the unknown
- opportunists and outlaws

It begins at [Caldrith's Gate](../places/caldriths-gate.md), where the party enters the Guild system as Bronze 3-Stars.

## Overview

The campaign opens with the worlds freshly remade after the [Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md) and the age of heroes just beginning to take shape. The old divine order has not returned, but the new one is not stable yet either. The Conclave is trying to control the newborn world, the [Adventurer's Guild](../factions/adventurers-guild.md) is still defining its reach, and dungeons have become both danger and livelihood.

The party arrives at [Caldrith's Gate](../places/caldriths-gate.md) and enters that system at the lowest city-ready tier with a Bronze 3-Star reputation. Their first chapter is about proving they actually belong in the city-tier Guild structure rather than burning out in the first set of assignments. That means testing rank, hearing the local politics, surviving pressure from higher-ranked adventurers, and taking on [Spireblack Hollow](../places/spireblack-hollow.md) under classified conditions.

The opening arc ends with the party earning access to harder, more dangerous work while learning that the real problem is not just the dungeon itself but the leak, the deaths around the mission schedule, and the way the Guild manages risk. The campaign launches the age rather than resolving it.
