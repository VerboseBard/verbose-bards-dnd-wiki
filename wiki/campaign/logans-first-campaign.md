# Logan's First Campaign

Logan's First Campaign is the earliest currently tracked [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) party thread in the wiki, beginning roughly three weeks before [Logan's Second Campaign](logans-second-campaign.md).

## Description

The surviving material comes from [Quill](../people/quill.md)'s notes, dated from July 25, 2020 through January 23, 2021. The notes do not yet form a full session-by-session archive, but they clearly outline a campaign built around divine and infernal interference, Wilbrix intrigue, a shipwrecked tower-island, undead and illithid threats, and the party's contact with [Dione](../people/dione.md), [Jinx](../people/jinx.md), and other old-power networks.

The [War of the Gods / New Beginning Campaign](war-of-the-gods.md) remains its own later [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) campaign page.

## Party Anchor

The notes currently center on [Quill](../people/quill.md), an arcane-capable adventurer who uses levitation, summons a sea hawk familiar, tracks magical items closely, purchases spell-copying materials, and moves comfortably through libraries, artificer spaces, and occult negotiation.

Quill's companions in the surviving notes are:

- Divok, a half-orc cleric with weather, water, healing, and latent merfolk-linked power
- Thane, an undead rogue tied to angelic remnants, undeath, and dangerous bargaining
- Tamiel, an aasimar paladin with a prior connection to the celestial being who confronts the party

## Opening Crisis

The campaign notes begin after Thane kills a merchant in a cave. Quill holds a box containing a knife and an hourglass, and two supernatural powers immediately converge on the group.

A celestial being connected to Tamiel demands the artifacts and offers payment. A devil woman also appears and tries to bargain for them. Thane, harmed simply by the celestial's presence, panics and tries to hand over the box. Quill responds quietly by levitating the artifact bag toward the ceiling while the others argue.

The confrontation ends with the devil dead and the celestial destroying the knife and hourglass. From the start, the campaign is not only about treasure or dungeon danger; it is about rival higher powers fighting over objects important enough to draw direct intervention.

## Wilbrix and Connor Lynch

After the artifact crisis, the party's trail leads back toward Wilbrix and the mage Connor Lynch, who had previously contracted Quill's master.

The notes later place Connor hidden on the docks at Wilbrix behind protections that block magical and divine sight. That makes him more than a routine local enemy. He is operating under unusually serious concealment, in a campaign where divine sight, prophecy, and planar interference are already active.

## The Scarlet and the Black Tower Island

One of the major middle arcs begins aboard the ship Popillol, traveling west along the coast toward Wilbrix while a fleet pursues. The allied ship Scarlet buys the party time by deploying a mage-powered chain-lightning cannon that destroys several enemy ships.

Divok's control of wind and water helps the party escape, but also accidentally summons real merfolk after he creates the illusion of them. During the storm that follows, the ship glances off rocks, sailors are injured, and then the ship and surrounding water are magically teleported to an island.

The island is dominated by sheer, flat-topped cliffs and a black tower with no obvious doors or windows. The party finds dead sailors, rubbery hand-mouthed creatures, a skull cave, a construct beast collecting bodies, and signs that the place has been pulling people in for a long time.

## Undead Halls and Strange Machinery

Inside the cave and tower complex, the party encounters undead sorcerers, a death-linked spectre, dining chambers, storerooms, cells, gardens, and secret rooms filled with dangerous machinery.

The notes include several important discoveries:

- an avatar or spectre tied to Orcus and undeath
- a skeleton animated enough to speak about a power trapped there and looking for a way out
- a summoning glove with elemental binding properties
- a magic rifle powered by a capacitor in the stock
- cylinders containing brains, swords, and magic rifles
- controls that open a passage through a void-like ring
- illithid attackers carrying psychic-focus daggers and squid clasps

This arc makes Logan's First Campaign one of the earliest surviving sources for the setting's recurring pattern of sealed experiments, undead power, strange crafted devices, and people trapped between divine, infernal, and technological systems.

## Dione, Jinx, and the Game Room

By January 9, 2021, the party reaches the manor of [Dione](../people/dione.md), halfling seer and oracle of Wilbrix. Time has behaved strangely around the party's disappearance: Dione has been gone only days locally, but experienced centuries elsewhere, while the party has lost negative days locally but experienced months in suspension.

At Dione's manor, Quill meets Alauren, aasimar captain of Dione's guard, passes through spaces larger inside than outside, and enters a craftsmen's corridor where [Jinx](../people/jinx.md) appears in an artificer role. Jinx is tied there to Lord of Blades parts, the cloak of elvenkind, crafted upgrades, and Quill's larger magical development.

The later January notes place Quill in a protected game room filled with anti-cheating magic, mixed currencies, dice games, card games, and a broker named Mr. Sheppard. This adds another layer to the campaign: behind the divine and tower material is a world of brokers, artificers, money, and protected magical commerce.

## Continuity Importance

Logan's First Campaign matters because it establishes several threads that echo into later Fifth-, Sixth-, and Seventh-Age material:

- [Dione](../people/dione.md) is active as a prophetic power with a mortal-facing manor and network.
- [Jinx](../people/jinx.md) appears much earlier than the later [War of the Gods / New Beginning Campaign](war-of-the-gods.md), already acting as an artificer and systems-level fixer.
- Quill survives long enough to be mentioned again in later [Logan's Third Campaign](logans-third-campaign.md) material alongside Jinx.
- The notes already involve Orcus, illithid traces, strange artifact engineering, divine sight-blocking protections, and old-power objects.
