# Logan's Second Campaign

Logan's Second Campaign is the party's follow-up campaign arc, set in the [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) and beginning roughly three years before [The Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md).

## Description

This campaign belongs in the closing stretch of the [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md), beginning about three years before [The Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md), and therefore still long before the [Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md) and the opening of the [Seventh Age](../concepts/seventh-age.md).

The material comes from the OneNote section `My Second Campaign.one`. It contains a linked, decision-tree style campaign structure with major branches, repeat locations, recurring factions, and several names that will matter later.

Some names here overlap with later-setting pages in the main wiki. For now, these notes are treated as their own [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) campaign continuity unless a later source proves they are meant to be the same place or person.

Current user notes also place this campaign about three weeks after [Logan's First Campaign](logans-first-campaign.md), the earlier Fifth-Age party thread represented by [Quill](../people/quill.md)'s notes.

## Overview

This campaign opens in the [Karakum](../places/karakum.md) trade web and widens into a desert-world power struggle centered on [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md), the [Merchant Gild](../places/merchant-gild.md), the [Shipping Gild](../places/shipping-gild.md), and the [Dungeon Divers](../places/dungeon-divers.md). From there it branches outward into arena politics at [Big Tusk](../places/big-tusk.md), the friction of rival trade houses, and the pressure of hidden debts and competing interests.

The early arc establishes the city-state economy and the people who profit from it: merchants, shippers, smugglers, freelancers, and the institutions that try to keep all of that under control. As the thread develops, the scope widens into war with the Drow Elves, the Gods War, and the stranger consequences that follow.

The campaign does not yet have a single clean ending in the wiki, but its major arc points are clear: build the trade network, survive the underworld around [Big Tusk](../places/big-tusk.md), and then carry the consequences into the later war threads.

The surviving party-note material now also confirms at least one central character from that arc directly: [Hawk](../people/hawk.md), whose notes track the campaign from Clipper Bay, Wilbrix, and Salvation Point through the road to [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md).

## Hawk Notes Expansion

[Hawk](../people/hawk.md)'s notes show the campaign beginning with a battle in Clipper Bay, where a lord is killed and Dione's light blesses Hawk with knowledge. The early road then leads into Wilbrix, where Pelicanese worship of [Sedna](../people/sedna.md), halfling protests, religious enforcement, and anti-outsider pressure turn the city into a political and spiritual pressure cooker.

Important early figures and threads include:

- Ayden, who changes into a lionman and later shows signs of a darker original form or divided self
- Leonard, the powerful "big cat" contact at the Cat's Paw Inn
- Toby, a halfling orphan tied to Carlton and the party's Wilbrix exit
- Herbert Stonefetter, the half-dwarf hiring members for the expedition to [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md)
- Captain Belmand, the dwarven captain connected to the ship Scarlet
- Erica, a halfling storm sorcerer and first mate

The campaign's middle travel arc moves through Salvation Point, merfolk attacks, troll territory, swamp roads, a slaad encounter, and a magically connected obelisk or stone tower. After the party returns from the swamp with trophies, a dwarf asks their party name, and they settle on "the Revengers."

## Silverbend and Guild Law

Once the party reaches [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md), the notes focus heavily on its ritual and guild structure.

At dusk, locals toast the moon. The notes describe a large orange moon called Dionysus and a larger red moon called Ares that rises roughly three hours later, moves quickly, and eclipses Dionysus. Strange things are seen during that cycle, and looking at the moons can bring blessings, curses, or forced revelations.

The Adventurers Guild is organized through three magic doors labeled Delvers, Treasure, and Trophies. Hawk pays guild fees and gains Treasure and Trophy medallions. The guild law recorded in the notes includes rules about agreed recompense, property and personhood, debtors, bounty hunters, travel permits, and a strikingly blunt rule that no one shall stay their hand from striking down a dwarf.

The Trophy Hall includes minotaurs, centaurs, elves, and more non-satyrs than the party has seen elsewhere. An older satyr named Tarif appears there.

## Ayden, Drow Tension, and Dragon Fallout

Hawk's notes make Ayden one of the campaign's most unstable and dangerous internal threads. Ayden's form changes, lunar exposure reveals or worsens something inside him, and by the Silverbend material Hawk begins to realize how deeply Ayden has manipulated him.

The drow tension grows at the same time. Silverbend high-ranking guild figures named Gabel and Jolel appear in the notes, while the drow are angry about a recent undead incursion and blame "upsiders." Hawk senses a connection between an evil dwarf staff and the undead attack on the drow.

The dragon thread then turns catastrophic. Ayden hatches a brass dragon named Texa Srainjer. Fizban later demands that Ayden prove he can control the darkness inside him or surrender the memory of the dragon. Ayden's darkness chooses to consume the baby dragon rather than lose it, and Fizban's response triggers dragon attacks across the town. The Dungeon Delvers headquarters is destroyed, the Adventurers Guild is damaged, the Treasure Hunters Guild library is destroyed, and the Big Game / Bounty Hunters take damage as well.

## Later Campaign Direction

By the later Hawk notes, the party is dealing with refugees from the dragon attack, drow bodies, blood-scented gear, a +3 ebony dagger, and prophetic dreams from [Dione](../people/dione.md). In one dream, Hawk sees mountains over the sea, a storm of skulls and death eating the earth, and a dark hand reaching toward him while Dione warns that darkness and hard choices are coming.

That material makes the Second Campaign more than a trade-road or guild campaign. It is already pointed toward divine pressure, drow conflict, undead consequences, dragon catastrophe, and the same pre-[Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md) darkness that later Fifth-Age material continues to escalate.
