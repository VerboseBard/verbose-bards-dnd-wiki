# Moon Stone Collectors Elsewares

Elsewares are a Moon Stone Collectors campaign device: cutaway scenes, visions, dreams, ritual views, and off-screen consequences that let the table see parts of the world the active party may not fully understand yet.

This page is the campaign-level hub for those scenes. The quick review index gives the short version. The detailed entries below it explain what happened, why it matters, and which campaign pages connect to the scene.

## Entry 0 Campaign Prologue Fall of the Sixth Age

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 1](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-1.md)

**Type:** Mythic prologue. It is not formally called an Elseware, but it functions like the campaign's opening Elseware.

**Review:** The prologue frames the end of the [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md): gods war for twenty-one days, millions die, [Mulligan / Molokan](../people/mulligan.md)'s betrayal opens the [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md) to catastrophe, and the [Hell's Bane Heroes](../factions/hells-bane-heroes.md) kill [Lucius](../people/lucius.md). [Greg](../people/greg-p-smith.md) then becomes the key figure who makes the [Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md) survivable.

**Campaign tie:** This gives Moon Stone Collectors its historical shadow. The party inherits a world built by other heroes, betrayed by old powers, and held together by desperate world-repair.

**Detailed breakdown:** The scene explains why the Divine Gate, the Citadel, Greg, Lucius, and the shattered worlds are already living problems by the time the Moon Stone Collectors begin. It also ties the campaign back to the [War of the Gods / New Beginning Campaign](war-of-the-gods.md) and forward into [The Hunt for Black Vipers](hunt-for-black-vipers.md).

## Entry 1 Architect of Worlds

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 2](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-2.md) / Session 2-3 source material.

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** The camera leaves the active party and shows heroes from the previous age closing Hellgates and defending the machinery of world-repair. [Jimbo](../people/jimbo.md), [Kestrel](../people/kestrel.md), [Junvara](../people/junvara.md), [Constance and Zuk](../people/constance-l-coggeshall.md), [Charlie](../people/charlie.md), [Greg](../people/greg-p-smith.md), and Jarvis/Jarvice appear in a coordinated final effort to align reality.

**Campaign tie:** This is the clearest cinematic bridge between Moon Stone Collectors and the War of the Gods material. It explains why Greg is not just an inventor, but the Architect of Worlds.

**Detailed breakdown:** Greg receives the ley-line alignment information from [Professor Hazard](../people/professor-hazard.md)'s side of the work, completes the final seconds of stabilization, and then stands before the Creator in an infinite white void. He sees the worlds, ley lines, and soul-flow before being asked what changes he will make.

## Entry 2 Queen Beneath the Waves

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 3](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-3.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** The scene dives into [Opaal](../places/opaal.md)'s Coral Palace. [Mira of Opaal](../people/mira-of-opaal.md) asks [Aurora](../people/aurora-queen-beneath-waves.md) how her father, [Mulligan / Molokan](../people/mulligan.md), could have become a cursed betrayer. Aurora answers with the more complicated Commodore truth.

**Campaign tie:** This scene ties Moon Stone Collectors to the [Commodore Prophecy](../concepts/commodore-prophecy.md), the [Opaal Old One](../concepts/opaal-old-one.md), and later Black Viper-era events.

**Detailed breakdown:** The seal beneath Opaal begins to fail. Aurora uses blood magic in the Coral Palace to call the Commodore back. A corpse regenerates, and the Commodore is reborn as the old one stirs.

## Entry 3 Junvara Veil and the God-Killing Dagger

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 4](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-4.md) and [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 5](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-5.md)

**Type:** Minor-to-major setup Elseware.

**Review:** [Veil](../people/veil-guardian-of-the-citadel.md) cannot leave the crystal directly, so [Junvara](../people/junvara.md) agrees to act as his field agent. Veil gives Junvara a box containing a small blade reshaped from an old god-killing arrow.

**Campaign tie:** This sets up the Maeve trade, Wendell's later punishment, and the continuing importance of god-killing material.

**Detailed breakdown:** Junvara reaches a dead-end alley near a demonic door, where a small demon guide appears. The guide leads him toward the Hell sequence involving Wendell, Maeve, and the bargain that follows.

## Entry 4 Constance Zuk and Annabelles Warning

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 4](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-4.md), recapped in [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 5](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-5.md)

**Type:** Major setup Elseware.

**Review:** [Constance and Zuk](../people/constance-l-coggeshall.md) discuss Greg's changes to reality and realize that the Divine Gate functions as a power gate. Dragons, including their own family, may be forced out of reality unless the magical code is altered.

**Campaign tie:** This sets up the dragon egg rescue and Constance's decision to keep dragons within the remade world.

**Detailed breakdown:** [Annabelle](../people/annabelle.md) appears and connects Constance to crying children trapped with dragon eggs. One of Zuk's young relatives and other children are bound up in the crisis. The scene turns abstract world-repair into a family and hoard emergency.

## Entry 5 Junvara and Jarth in Wendells Black Keep

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 5](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-5.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** [Junvara](../people/junvara.md) and [Jarth](../people/jarth.md) cross Hell with a small demon guide and reach [Wendell's Sixth-Layer Fortress](../places/wendells-sixth-layer-fortress.md). They find [Maeve](../people/mave.md) alive but drained of divine blood.

**Campaign tie:** This establishes Wendell's post-Gate ambition, Maeve's sacrifice, and the Black Keep as one of the campaign's major Hell-side locations.

**Detailed breakdown:** Wendell accepts the god-killing dagger as payment and releases Maeve in a dying state. He also releases a captive who later proves important to Juan's return. Wendell makes clear that his old leash is gone and that he expects the Concurrence to free him from mortal-side control.

## Entry 6 Maeves Transfer Gregs Ascension and Jarvis

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 5](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-5.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** Maeve is brought into the crystal-space around [Veil](../people/veil-guardian-of-the-citadel.md), where her remaining divine energy is spent to transfer her consciousness into another body on the mortal side of the Gate. At the same time, [Greg](../people/greg-p-smith.md) becomes too powerful to remain and ascends.

**Campaign tie:** This explains why Maeve can remain active later despite losing divinity and why Greg becomes a distant divine-level architect rather than a normal party member.

**Detailed breakdown:** Greg leaves behind a small knife-like tool for Junvara and chooses a mortal-side path for Jarvis/Jarvice rather than letting him vanish with the ascension. This choice becomes part of later [Vis / Jarvice](../people/vis-jarvice.md) continuity.

## Entry 7 Concurrence Warehouse and Dragon Egg Rescue

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 5](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-5.md)

**Type:** Major cinematic event with the party present.

**Review:** At the [Shippers and Delivery Guild Warehouse](../places/shippers-and-delivery-guild-warehouse.md), Constance, Zuk, Annabelle, and the Moon Stone Collectors use the peak of the Concurrence to open a route to trapped children and dragon eggs.

**Campaign tie:** This is one of the campaign's defining Concurrence events. It links the Moon Stone Collectors to the survival of dragonkind and to the rescue infrastructure around the Citadel.

**Detailed breakdown:** The portal opens onto an ancient dwarven ruin filled with thousands of dragon eggs and hostile defenders. Constance chooses the eggs and children as hers, and she and Zuk enter in full dragon form.

## Entry 8 Freed Satyr and Juans Return

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 5](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-5.md)

**Type:** Character return Elseware.

**Review:** The captive released beside Maeve helps carry her through Hell, then receives Maeve's last blessing. His form resolves into [Juan](../people/juan.md), restored to himself.

**Campaign tie:** Juan's return restores a key Moon Stone Collectors party member and gives him the [Divine Soul Coin](../items/divine-soul-coin.md), a tool that later unlocks fortress history.

**Detailed breakdown:** The scene shows Juan leaving Hell with knowledge, beauty, and art still echoing through Maeve's fading divinity. He knows where his friends are and returns to the dragon egg rescue.

## Entry 9 Shaq Watches the Concurrence

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 5](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-5.md)

**Type:** Major emotional Elseware.

**Review:** [Gregario / Shaq](../people/gregario-shaq.md) returns to the alley where his first team died and watches the worlds align around the [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md).

**Campaign tie:** This scene bridges the War of the Gods, Belle's daughters, and the post-Concurrence world. It directly points toward [Lilith](../people/lilith.md)'s protector thread.

**Detailed breakdown:** As the worlds become one, the hole inside Gregario is filled. He hears a child crying and meets the little girl connected to his old oath, establishing why Lilith later lands near him.

## Entry 10 Missing Mercenaries and the Dwarf Door

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 6](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-6.md)

**Type:** Minor scout cutaway.

**Review:** A bat familiar scouts after a missing mercenary group and finds a shifting chamber where a dwarf pulls bracers from a hidden wall-space before the distortion closes.

**Campaign tie:** This points toward the dwarven/infernal machinery beneath the larger egg-fortress and goo-control history.

**Detailed breakdown:** The dwarf notices the bat and attacks, leaving the party with an unresolved thread: someone used a hidden exit or control route while the rescue chaos unfolded.

## Entry 11 Belle Breaches the Black Keep

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 6](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-6.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** [Belle](../people/belle.md) enters Wendell's Black Keep in Hell. She tears fire from demonic blood, melts the gates, and advances through the fortress.

**Campaign tie:** This is the start of Belle's revenge for Wendell taking and draining Maeve.

**Detailed breakdown:** Belle's assault is not framed as a normal military strike. It is punishment and ownership: Wendell touched what Belle considers hers, and the fortress becomes the stage where she teaches him the cost.

## Entry 12 Jimbo and Charlie Ambushed

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 6](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-6.md) and [Assassination of Jimbo and Charlie](../sessions/assassination-of-jimbo-and-charlie.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** [Jimbo](../people/jimbo.md) and [Charlie](../people/charlie.md) are ambushed by Black Viper-linked attackers, demons, and infernal/dwarven portal work. Both are killed, and their souls are stolen.

**Campaign tie:** This scene becomes the launch point for [The Hunt for Black Vipers](hunt-for-black-vipers.md), [Jimbo's Soul Ritual](../concepts/jimbo-soul-ritual.md), and the Black Viper pursuit.

**Detailed breakdown:** Junvara senses the problem too late to prevent it. Charlie's machines are destroyed, Jimbo is dead and soulless, and soul cages leave with the attackers. The later Black Viper arc grows directly from this cutaway.

## Entry 13 Belle Confronts Wendell

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 6](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-6.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** Belle reaches Wendell's throne room, where he waits with high-ranking demons prepared for her. Wendell believes Belle is weakened by the Gate and that he has studied her enough to win.

**Campaign tie:** This scene reveals how badly Wendell misunderstands Belle's rules around possession, family, and punishment.

**Detailed breakdown:** Belle frames his mistakes plainly: he underestimates her, touches what is hers, and accepted a contract involving Maeve's divine blood. The confrontation becomes a fight defined less by victory than by Belle's planned punishment.

## Entry 14 Wendell Punished

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 6](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-6.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** Wendell wakes maimed in ritual circles. Belle forces him to remember that his god-killing strike was reflected back through a mirror-like device made with Greg's work.

**Campaign tie:** This sets Wendell's later anchor-state and explains why the sixth layer of Hell can later be damaged through him.

**Detailed breakdown:** Belle does not kill Wendell because he did not kill Maeve. Instead, she uses Maeve's divine blood to bind and punish him, leaving him alive but transformed into a living consequence.

## Entry 15 Wendells Final Destruction

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 8](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-8.md)

**Type:** Major aftermath Elseware.

**Review:** During the Hell assault, the party finds Wendell reduced to a flaming stone-heart anchor. Allied forces withdraw while dragons and strike teams destroy the fortress around him.

**Campaign tie:** This ends Wendell's sixth-layer rule and turns Belle's earlier punishment into a world-scale Hell consequence.

**Detailed breakdown:** Wendell's soul is tied to the keep as an anchor. When he and the fortress are destroyed, the damage ripples through Hell, and the sixth layer is lost.

## Entry 16 Deceivers Contingencies and Veils Ascension

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 11](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-11.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** After the Deceiver-linked enemy dies, contingency memories and commands trigger across the [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md). People act on hidden orders, causing violence, sabotage, and chaos.

**Campaign tie:** This explains why the party's victory over one enemy immediately becomes a wider Citadel crisis.

**Detailed breakdown:** The view then shifts to [Veil](../people/veil-guardian-of-the-citadel.md), who chooses not to remain guardian, not to transfer into another host, and not to keep carrying every crisis. He ascends, leaving the Citadel changed.

## Entry 17 School Massacre and Alysia Consumed

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 11](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-11.md) and [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 12](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-12.md)

**Type:** Major consequence cutaway.

**Review:** The party returns to the school during the Citadel chaos and finds bodies, blood, and signs of a defensive massacre. [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md)'s protected form survives inside a shield.

**Campaign tie:** This scene pushes the party out of the school and deepens the [Six Sisters](../people/index.md#the-six-sisters) thread.

**Detailed breakdown:** A celestial-demonic sister-being associated with [Julia / Julisia](../people/julia-julisia.md) appears near Phlukk. The scene suggests one sister-fragment has consumed or merged with another, foreshadowing [Sovereignty](../people/sovereignty.md).

## Entry 18 Juans Soul Dream

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 13](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-13.md)

**Type:** Major internal Elseware.

**Review:** [Juan](../people/juan.md) drifts through a dreamscape that resembles the Fey realm and sees memories around Fate, his brother, scars, and altered destiny.

**Campaign tie:** This is one of the clearest links between Juan, [The Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md), stolen memory, and manipulated fate.

**Detailed breakdown:** Juan realizes he is inside his own soul and that a malevolent presence has been twisting or hiding memories. He steps off the path laid for him, burns black tendrils out of his soul, and wakes knowing that his luck is real but his choices still belong to him.

## Entry 19 Juan and the Amulet of Lost Memories

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 13](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-13.md)

**Type:** Internal vision.

**Review:** Juan uses the [Amulet of Lost Memories](../items/amulet-of-lost-memories.md) to seek a specific memory of fishing with his brother. Instead, memory rushes around him like a current.

**Campaign tie:** This connects Juan's personal restoration to the wider dwarven memory technology that later reveals the goo control room.

**Detailed breakdown:** The recovered portion shows Juan choosing willpower over arcane control, continuing the growth that began when he rejected the soul corruption. The exact later contents of this first amulet vision remain only partly recovered.

## Entry 20 Phlukks Restoration by Viscus

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 13](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-13.md)

**Type:** Major ritual Elseware.

**Review:** [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md) performs a memory-restoration ritual for [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md). Spores and pollen display damaged memories, then gather into a restorative form.

**Campaign tie:** This makes Phlukk's recovery part of the [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) network and sets up the later dream guidance.

**Detailed breakdown:** Viscus chooses a skull-root route rather than a chest-root route. A root fragment remains inside Phlukk, ritual marks burn into him and then vanish under regrown feathers, and he wakes beside his gear.

## Entry 21 Session 14 Elseware Rolls Pending

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 14](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-14.md)

**Type:** Mechanical Elseware tracker.

**Review:** The table rolls for off-screen Elseware events, but the recovered notes do not include the resolved scene.

**Campaign tie:** This is a placeholder for missing off-screen consequences in the available archive.

**Detailed breakdown:** The available snippet includes jokes that Junvara, Kestrel, or the Commodore may have died or returned, but no final cutaway resolution has been recovered.

## Entry 22 Paul Belle and the Crown of Fools

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 15](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-15.md)

**Type:** Divine dream and consequence Elseware.

**Review:** [Paul](../people/paul.md) dreams of three aspects of [Belle](../people/belle.md): Spring, Mischief, and Dreams. They judge him for destroying the mushroom circle that linked [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md), [Viscus](../people/viscus.md), and the Feywild.

**Campaign tie:** This creates Paul's [Crown of Fools](../items/crown-of-fools.md) burden and drives him further into [Julara](../people/julara.md)'s counsel.

**Detailed breakdown:** Paul is marked with faerie fire and a visible mushroom-crown sign. Julara later explains that he survived because Belle's Mischief aspect enjoyed the chaos, but the broken Fey bond still demands repair.

## Entry 23 Conquer Love and Justice Merge

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** In the celestial prison-area beneath Hell, Power / Conquer reaches Love and tries to merge with her. Justice intervenes from within the mindscape, warning that Conquer means to consume Love.

**Campaign tie:** This is the first clear formation stage of [Sovereignty](../people/sovereignty.md).

**Detailed breakdown:** Justice cannot defeat Conquer alone, so she asks Love to accept a union. Love agrees. Conquer still wins dominance, but the victory changes her because two other aspects now live inside the merged being.

## Entry 24 Viscus Shows Phlukk the Wound

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md)

**Type:** Dream vision.

**Review:** [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) gently pulls [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md) into a dream and lets him experience the grove through roots, bees, flowers, smoke, undead workers, and distant creatures.

**Campaign tie:** This reveals why the party must repair the Fey link and how deeply Viscus is tied to the grove's survival.

**Detailed breakdown:** Phlukk sees seven functioning mushroom circles and the destroyed eighth circle as a wound. Viscus feeds on those Fey portals and directs him toward the portal the party can use.

## Entry 25 Knowledge Offers Paul Four Paths

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md)

**Type:** Personal dream Elseware.

**Review:** [Julara](../people/julara.md), the knowledge aspect, gives Paul a cold-iron amulet that can suppress the Crown of Fools. She explains that the Fey gatekeeper stole sixty-four years from him.

**Campaign tie:** This becomes the turning point that pushes Paul away from the Moon Stone Collectors.

**Detailed breakdown:** Julara offers Paul several paths: accept the loss, fully bind his life to hers, force the Fey to return what was taken, or be reborn through a child made with her. The options make his fear, resentment, and attachment to Julara more urgent.

## Entry 26 Luka and Junvara Receive the Church Mission

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** [Luka](../people/luka.md) and [Junvara](../people/junvara.md) are briefed by church leaders on the Six Sisters. The churches fear that if the fragments recombine, an arch-demoness or arch-celestial-demon level threat may return.

**Campaign tie:** This turns the sister-fragment plot into an official [Conclave of Churches](../factions/conclave-of-churches.md) mission and makes Paul a target because of his link to Julara.

**Detailed breakdown:** Luka is ordered to find Paul and free him from Julara or kill Julara through the connection. Junvara is pointed toward another fragment, Lust, but the mission becomes more complicated once Sovereignty begins forming.

## Entry 27 Dwarven Commander and the Goo Control Room

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md)

**Type:** Historical memory Elseware.

**Review:** Juan uses the Amulet of Lost Memories and sees the final actions of a dwarven commander before the [Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md). The commander opens a hidden moonlit cliff door with three discs and descends toward a master control chamber.

**Campaign tie:** This gives the party the knowledge needed to reach the old [Gateway to the Empire](../places/gateway-to-the-empire.md) control room.

**Detailed breakdown:** In the chamber, a massive gray ooze is bound by cables and magical chains. The ooze impales the commander and touches the amulet as he dies. Juan wakes knowing a panel exists that can affect the entire [Demonic Dwarven Goo](../concepts/demonic-dwarven-goo.md) network.

## Entry 28 Sorrels Summer Court Summons

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md)

**Type:** Minor court Elseware.

**Review:** [Sorrel](../people/sorrel.md) is called through the [Summer Court](../places/summer-court.md), where her grandfather has named her his successor.

**Campaign tie:** This puts Sorrel in position to receive the Moon Stone Collectors when they enter the Feywild.

**Detailed breakdown:** Sorrel expects routine court duty but is told that mortal visitors have asked for her. She hears the familiar hum of bees, opens the door, and finds the party.

## Entry 29 Lust Returns to Sovereignty

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** In a heavenly labyrinth, Lust hears armored footsteps and encounters the merged Conquer-Love-Justice being.

**Campaign tie:** This raises [Sovereignty](../people/sovereignty.md) from three aspects to four and makes the church mission far more dangerous.

**Detailed breakdown:** Lust recognizes herself in the being before her. Sovereignty touches her forehead and tells her to return. Lust is absorbed, and the merged being now carries Conquer, Love, Justice, and Lust.

## Entry 30 Junvaras Judgment of Sovereignty

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** Junvara arrives expecting one fragment and instead finds Sovereignty. She argues that Conquer's crimes were the acts of a severed fragment, not necessarily the whole restored being.

**Campaign tie:** This is the moral center of the sister-fragment plot: whether restoration is justice, danger, or both.

**Detailed breakdown:** Sovereignty compares herself to Junvara's echo and asks whether a separated part's actions should condemn the whole. Junvara recognizes that she is dangerous but chooses not to fight her that day, warning that another conversation will be necessary if she becomes whole.

## Entry 31 Knowledge Returns to Sovereignty

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 17](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-17.md)

**Type:** Major Elseware.

**Review:** Paul leaves the grove to protect [Julara](../people/julara.md), fearing the party and church will let her be killed or forcibly merged. Sovereignty finds him anyway.

**Campaign tie:** Paul's departure cannot prevent Julara's return to the whole, but it defines his personal loyalty and the cost of the sister-fragment story.

**Detailed breakdown:** Knowledge is absorbed into Sovereignty, leaving the merged being with five of the six aspects: Conquer, Love, Justice, Lust, and Knowledge. Paul survives, but the bond that shaped him is severed.

## Entry 32 Viscus Takes the Goo Network

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 17](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-17.md)

**Type:** World consequence montage.

**Review:** In the old dwarven control chamber, the party inserts a living cutting of Viscus into the control apparatus and protects the ritual long enough for Viscus to take the network.

**Campaign tie:** This is one of the campaign's biggest world-state changes. [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md) becomes a geopolitical power.

**Detailed breakdown:** The master goo, hidden tunnels, infected bodies, undead soldiers, and remnants of the plague fall under Viscus's influence. The mass-infection threat is neutralized, but Viscus now controls power on the scale of nations.

## Entry 33 Wisdom Merges and Sovereignty Becomes Whole

**Source:** [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 17](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-17.md)

**Type:** Final major Elseware.

**Review:** After the goo crisis resolves, the camera turns to the [Summer Court](../places/summer-court.md). The Green Man's old bargain is fulfilled, and [Wisdom](../people/wisdom.md) is brought safely to [Sovereignty](../people/sovereignty.md).

**Campaign tie:** This closes the Six Sisters thread for the campaign by undoing [Lucius](../people/lucius.md)'s ancient division.

**Detailed breakdown:** Wisdom joins her sisters, completing Sovereignty as a new whole. The church's attempt to prevent the reunion fails, but the result is not framed as simple conquest. The final Elseware closes with transformation: Viscus is a power among nations, and Sovereignty is whole in the Feywild.
