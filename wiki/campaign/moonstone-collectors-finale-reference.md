# Moon Stone Collectors Finale Transcript Reference

This page is a working reference for the two uploaded finale transcripts covering the last stretch of the [Moon Stone Collectors](moonstone-collectors.md) campaign.

Source videos:

- [Finale transcript segment 1](https://youtu.be/y75Rlhz6XjA?si=wMJCoWcQNKyMBZv7)
- [Finale transcript segment 2](https://youtu.be/gTCUhuWTclU?si=SjhdlvoG_-yjtHcA)

The existing session pages remain the playable summaries. This page keeps the higher-level continuity details in one place so later Seventh Age material can reference them without re-reading the raw transcript.

## Part 16 Summary

The finale opens with the six sister-fragments being tracked by name: [Elisia](../people/elisia.md), [Julia](../people/julia-julisia.md), [Alura](../people/alura.md), [Julessa](../people/julessa.md), [Julara](../people/julara.md), and [Sophia](../people/wisdom.md). The name Julisia is used for the merged Julia-plus-Elisia state after Julia absorbs or merges with Elisia.

In the celestial refuge beneath Hell, [Julia](../people/julia-julisia.md) finds [Alura](../people/alura.md). [Elisia](../people/elisia.md) warns Alura that Julia intends to consume her, and the scene becomes a three-way mental struggle. Alura and Elisia merge to resist, but Julia wins dominance. The victory changes her anyway: the resulting being is now outnumbered internally by divine-aligned sisters and later takes the name [Sovereignty](../people/sovereignty.md). Afterward, Sovereignty absorbs [Julessa](../people/julessa.md) and becomes four parts of the original whole.

Back at the cabin after the hydra fight, [Juan](../people/juan.md) shares the [Amulet of Lost Memories](../items/amulet-of-lost-memories.md). The amulet is identified as a rare pre-Breaking dwarven runecraft artifact using divination and memory-copying magic. It does not require a normal attunement slot, but sleeping with it can reveal lost memories from the wearer or previous users. A failed Constitution save causes exhaustion and prevents the benefits of a long rest.

[Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md) dreams through [Viscus](../people/viscus.md)'s roots. He experiences the grove through plants, bees, undead workers, smokers, and mushroom circles. Viscus shows that the grove is fed by fey energy through eight circles, with the destroyed Spring-linked circle leaving a wound.

[Julara](../people/julara.md) gives [Paul](../people/paul.md) a cold-iron amulet that suppresses the faerie-fire portion of the [Crown of Fools](../items/crown-of-fools.md). She explains that the Keeper of the Gates stole 64 years from Paul, a punishment wildly disproportionate for a non-fey mortal. Paul refuses a full bond with Julara, and later leaves the party rather than let the churches use him as a route to kill or force-merge her.

On the [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md), [Luka](../people/luka.md) and [Junvara](../people/junvara.md) are briefed by church leaders. The churches fear the recombined sister-being could become an arch-demoness or arch-celestial-demon threat. Luka is sent to free Paul from Julara or kill Julara through the connection if necessary. Junvara is sent after another fragment, but his path leads to a conversation with Sovereignty instead of a fight.

The party enters the [Summer Court](../places/summer-court.md), where [Sorrel](../people/sorrel.md) serves under her grandfather, the Brownie adviser to the Green Man. [Sophia](../people/wisdom.md) recognizes [Juan](../people/juan.md) as marked by her nemesis, [The Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md), and explains the hidden Fey oath, the Wild Hunt's enforcement role, and the cornerstone spellwork that holds the [Feywild](../places/feywild.md) together. She also explains Viscus's origin as a fey-altered offshoot of the demonic dwarven goo and the moral problem of forcing sister-fragments to merge or split. The party petitions the Green Man to replace the broken Spring Court connection. The request is accepted, restoring the grove's fey link, removing Paul's crown, and returning his stolen years.

The party returns to [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md) after three weeks have passed outside. They decide to take a living cutting of Viscus into the old dwarven control room. If the cutting is destroyed, [Vylra](../people/vylra.md) or Phlukk may have to sacrifice himself through the Viscus connection, or the party may have to destroy all goo, killing Viscus as well.

## Part 17 Summary

The party reopens the hidden cliff door at the old [Gateway to the Empire](../places/gateway-to-the-empire.md) using the three recovered discs, darkness, moonbeam, and dwarven riddle logic.

Inside the facility, the party solves a statue-and-lever puzzle, survives dwarven trap gauntlets, uses [Denny](../people/denny.md) and [Soda](../people/soda.md) to test or navigate hazards, and reroutes the facility's portal controls toward the master control chamber.

A failed or experimental attempt to interact with a giant skeleton stirs gray goo. The party contains it with fire, holy water, disintegration effects, and battlefield control while protecting the living Viscus cutting.

In the master chamber, [Juan](../people/juan.md), [Zen](../people/zen.md), [Vylra](../people/vylra.md), [p'Op](../people/p-op.md), [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md), [Luka](../people/luka.md), and the others defend the control operation long enough for Viscus to claim the master goo. Luka nearly destroys the master goo outright, which would have ruined the plan, and is briefly infected before purging the goo through zealot divine force.

The final control succeeds. Viscus gains command over the master goo, the wider goo network, infected animals and bodies, the dwarven tunnel infection, and roughly 500,000 undead soldiers preserved in frozen lakes and old dwarven systems.

The ending makes [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md) one of the greatest powers on the world. Viscus is not immediately interested in conquest, but the grove is no longer a hidden enclave. It becomes a power that nations, churches, and later Seventh Age campaigns may need to reckon with.

The final cutaway returns to the Summer Court. The Green Man confirms he kept Sophia safe for roughly a thousand years. Sophia and Julara merge into Sovereignty, completing the sister-being as a new whole. Because the merger occurs in the Feywild, Sovereignty is not in the mortal realm and does not ascend into the heavens; she is too powerful to come directly into the mortal world.

## Seventh Age Relevance

- [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md) is now a northern power with control over the old goo network and a vast undead military reserve.
- [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) is no longer only a local sacred intelligence; he is linked to a world-scale ancient control system.
- [Sovereignty](../people/sovereignty.md) exists as a completed sister-being in the [Feywild](../places/feywild.md), neither destroyed nor ascended.
- [Sophia](../people/wisdom.md)'s testimony makes [The Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md) not only Juan's fate-thief but her own nemesis, tied to hidden Fey law and the cornerstone of Fey reality.
- The [Conclave of Churches](../factions/conclave-of-churches.md) has direct reason to fear, monitor, or politicize the sister-fragment outcome.
- [Paul](../people/paul.md)'s departure leaves a personal unresolved thread around Julara even though Julara later merges into Sovereignty.
- The finale explicitly frames the early [Seventh Age](../concepts/seventh-age.md) / [Age of Heroes](../concepts/age-of-heroes.md) as an age shaped by individuals becoming more than they were expected or allowed to be.
