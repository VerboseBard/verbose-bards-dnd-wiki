# Commodore Prophecy and Legend-Fed Rebirth

The Commodore Prophecy is the belief that [Mulligan](../people/mulligan.md), the ancient Pirate King also remembered as [Molokan](../people/molokan.md), returns when the [Opaal Old One](opaal-old-one.md) stirs.

## Legend-Fed Power

The Commodore is not a god in the ordinary sense.

His return is powered by something adjacent to worship but not identical to divine faith. The belief in the Commodore's eventual return, the prophecy that names his role, the fear and hope attached to his legend, and the deeds that history could not fully erase all generate mythic pressure.

That pressure can pull him back when the old threat rises.

## Difference from Divine Worship

Gods are empowered by worship, domains, mantles, and divine structure. The Commodore is empowered by legend: cultural memory, prophecy, expectation, and the hole left in history when his name was erased but his deeds could not be.

This makes his return harder for gods to control. It also means that when he rises, he does not necessarily belong to a church, pantheon, or divine patron.

## Premature Return

During the [War of the Gods / New Beginning Campaign](../campaign/war-of-the-gods.md), the prophecy was hijacked.

[Mulligan](../people/mulligan.md) returned prematurely on [Opaal](../places/opaal.md), washed ashore as a corpse with no memory. Because an air elemental hovered near him, he believed the being later called [Straka](../people/straka.md) had brought him back.

The truth is stranger: Straka recognized him from his prior life but did not resurrect him. The actual return had been forced early by meddling around pantheons and old enemies.

## True Return

When the Commodore returns again during the later [Moon Stone Collectors](../campaign/moonstone-collectors.md) / [The Hunt for Black Vipers](../campaign/hunt-for-black-vipers.md) period, that is the return the prophecy was originally pointing toward.

That timing means the seals are failing and the old one is close to stirring again.
