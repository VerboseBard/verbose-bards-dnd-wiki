# Contractor Status

Contractor Status is the party's post-discharge legal position after leaving the [Unified Armed Forces](../factions/unified-armed-forces.md) but accepting classified work.

## Description

Contractors can take military jobs without being regular enlisted soldiers. This gives them more freedom, access to special contracts, and in some cases legal loopholes around handling restricted objects during classified operations.

## Party Version

[Colonel Core](../people/colonel-core.md) offers the party contractor medallions and a classified mission rather than asking them to extend ordinary service. They negotiate [priority teleport tokens](../items/priority-teleport-tokens.md) as payment.

Session 5 clarifies the structure: the party does not take a normal three-month reenlistment, but the contractor mission itself is expected to cover up to about three months / 90 days. Payment is tied to completion or that mission window, with possible longer-duration bonuses if the job runs past the expected term.

The first real test of that status is the [Lost Homeland Mission](lost-homeland-mission.md), where the party operates with military backing but without ordinary rank protection or barracks obligations.

## Legal Importance

The party identifies contract loopholes that let them handle fog materials and refuse bag searches except through proper high-level authority.

This matters immediately because it helps them:

- keep certain discoveries classified instead of surrendering them automatically
- avoid low-level personnel forcing disclosure
- move through [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) as sanctioned specialists rather than deserters or scavengers
