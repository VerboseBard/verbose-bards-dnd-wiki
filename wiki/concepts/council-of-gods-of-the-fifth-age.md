# Council of Gods of the Fifth Age

The Council of Gods of the Fifth Age is the main divine political structure preserved in the surviving [Fifth Age](fifth-age.md) notes.

## Divine Structure

[Maeve, Queen of the Fae](../people/maeve.md), [Bridget](../people/bridget.md), and [Maeve, the Schoolmistress](../people/mave.md) are separate divine figures:

- [Maeve, Queen of the Fae](../people/maeve.md) was the goddess of magic, formerly fae, and the most powerful member of the pantheon.
- [Bridget](../people/bridget.md) was the goddess of music, art, and beauty, and roughly third in power.
- [Maeve, the Schoolmistress](../people/mave.md), the half-elven paladin and school-running figure seen in later Fifth- and Sixth-Age campaign material, was a different person from [Maeve, Queen of the Fae](../people/maeve.md). After [Bridget](../people/bridget.md) was killed by [Lucius](../people/lucius.md), [Maeve, the Schoolmistress](../people/mave.md) rose into [Bridget](../people/bridget.md)'s place.

[Maeve, Queen of the Fae](../people/maeve.md) was also [Belle](../people/belle.md)'s mother and had ruled both the Summer Court and Winter Court before becoming a goddess.

## Assemblies

The record names two major blocks. The Sages' Assembly list is comparatively complete, while the Sword and Purse Assembly list preserves only some positions.

## The Sages' Assembly

The surviving source note lists the Sages' Assembly in this order:

1. [Maeve, Queen of the Fae](../people/maeve.md), goddess of magic, formerly fae, and most powerful member of the pantheon.
2. [Bridget](../people/bridget.md) / [Maeve, the Schoolmistress](../people/mave.md), preserving the source note's succession shorthand for the goddess of music, art, and beauty, ranked third in power.
3. [Dione](../people/dione.md) and [Savras](../people/savras.md), gods of foreknowledge and prophecy, ranked second in power, with the [Seekers](../factions/seekers.md) named as their children.
4. [Belle](../people/belle.md), goddess of fire and mischief, ranked fourth in power.
5. [Sedna](../people/sedna.md), goddess of sea and sky.
6. [Straka](../people/straka.md), god of second chances, storms, and freedom.
7. [Aylinn](../people/aylinn.md), a godlike power who never fully ascended, associated with darkness, retribution, and punishment.
8. [Lucius](../people/lucius.md), a powerful unascended figure.

The Bridget / Maeve shorthand should not be read as a single person. Current canon treats [Bridget](../people/bridget.md) and [Maeve, the Schoolmistress](../people/mave.md) as separate figures, with Maeve later rising into Bridget's divine place after Bridget's death.

## The Sword and Purse Assembly

The Sword and Purse Assembly source note preserves positions 1, 2, and 7. Positions 3 through 6 remain blank or unrecovered in the current archive.

1. [Finnian / the Green Man](../people/finnian-green-man.md), also called the Leprechaun, god of deals, merchants, swindlers, and gamblers.
2. [Grommok](../people/grommok.md), orc god of war.
3. Unknown.
4. Unknown.
5. Unknown.
6. Unknown.
7. [Sir Humphrey Puddington](../people/sir-humphrey-puddington.md), god-emperor of the [Giff](../factions/giff-compendency.md) in the [Astral Sea](astral-sea.md), explicitly noted as not actually a god.

## Historical Role

The council belongs to the same broad era that later collapses into the [War of the Gods](war-of-the-gods.md), when divine hierarchy, prophecy, succession, and personal ambition had all become unstable enough to rupture into open conflict.
