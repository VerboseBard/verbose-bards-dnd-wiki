# Fourth Age

## Description

Very little Fourth-Age material is fully documented in the current wiki, but one major surviving point of canon is [Hestia](../people/hestia.md), a goddess of hearth, home, and refuge.

[Hestia](../people/hestia.md) was widely thought to have been killed in the Fourth Age.

Another major surviving point is that the Fourth Age included a broad war against [Lolth](../people/lolth.md) and her worship. Many races took part, but the dwarves were the dominant power of the age and carried the heaviest role in that campaign.

[Lolth](../people/lolth.md)'s destruction was pursued in part because she betrayed the other gods while seeking greater power and helped bring about Hestia's downfall.

Both powers were tied to fragmented survival afterward: Lolth through scattered remnants of her own spirit, and Hestia through hidden surviving fragments. At least some of Hestia's fragments were later captured by Lolth's priestesses and hidden near Lolth-linked temple sites.

Her reappearance in [Session 6](../sessions/session-6.md) of the [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md) suggests that at least some powers believed lost in that age may have survived in hidden, sealed, fragmented, or forgotten forms.

[Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md) adds a living Fourth-Age witness: [Bartholomew Hildebrant](../people/bartholomew-hildebrant.md), a dwarf from the middle of the Fourth Age who survived an assault on [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md) by entering stasis. His memory indicates roughly ten assault teams, about forty people total, attacked the temple and nearly brought it down.

Bartholomew's medallion matches [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](../people/sergeant-jefferson-stone.md)'s Hestia-linked amulet, tying the Fourth-Age assault directly back to Hestia's surviving divine pattern.
