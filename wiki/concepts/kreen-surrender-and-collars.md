# Kreen Surrender and Collars

[Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) surrender and collar policy emerges from the late-war discovery that not all [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) remain uncontrollably hostile.

## Description

The party helps reveal that some [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) can protect civilians and surrender. Later, captured [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) are sedated, hauled away, and experimented upon. The resulting magical method makes [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) unable to attack a protected category of people and turns their skin gray.

Session 2's spoken explanation says collared Kreen cannot hurt humans. Session 3's opening recap broadens the idea to sentient races, so the exact legal or magical boundary remains open.

## Moral Weight

The collars solve an immediate security problem but raise serious ethical questions. They blur the line between mercy, enslavement, punishment, and survival policy.

The first mass payoff is the dark elf queen's surrender of 100,000 Kreen in the Underdark after the Hounds' capture work proves the collars can make surrender administratively possible.
