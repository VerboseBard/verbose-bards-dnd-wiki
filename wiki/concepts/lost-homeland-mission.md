# Lost Homeland Mission

The Lost Homeland Mission is the contractor operation assigned to the party by [Colonel Core](../people/colonel-core.md) after their discharge from military service.

## Objective

The party must scout a wagon-passable route through [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md)'s fog to [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md). They must place linked markers, identify safe camps, avoid unnecessary engagement, and communicate danger.

In Session 4 and Session 5, the party is explicitly the pathfinder team. The larger expedition follows roughly several days to about a week later; Session 5's recap says the party leaves about five days ahead. That lead time gives their marked route, beacon choices, and side detour to the hidden ship strategic weight.

Along the way, the route passes a hidden crashed goblin ship that becomes a major detour in [Session 4](../sessions/session-4.md) and [Session 5](../sessions/session-5.md). By [Session 6](../sessions/session-6.md), the party has turned that detour into a secret asset and resumed the mission in earnest.

## Strategic Stakes

The mission may reconnect displaced fog-walker descendants with their ancestral homeland. It also carries military, religious, and political risk because a larger expedition including diplomats, soldiers, and church representatives will follow.

The job turns out to be slower and harsher than the contract makes it sound. The party has to test multiple routes, mark dead ends, navigate storm-struck mountain country, and make sure the final path can support wagons rather than just scouts. Session 6 also confirms the operational risk of the [Kex](../places/kex.md) detour: the party disguises roughly an hour of the route back from the hidden ship before resuming marker placement, keeping the ship from becoming obvious to the larger expedition following behind them.

When they finally reach [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md), the mission's basic claim is validated: the valley exists, it is inhabited, and first contact is now a live issue instead of a theory. Session 7 makes the operational problem worse when the valley patrol produces some of the party's marker discs from a dead human scout found near the pass. That means the marked route may already be compromised before the party has any chance to manage first contact cleanly.

That first contact opens the way to [the Sunhollow Council](../factions/sunhollow-council.md), the valley's clan politics, and the deeper threat of [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md). By [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), the mission also changes shape again: the party learns the route may already be compromised, the valley's crystal wealth makes outside exposure catastrophic, and the local council asks them to investigate the spreading blight in the [Dead Lands](../places/dead-lands.md).

By [Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md), the mission is no longer only about finding the valley or identifying the blight. The party learns that many Sunhollow inhabitants may be oath-bound to defend [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md), while outsiders following the marked route may exploit or kill them for the valley's crystal wealth. The mission's central question becomes how to protect Sunhollow without triggering conquest, mass death, or a doomed defense of the temple.

By [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), the mission has become a countdown. The Inquisition is close behind, [Bevar Lurton](../people/bevar-lurton.md) remains missing after the temple emissary path, [Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](../people/mistress-selyra-vexryn.md) claims the party should redirect the Church toward a demon source south along the wall, and [Rurik Valdren](../people/rurik-valdren.md) is contacted as a possible shield against seizure or purge. The party's immediate objective shifts to briefing the [Sunhollow Council](../factions/sunhollow-council.md) before the outside force reaches the gate.
