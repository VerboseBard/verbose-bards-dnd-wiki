# Post-Concurrence Magic Theory

Post-Concurrence Magic Theory explains why magic is becoming more common but individually less limitless after [the Concurrence](the-concurrence.md) and [Divine Gate](divine-gate.md).

## Core Theory

Scholars theorize that reality contains a finite amount of magical potential. Before the [Divine Gate](divine-gate.md), fewer people could access magic, so exceptional casters could draw enormous power. After the Gate, [ley lines](ley-lines.md) consume much of that potential to stabilize reality, while far more people are born with access to magic.

Session 3 frames this in rough scholarly terms: before the Concurrence perhaps one in 100 people had magical potential, while afterward it may be closer to one in 10. At the same time, ley-line stabilization may consume roughly half of all available magical potential.

## Consequences

This creates more low-level or moderate magic users but fewer truly overwhelming casters. Artificery, ritual magic, refined ether, and external magical infrastructure may become the main ways to reach older heights. The dwarf scholar [Professor Hazard](../people/professor-hazard.md) is cited as one voice arguing that structured magical technology can supplement personal limits.

## Campaign Role

This theory is discussed during the party's transfer to [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md), where teleportation now requires more mages or refined ether than it once did.
