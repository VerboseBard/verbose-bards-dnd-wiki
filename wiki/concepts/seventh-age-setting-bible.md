# Seventh Age Setting Bible

This page is a reader-facing overview of the current setting frame. It condenses the broader [Seventh Age](seventh-age.md) material into one place for quick orientation.

## Core Premise

The [Seventh Age](seventh-age.md) begins after [the Concurrence](the-concurrence.md), when broken worlds were linked into a new survivable order. The gods are not gone, but the [Divine Gate](divine-gate.md) keeps them distant. Miracles still happen, clerics still draw power, and divine politics still matter, but the old age of direct divine rule has ended.

The result is not peace. It is a world trying to function after being rebuilt from wreckage.

## Shape of the World

[Unity](../places/unity.md) is the central world and logistical hub of the current order. It holds the [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md), the great [Nexus](nexus.md), true [dungeons](dungeons.md), the [Aether Sea](aether-sea.md), and the inner realm commonly called the [Underdark / Upside-Down](../places/underdark-upside-down.md).

Other major worlds and realms remain attached or reachable through sanctioned routes. [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md), [Norvindr](../places/norvindr.md), [Saharun](../places/saharun.md), and [Opaal](../places/opaal.md) each carry their own politics, damage, and post-Concurrence consequences.

The [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md) is the choke point. Whoever controls its travel, banking, and information systems controls much of the new world's future.

## Magic and Infrastructure

[Ley lines](ley-lines.md) were used to keep the remade reality stable. Where they converge, magic becomes powerful and dangerous enough to warp space, generate sealed pockets, and feed dungeon growth.

Modern magical theory increasingly treats magic as infrastructure rather than only spellcraft. [Ether Crystals](../items/ether-crystals.md), fixed travel, dungeon cores, artificery, and military magitech all belong to the same broad shift: mortals are learning to store, redirect, weaponize, and industrialize forces that earlier ages treated as divine or legendary.

## Dungeons and the Age of Heroes

The early Seventh Age is commonly called the [Age of Heroes](age-of-heroes.md). The name does not only mean legendary champions. It also means outcasts, refugees, guild members, dungeon divers, and ordinary people who survived long enough to matter.

True [dungeons](dungeons.md) are most strongly associated with [Unity](../places/unity.md). Ruins, bunkers, magical wounds, and dungeon-like sites exist elsewhere, but Unity is where the living dungeon ecology is most central to politics and economy.

The [Adventurer's Guild](../factions/adventurers-guild.md) rises because dungeons turn danger into opportunity. Treasure, cores, salvage, and renown become ways for people without noble blood or divine backing to gain power.

## Faith and Power

The [Conclave of Churches](../factions/conclave-of-churches.md) tries to regulate divine legitimacy after the [Divine Gate](divine-gate.md). That means deciding which gods, demigods, saints, patrons, and quasi-divine beings may be worshiped openly.

Two major arms reflect the split inside church power:

- [Crusaders](../factions/crusaders.md) are elite holy warriors more openly respected by soldiers.
- [Inquisition](../factions/inquisition.md) handles investigation, treason, mind-control detection, and coercive enforcement.

The gods are sealed, not dead. That distinction matters everywhere.

## The Kreen War

The [Kreen War](kreen-war.md) breaks the hope of the early Seventh Age. What begins as a crisis centered on [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) becomes a multi-world war of extinction.

The war creates mass conscription, level-zero soldiers, emergency doctrine, and brutal military logic. Millions are trained just enough to march and survive just long enough to be useful. The party in the [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md) begins inside that pressure.

[Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md), [Kreen Control Collars](../items/kreen-control-collars.md), and the mass surrender problem all grow from the ugly realization that victory does not end the moral consequences of war.

## Driftvale After the War

[Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) remains a fog-shrouded frontier after the [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) reinforcement gate is closed. Its fog is not merely weather. Settled areas survive through containment artifacts, while free fog behaves like a living environmental force with tides, moods, and hidden dangers.

After the war, military authority increasingly monopolizes artifact recovery. Civilians, native fog walkers, smugglers, and soldiers all compete around the same question: who gets to profit from what the fog reveals?

[Fort Victory](../places/fort-victory.md), [Sin](../places/sin.md), [Calvin's Curios](../places/calvins-curios.md), [The Silk Parlor](../places/silk-parlor.md), and the [Lost Homeland Mission](lost-homeland-mission.md) all sit inside that postwar gray zone.

## Current Campaign Frame

The [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md) begins in Year 11 of the [Seventh Age](seventh-age.md), when the war is ending but its consequences are still raw.

The party starts as ordinary soldiers in the [Bloody Fifth](../factions/bloody-fifth.md), becomes part of [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md), survives moral and political fallout, receives [Contractor Status](contractor-status.md), finds the [Kex](../places/kex.md), and eventually reaches [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md).

The campaign's central question is not whether the war can be won. It is what happens when survivors inherit secrets, weapons, debts, and old powers that the institutions around them cannot safely control.
