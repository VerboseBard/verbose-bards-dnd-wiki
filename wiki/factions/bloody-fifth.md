# Bloody Fifth

The Bloody Fifth is a military unit with a reputation for brutality, effectiveness, and surviving the worst parts of the [Kreen War](../concepts/kreen-war.md).

## Description

The unit is associated with hard fighting, harsh command culture, and veterans who carry the war's uglier lessons into the postwar era.

Its command structure is built around disposable waves and relentless tempo. Command assumes losses, weaponizes fear, and treats every engagement as a question of how many bodies the line can afford to spend.

## Command Hierarchy

### General

**General Faelar Grimward**

Alias: "Old Blood and Guts"

Race: Elf

Role: Supreme commander of the Bloody Fifth

Authority scope:

- Defines doctrine
- Authorizes mass assaults
- Allocates ether and manpower
- Accepts catastrophic casualty ratios as normal

Presence:

- Appears only via portals, projections, or formal addresses
- Never enters combat zones
- Never exposed to danger
- Gives speeches, then leaves

Doctrine:

Bodies are cheaper than equipment.

Pressure breaks lines.

Pressure requires blood.

### Captains

Captains execute the general's doctrine. They control tempo, waves, and sacrifice.

**[Captain Aelira Vanthe](../people/captain-aelira-vanthe.md)**

- Race: Elf
- Gender: Female
- Role: Assault Captain, Bloody Fifth
- Status: Active after Session 1 actual play; wounded and stabilized on-screen
- Function: Arrives with the troops, stands before the men after the general's speech, assumes battlefield control after the first losses, and orders the assaults that matter

### Lieutenants

Lieutenants lead individual waves. They are visible, aggressive, and expected to die.

Actual-play and planning material currently preserve two overlapping lieutenant layers.

Confirmed actual-play continuity keeps [Lieutenant Rethiel Kaine](../people/lieutenant-rethiel-kaine.md) alive after Session 1 so he can appear in the Norinar operation in Session 2. A newer command-hierarchy note instead lists him as killed in the initial trench engagement. That conflict is tracked in the Session 1 canon check.

Clean wave-command notes identify:

- [Lieutenant Corvin Hale](../people/lieutenant-corvin-hale.md), logistics-minded Wave 2 commander tied to the ticker-silence scene
- [Lieutenant Sera Dorn](../people/lieutenant-sera-dorn.md), aggressive Wave 3 commander
- [Lieutenant Brenic Ashlow](../people/lieutenant-brenic-ashlow.md), quiet Wave 4 commander

### Sergeants

Sergeants command squads directly. They are the spine of the Fifth and the people closest to the troops.

Known Session 1 sergeants include:

- [Sergeant Halric Voss](../people/sergeant-halric-voss.md), introduced in the transcript as Sergeant Voss
- [Sergeant Feder](../people/sergeant-feder.md), corporal assignment sergeant
- [Sergeant Mireya Kest](../people/sergeant-mireya-kest.md), ticker-scene sergeant from planning notes

The current hierarchy also recognizes sergeants and squad leaders as the final layer of operational command, though their names vary by theater and casualty replacement.

## Ticker Silence

Session 1 turns on the failure of the tickers. Once the heavy guns go quiet, command understands that the line cannot hold for hours without replacement weapons or parts. Because fallback is not viable and the Bloody Fifth is unlikely to receive priority support while still technically holding, the unit attacks forward.

## Campaign Role

The Bloody Fifth appears in early military context, helping establish the culture of the unified army and the kinds of commanders who shaped the war.
