# The Ecclesiastical Order

The Ecclesiastical Order is the internal role hierarchy used by major Conclave-aligned churches. It is less a strict ladder of character level and more a structure of role, authority, and perception inside the church.

## Divine Pinnacle

### Exemplar

Class association: cleric.

Known as "the Living Ideal," an Exemplar is the highest spiritual authority among mortals. Exemplars embody what the faith should be. They are not always battlefield commanders or administrators, but everyone answers to the moral force of their example.

Exemplars guide doctrine, morality, and the broad direction of the church. They function as living saints, high clergy, and symbols of spiritual perfection.

## Divine Blooded and Interpreters

### Divine Bloodline

Class association: sorcerer.

Known as "Born of the Divine," Divine Bloodline figures are seen as chosen rather than trained. Their inherent power is treated as proof of divine favor, which often elevates them politically and spiritually.

Their authority comes from birthright, manifestation, and the visible mark of divine inheritance.

### Adjudicator

Class association: wizard.

Known as "the Voice of Doctrine," Adjudicators are scholars and interpreters of divine law. They define what is correct, heretical, permitted, or forbidden.

Adjudicators often serve as judges, theologians, legal authorities, and doctrinal architects.

Together, Divine Bloodline figures and Adjudicators shape much of the church's will: one through inherent divine legitimacy, the other through knowledge and interpretation.

## Enforcers of Faith

### Arbiter

Class association: paladin.

Known as "Judgment Made Flesh," Arbiters deliver divine justice. They act with authority to condemn, punish, or absolve, and are both feared and respected.

In normal church structure, an Arbiter is an enforcer of judgment. This should not be confused with the proposed [Arbiter of the Conclave](../concepts/arbiter-of-the-conclave.md), which is a separate secular oversight role offered to [Junvara](../people/junvara.md).

### Inquisitor

Class association: bard.

Known as "the Seeker of Truth," Inquisitors root out corruption, lies, hidden evil, and heresy. They extract confessions, expose falsehoods, and often decide who must be judged.

The common dynamic is simple:

- Inquisitors find the truth.
- Arbiters deliver the sentence.

## Martial Arm

### Templar

Class association: fighter.

Known as "Shield of the Faith," Templars are the organized military force of the church. They protect holy sites, hold territory, defend clergy, and fight wars.

Templars are loyal, disciplined, and direct. They fight the enemy in front of them.

### Justicar

Class association: ranger.

Known as "the Relentless Pursuit," Justicars hunt those who escape judgment. They track apostates, fugitives, deserters, and condemned targets who flee church authority.

Justicars may operate alone or in small units. A derogatory slang term for them is "Hounds," meaning those sent after anyone who runs. This is a church slang term and should not be confused with [Ash's Hounds](ashs-hounds.md).

## Fanatics and Faithful

### Zealot

Class association: barbarian.

Known as "Faith Without Restraint," Zealots embody raw devotion. They are often used as shock troops or divine berserkers. They are less controlled than Templars, but terrifyingly effective when pointed at an enemy.

## Divine Messengers

### Prophet

Class association: druid.

Known as "Voice of Revelation," Prophets receive visions, omens, and divine insight. They often operate outside the clean institutional hierarchy and may be feared, revered, ignored, or protected depending on the era and faction.

### Wayfarer / Friar

Class association: monk.

Working title unresolved.

Known as "Bearer of the Light," Wayfarers or Friars are traveling missionaries. They spread the faith among ordinary people, often carrying minimal possessions and maximum conviction.

## Redeemed and Hidden

### Penitent

Class association: rogue.

Known as "Those Who Seek Redemption," Penitents have committed grave sins or carry severe moral debts. They serve the church through actions that cannot be cleanly public: assassination, espionage, infiltration, sabotage, or other morally dark work.

They do what others cannot be seen doing.

### Envoy

Class association: warlock with celestial patron.

Known as "Voice of the Divine Servants," Envoys serve angels or divine intermediaries. They often act outside the normal structure, are frequently misunderstood, and remain sanctioned when their patronage is recognized.

## Authority Flow

- Exemplar: doctrine and ideal.
- Divine Bloodline / Adjudicator: interpretation, legitimacy, and power.
- Inquisitor: investigation.
- Arbiter: judgment.
- Templar / Justicar: enforcement.

## Simple Mental Model

- Exemplar: what is right.
- Adjudicator: what the law says.
- Inquisitor: what is true.
- Arbiter: what must be done.
- Justicar: no one escapes.
- Templar: we hold the line.
- Penitent: we do what must not be seen.
