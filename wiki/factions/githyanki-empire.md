# Gith'yanki Empire

The Gith'yanki Empire is the dominant military power of the [Astral Sea](../concepts/astral-sea.md) and the strongest of its six major sectors.

## Culture

Older cultural notes describe the Yanki as rigid, martial, xenophobic, and intensely hierarchical.

Sitting with someone is treated as a sign of great respect, while forcing someone to sit can be a humiliation or assertion of power. Eating is one of the only broadly protected social acts: consumption is treated as sacred enough that even duels are often delayed until after the meal ends.

Several formal terms survive in the notes:

- `Fus'hara`: formal greeting
- `Fus'nura`: formal farewell
- `Un'kleth`: idiot
- `Klar'tek`: half-blood, especially for infernal or angelic bloodlines

## Family and Social Order

Marriage is not central to Yanki society. Reproduction is treated as duty rather than intimacy, and motherhood is culturally minimized after birth. Fatherhood, however, is treated as one of the only socially permitted sites of open attachment, though even that affection is expected to remain controlled.

At age twelve, children are taken from their fathers and moved into the harsher path toward state-defined adulthood.

## City of Shame

The City of Shame is where the weak, failed, and socially unwanted are sent. Its people are called Yaerka. Some of the gith there reject Yanki identity and call themselves Zerai or Gith'Zerai, developing a more pacifistic, trade-oriented society with actual family structures and less hatred toward other races.

## Weapons and War Beasts

The Yanki are not gun-focused in the same way as the Giff, but they do field several distinctive ranged weapons:

- Hocao: an acid-launching pistol built from Thek venom systems
- Jaqars: living or semi-living ranged weapons that fire electrified spit
- Saentious: sentinel rifles using Somalygot blood to create a psychic domination link with the target

Their civilization also relies on:

- Hapar: flying and teleporting cities
- Qutear: whale-like living ships hollowed and modified into warcraft
- Krakars: heavily bred multi-limbed guard beasts used to protect ship cores

## Path to Adulthood

The martial path of Caerth is brutal. Surviving notes describe several key thresholds:

- Age 10: the Phas'ical, proving a base level of psionics
- Age 12: the Ja'hok, a week-long survival test among deadly Theks
- Age 15: the Bahabak, in which the initiate kills the Thek they bonded with and forges part of their own weapon from its body
- Age 23: the first raid, followed by a judgment over a Yaerka prisoner as proof of power over the weak

The rites are not framed as honorable by the notes. They are designed to normalize cruelty, domination, and emotional severance.

## Political Structure

The empire is partly matriarchal. A queen always rules at the top, but directly below her stands the Council Caerathaem, a body of five generals who control the major cities.

Below them are the Caohoks, then Charrek ship captains, then soldiers, then civilians, then Zerai, and finally all non-gith outsiders.

## Religion

The Yanki once honored [Tiamat](../people/tiamat.md), but most later rejected divine dependence and became functionally anti-theistic. The major exception is the [Gith'Charek](gith-charek.md), who retained the old [Tiamat](../people/tiamat.md) pact and rider tradition.
