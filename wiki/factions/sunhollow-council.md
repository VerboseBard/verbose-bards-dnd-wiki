# Sunhollow Council

The Sunhollow Council is the formal ruling body of [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md), combining clan elders, inherited obligations, and long-isolated valley politics.

## Structure

Each major clan holds one council seat:

- [Ashmane](ashmane.md)
- [Windcrest](windcrest.md)
- [Briarstep](briarstep.md)
- [Burrowroot](burrowroot.md)
- [Longbough](longbough.md)

Individual races within those clans still maintain their own speakers or chieftains, but those species-level leaders do not all hold formal votes.

The old [Moonhare](../people/edmund-moonhare.md) rabbit seat is no longer recognized. Rabbit voices are forced to speak through [Burrowroot](burrowroot.md), which remains one of the valley's central political grievances.

## Current Seated Elders

- [Siv Halven](../people/siv-halven.md) of Ashmane, the dominant temple-loyal and security-focused council voice
- [Sabine Voss](../people/sabine-voss.md) of Windcrest, observant, careful, and more willing to question old secrecy
- [June Mercer](../people/june-mercer.md) of Briarstep, practical, shrewd, politically flexible, and solution-oriented
- [Tavero Bellan](../people/tavero-bellan.md) of Burrowroot, formal, controlling, and determined to contain the rabbit/Moonhare issue
- [Matthias Duren](../people/matthias-duren.md) of Longbough, severe, disciplined, and an old-duty traditionalist tied to temple protocol

The council most often meets in [Hollowmere](../places/hollowmere.md).

During formal proceedings, the chamber can invoke older runic workings that close the doors, enable broad language comprehension, and suppress easy deceit. That truth-bound atmosphere becomes explicit in [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md).

The Session 7 transcript also shows the chamber's architecture preserving older elven stations or symbolic positions, including drow-associated structure. This supports the later suspicion that the valley's original masters were not simply generic elves.

## Hidden Duty

The council does not merely govern villages and disputes. Its elders also inherit the concealed duty of guarding [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md), which holds a fragment of Lolth's divinity.

Only the seated elders and their heirs know that full truth.

That does not mean other figures are unimportant. [Walter Wifflewick](../people/walter-wifflewick.md), the rabbit lore master, carries serious old-law influence inside council proceedings even without a formal vote.

Walter's public invocation of the emissary clause changes the council's options: because [Gwen](../people/gwen.md) qualifies as a sentient spider emissary, the party can legally approach forbidden sacred lands the valley's oath-bound locals cannot freely enter.

## Session 9 Crisis

By [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), the council is about to face the consequences of the party's temple return. [Bevar Lurton](../people/bevar-lurton.md) remains missing inside or with the temple work, the [Inquisition](inquisition.md) is approaching the valley, and [Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](../people/mistress-sila-vexry.md) claims she needs one to two weeks before the blood oath problem can be resolved.

[Sgt. Jefferson Stone](../people/sergeant-jefferson-stone.md) also realizes that the council site is the old elven conclave and may still be tied to valley defenses meant to admit elves rather than outsiders. The next council meeting therefore has to handle the temple oath, the Inquisition countdown, Rurik Valdren's possible intervention, and the risk that old defensive systems could kill the very people the council is trying to protect.

## Internal Currents

Three political currents matter most inside and around the council:

- [The Old Charge](old-charge.md), the secrecy-first isolationists
- [The Open Trail](open-trail.md), the cautious pragmatists
- [The Sixth Seat](sixth-seat.md), the quiet cause to restore rabbit dignity and standing
