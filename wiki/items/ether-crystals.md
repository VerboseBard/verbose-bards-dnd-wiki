# Ether Crystals

Ether crystals are condensed magical power sources used in weapons, teleportation, and advanced military or artificer systems.

## Description

Ether crystals occur naturally in regions of intense magical saturation, especially where ley lines intersect, in dungeons formed around arcane nexuses, and in places scarred by immense magical devastation.

Modern arcanists also experiment with artificial growth through controlled spellcasting into accumulators, though the process remains experimental and poorly understood.

## Nomenclature

The wiki uses `Ether Crystal` for the resource. Some item cards and device names use `Aether`, especially the [Aether Conductor](aether-conductor.md) line. Unless a later canon note separates them, these spellings point to the same crystal resource family.

## Forms

### Raw Ether Crystal

Raw Ether Crystals are jagged, irregular crystal growths shot through with deep violet and purple hues. They are unstable and highly reactive to magical energy. Their sharp protrusions and chaotic internal lattice make them dangerous to manipulate.

Introducing magic into a raw crystal often produces unpredictable effects: wild magic surges, amplification, violent discharge, or catastrophic detonation.

Raw crystals are typically mined using nonmagical tools. Magical equipment is avoided, and experienced operations may establish magic-dampening fields to prevent accidental reactions. Even so, mining ether crystals is dangerous and tightly regulated in most regions.

- Card category: wondrous item, uncommon.
- Value: 10 gp per pound.
- Economic role: primarily industrial, since the instability limits direct use.

### Processed Ether Crystal

Once the unstable outer lattice has been removed, the remaining crystal core is shaped and cut with jeweler's tools into a symmetrical, eight-sided cylindrical block with flat ends. This form dramatically stabilizes the crystal's internal structure, rendering it safer for magical interaction.

Processed Ether Crystals glow with calm luminous blue light, often faintly veined with residual purple. At this stage, the crystal can safely store, channel, and interact with spell energy without unpredictable effects.

Uses:

- Power source for arcane devices.
- Fuel for magical weapons and constructs.
- Intermediate form for further refinement.

- Card category: potion, rare. This is a D&D Beyond technical category; in-world, it is a processed crystal block.
- Value: 100 gp per pound.

### Refined Ether Crystal

Processed Ether Crystals can be broken down into numerous small disk-shaped pieces. Each refined crystal is a miniature eight-sided cylinder, roughly the size of a coin stood on edge, precisely cut to maximize magical efficiency.

These refined disks are the standardized fuel units used throughout magical society.

Uses:

- Powering arcane weapons such as [Aether Conductors](aether-conductor.md).
- Fuel for magical devices and tools.
- Consumable energy sources for magitech systems.

- Card category: potion, uncommon. This is a D&D Beyond technical category; in-world, it is a refined fuel disk.
- Value: 5 sp per refined disk.

## Ether Dust

The removed outer lattice becomes [Ether Dust](ether-dust.md), a volatile byproduct used in component substitution, alchemical work, and illicit magical narcotics.

## Campaign Role

Ether crystals explain why long-range teleportation has become costly and why military logistics depend on controlled supply. They power weapons such as [ether firearms](ether-firearms.md), the [Plasma Greatblade](two-handed-plasma-blade.md), and other magitech systems.

## Sunhollow Exception

[Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md) contains ether crystal deposits on a scale that shocks the party in [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md). Crystals rise out of [Lake of Glass](../places/lake-of-glass.md), appear on land in huge fields, and in some places stand like small hills or towers.

This is not normal ley-line crystal growth. Session 7 states that [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) has no ley lines and that Sunhollow's central crystal ecology is tied to ancient divine power connected to [Big Shad](../concepts/big-shad.md). Local people use the crystals casually as decorative glass, lamps, household objects, and part of the fish-fed valley ecology, while outsiders would see them as military, teleportation, and magitech wealth.

The party also confirms that outside ether weapons still function in the valley, making the discovery strategically dangerous.
