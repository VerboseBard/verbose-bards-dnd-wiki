# Fog Marker Tokens

Fog marker tokens are linked magical markers used to chart safe routes through [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md)'s fog.

## Description

Different marker types show different information on linked maps: safe camps, route turns, hazards, and emergency signals.

By the contractor era, the known marker types include:

- route markers for the main safe path
- camp or rest markers
- turn markers for route changes
- red danger markers for dead ends, lethal zones, or active threats

The markers are meant to be read on linked expedition maps rather than only by line of sight in the field.

## Campaign Role

The party uses or receives marker tokens for patrols, hidden discoveries, and the [Lost Homeland Mission](../concepts/lost-homeland-mission.md). Marker choice can determine whether a discovery goes to the military, the [Silk Parlor Network](../factions/silk-parlor-network.md), or remains secret.

In [Session 6](../sessions/session-6.md), the tokens become central to the contractor routefinding job. The party uses them to mark dead ends, preserve the final wagon-passable path, and keep the later expedition from blundering into the same mountain mistakes twice.

In [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), [Captain Havlin](../people/captain-havlin.md)'s patrol produces some of the party's marker discs. They were found with or on a dead human scout on the downhill side of the pass. [Voss](../people/voss-kenku.md) notes that the body had many arrows in it while denying that the patrol killed him.

That discovery confirms the route is no longer fully secret and that simple false-trail tricks may not be enough to protect [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md). The larger following expedition may already have enough of the original route to keep tracking forward or backtrack from a failed decoy path.
