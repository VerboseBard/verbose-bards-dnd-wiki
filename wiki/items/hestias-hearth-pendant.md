# Hestia's Hearth Pendant

Hestia's Hearth Pendant is the necklace that appears on [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](../people/sergeant-jefferson-stone.md) after he releases a surviving fragment of [Hestia](../people/hestia.md) in [Session 6](../sessions/session-6.md).

## Appearance

The pendant depicts an old kitchen: a fire, a table, and bread. It is not a symbol Jefferson recognizes from modern church practice.

Its meaning is felt before it is understood. It carries the sensation of home, remembered safety, and hearthfire rather than the militant divine presence most postwar soldiers would expect.

## Origin

In the rune-ruins on the route to [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md), [Jose](../people/jose.md) opens the [Music Box Anomaly](music-box-anomaly.md) to banish a chimera. The box pulls spirits toward itself and reveals a malevolent presence beyond its portal.

Jefferson resists that presence, bleeds onto the altar, and activates a hidden fragment of Hestia. After the fragment thanks him and burns away the old binding runes, the pendant is simply there around his throat.

## Known Mechanics

The live item text should be checked against the character sheet, but the Session 6 transcript confirms these working mechanics:

- while wearing the pendant, Jefferson can use the goddess's blessing to turn a natural 1 into a 20
- this blessing can be used proficiency bonus times per day
- the blessing can apply to travel or routefinding checks
- the pendant grants a backswing-as-bonus-action style benefit

Session 7's recap is the source that sharpens the natural-1 effect from `change a natural 1` to `turn a natural 1 into a 20`. The exact live item text should still be checked before play.

## Session 8 Resonance

In [Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md), [Bartholomew Hildebrant](../people/bartholomew-hildebrant.md) is recovered from a Fourth-Age stasis pod wearing [a medallion](bartholomews-hearth-medallion.md) identical or closely matching Jefferson's hearth pendant.

[The Ssar'Velyn Temple Guardian](../people/ssar-velyn-temple-guardian.md) notices the match between Bartholomew's medallion and Jefferson's amulet and treats it as significant. This suggests that the pendant is not an isolated blessing, but part of a broader Fourth-Age divine pattern tied to [Hestia](../people/hestia.md) and the old assault on [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md).

## Session 9 Resonance

In [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), [Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](../people/mistress-sila-vexry.md) claims Hestia still receives power through every act of hearth, home, welcome, and motherhood, even when people do not consciously know they are honoring her.

Bartholomew senses the combined presence of his medallion and Jefferson's pendant as a campfire in the void rather than only a distant candle. This confirms that the pendant is part of an active and growing divine-fragment pattern, not only a static blessing.

## Canon Caution

The transcript calls the blessing the blessing of `Hest`, goddess of the hearth. The wiki treats this as the same Fourth-Age goddess currently stabilized as [Hestia](../people/hestia.md), unless later table notes separate the names.
