# Ironwood Longbow

The Ironwood Longbow is an ancient strikeforce archer relic. It is a standalone relic rather than part of a set.

## Statistics

- Weapon type: martial longbow.
- Material: ironwood.
- Properties: heavy, two-handed.
- Requirement: Strength 18.
- Attack stat: Dexterity.
- Bonus damage: add Strength modifier to attack and damage rolls if the Strength requirement is met.
- Damage: 2d10 + Dexterity modifier + Strength modifier.
- Damage type: piercing.
- Range: 300 feet / 1200 feet.
- Attunement: required.
- Rate of fire: two shots per Attack action.
- Upgrade slots: none.

## Titan Draw

This bow requires Strength 18 to wield effectively. If the wielder meets the requirement, they add their Strength modifier to attack and damage rolls made with the weapon.

## Summoned Arrow

When the string is drawn, the bow magically forms a +2 arrow. The listed base damage does not include the arrow's bonus.

## War Bow Rate

When the wielder takes the Attack action with this bow, they may make two ranged attacks with it as part of that action.

## Spell-Drinker

A number of times per long rest equal to proficiency bonus, when a spell of 7th level or lower is cast and the wielder can perceive it, they may use their reaction to draw the spell into the bow. The bow consumes the spell, causing it to be wasted and preventing its effects.

## Ironwood Resistance

Ironwood naturally resists many forms of magic, allowing the bow to endure hostile magical environments and channel the Spell-Drinker effect.

## Origin Note

The bow was recovered from the remains of an ancient human archer who died before completing his mission in the temple's lower approach. Unlike the dark archer relics, this weapon stands alone, built for brutal draw strength, impossible range, and anti-magic warfare.
