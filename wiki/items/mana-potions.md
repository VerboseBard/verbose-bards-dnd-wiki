# Mana Potions

Mana potions are alchemical solutions derived from properly refined [Ether Dust](ether-dust.md).

## Description

Unlike raw [Ether Dust](ether-dust.md), mana potions are stable when used as intended, though misuse can still cause harm. They restore spellcasting capacity rather than raw health.

## Use

When a creature drinks a mana potion, it rolls the listed dice and gains that many spell points. These points can be spent immediately to restore any combination of expended spell slots, with each slot costing points equal to its level.

For example, a 1st-level spell slot costs 1 point, a 2nd-level slot costs 2 points, and so on.

Mana potions can only be safely used once per day.

## Potion Grades

### Common Mana Potion

- Wondrous item, common
- Market value: 50 gp
- Restores 1d4 spell points

### Greater Mana Potion

- Wondrous item, uncommon
- Market value: 100 gp
- Restores 2d4 spell points

### Superior Mana Potion

- Wondrous item, rare
- Market value: 500 gp
- Restores 3d4 spell points

### Supreme Mana Potion

- Wondrous item, very rare
- Market value: 1,000 gp
- Restores 4d4 spell points

## Rules

- Spell points can be spent to restore expended spell slots of a level the drinker can cast.
- You may only benefit from one mana potion every 24 hours.
- Consuming more than one mana potion in a 24-hour period triggers the overuse warning below.

## Overuse Warning

If a creature consumes more than one mana potion within a 24-hour period, it must roll 1d4. It permanently loses that many spell slots, choosing which levels are lost, until `Greater Restoration` or equivalent magic restores them.

Overuse can cause long-term damage to magical pathways.

## Ether Dust Warning

If [Ether Dust](ether-dust.md) is added to a mana potion, the mixture is treated as direct [Ether Dust](ether-dust.md) ingestion and triggers all contamination effects.
