# Widow's Bracer

Widow's Bracer is a dark elf assassin relic recovered in [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md). It is a standalone relic rather than part of a set.

Forged for temple hunters and silent killers, this bracer casts living silk with predatory precision. Its web catches what the knife cannot, and its line carries the wearer where shadows cannot walk.

## Statistics

- Item type: wondrous item, bracelet / bracer.
- Material: dark drow steel, spider silk, and a shadow-web reservoir.
- Properties: wrist-worn, lightweight, web-forged.
- Attack stat: Dexterity.
- Range: 60 feet.
- Activation: action / special.
- Uses: Grapple Line at will; Entangling Shot proficiency bonus per long rest.
- Attunement: required.
- Upgrade slots: none.

## Entangling Shot

As an action, the wielder fires a webline at a creature within 60 feet. The target must make a Strength saving throw or become restrained by magical spider silk.

## Web of Hunger

A restrained creature may use its action to repeat the Strength save, breaking free on a success. If it does not break free on its turn, small spiders swarm from the silk and bite it, dealing 2d8 damage each turn until freed.

## Grapple Line

The wielder may fire the webline at a wall, ledge, ceiling, or anchor point within 60 feet and pull themselves across gaps, up surfaces, or toward elevated positions.

## Widow's Reach

The bracer's silk is unnaturally strong and precise, making it ideal for assassins, ambushers, and temple hunters.

## Webline Detail

A living strand of enchanted spider silk launches from the hidden spinneret, clinging with unnatural strength and flexing like a predator's tether.

## Origin Note

The bracer was recovered from an ancient cloaked dark elf assassin in the temple stasis chamber. It was found alongside the [Twin Bloodsteel Daggers](twin-bloodsteel-daggers.md) and [Shroud of Invisibility](shroud-of-invisibility.md).

## Power Source

Shadow-web resonance stores a reservoir of enchanted silk and predatory drow magic, awakened by motion and intent.
