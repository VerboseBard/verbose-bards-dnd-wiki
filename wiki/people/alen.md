# Alen

Alen was the figure later known as the [Raven Queen of Saharun](raven-queen-of-saharun.md), tied to [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md), the old vampire queen's holdings, and the hidden ritual spaces beneath the [Silverbend Coastal Cave Hideout](../places/silver-bend-coastal-cave-hideout.md). Late [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) material identifies her as a former queen of vampires with ambitions tied to death, undeath, and godhood, and later records show that those ambitions and titles converged in the same person rather than in separate death powers.

## Raven Queen Identity

Alen and the [Raven Queen of Saharun](raven-queen-of-saharun.md) are the same being.

That matters because older pages sometimes describe "the Raven Queen" and "Alen" from different angles: one as a throne or mantle within [Saharun](../places/saharun.md)'s religious history, the other as a manipulative family figure tied to [Belle](belle.md), [Lilith](lilith.md), and [Gregario](gregario-shaq.md). Both refer to the same person at different stages of her story and under different names or roles.

## Family Position

Alen is not the biological mother of [Belle](belle.md), [Eve / Eva](eva.md), or [Lilith](lilith.md).

Instead, Alen occupies a more tangled family role. She is treated in later material as an elder family figure to [Belle](belle.md) and, in the [Hunt for the Black Vipers](../campaign/hunt-for-black-vipers.md) line especially, as something much closer to a stepfather or parental-guardian figure in [Lilith](lilith.md)'s life.

That distinction matters because Alen is often spoken about with family language, but the birth line belongs to [Belle](belle.md).

## Hidden Room

The cave shrine includes a protected portrait of a younger, softer Alen alongside several figures from her earlier life. The image feels out of place among the blood rites and serpent iconography, leaving its purpose unclear: anchor, memory, mockery, or warning.

## Legacy

The [Cobra Cult of Rax'al Karath](../factions/cobra-cult-of-raxal-karath.md) considers Alen a polluting presence on what they regard as holy ground. [Junara](junara.md) considers her old rooms too dangerous to disturb without preparation.

## Fifth Age Ambition

In the extracted [War of the Gods, Session 19](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-19.md), Alen appears much earlier in the timeline as an active power rather than a finished relic. There she describes herself as a former vampire queen who lost everything because she had not yet ascended to true godhood, and she frames her current agenda as the seizure of authority over death, afterlife, and undeath.

The same material also ties her to warnings about the [Divine Gate](../concepts/divine-gate.md), dungeon cores, and recombining worlds. Whether all of her claims are objectively correct is harder to prove, but the session makes clear that she was not merely a local vampire problem. She was thinking in setting-scale metaphysical terms long before later [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) fallout closed around her story.

In [War of the Gods, Session 21](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-21.md), Alen's manipulation continues in quieter form. She privately reassures Rose that her service is almost complete, praises [Gregario / Shaq](gregario-shaq.md) for sacrifices the rest of the party does not fully understand, and keeps framing herself as the one who appreciates what others dismiss. It is a textbook example of how she turns dependency, gratitude, and exhaustion into leverage.

In [War of the Gods, Session 22](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-22.md), that manipulation becomes much more direct. Rose dies while still tied to Alen's orbit, [Gregario](gregario-shaq.md) is urged to claim Rose's gear after the death, and Alen later shows him the newborn child she considers most precious and frames that child as his future charge or reward.

The same session also reveals that Alen remains one of the powers others still suspect of playing a much larger board than the party can see. Even when she is not visibly moving armies, she is still shaping loyalties, inheritances, and who gets used as part of the Gate crisis.

In [War of the Gods, Session 23](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-23.md), Alen finally enters the [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md) crisis directly. She manifests to confront [Lucian](lucian.md) during the first portal-wave siege, tries to stop him from capitalizing on the collapse around the death domain, and is killed in the clash. Her fall immediately strips away much of the divine structure [Gregario](gregario-shaq.md) had still been using, even if it does not erase all the soul-level changes already carved into him.

## Birth Assistance and Aftermath

Alen assisted [Belle](belle.md) during the birth process that produced [Eve](eva.md) and [Lilith](lilith.md), but that is not the same thing as being their biological parent.

Later family material suggests that after Alen's own death in that older chain of events, [Belle](belle.md) effectively retained or reclaimed a maternal role over the children and raised them until the later [Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md)-era [Divine Gate](../concepts/divine-gate.md) alterations pulled the girls apart and sent them back out into the worlds.

## End

[Annabelle](annabelle.md) removes the last active influence of Alen from [Gregario / Shaq](gregario-shaq.md). After this later [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) endpoint, Alen cannot return in any form. The Raven Queen mantle that had belonged to Alen now passes toward [Lilith](lilith.md) instead of consuming her.
