# Alen

Alen was the figure later known as the [Raven Queen of Saharun](raven-queen-of-saharun.md), tied to [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md), the old vampire queen's holdings, and the hidden ritual spaces beneath the [Silverbend Coastal Cave Hideout](../places/silver-bend-coastal-cave-hideout.md). Late [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) material identifies her as a former queen of vampires with ambitions tied to death, undeath, and godhood, and later records show that those ambitions and titles converged in the same person rather than in separate death powers.

## Raven Queen Identity

Alen and the [Raven Queen of Saharun](raven-queen-of-saharun.md) are the same being.

That matters because her story spans both the Raven Queen mantle within [Saharun](../places/saharun.md)'s religious history and the manipulative family role tied to [Belle](belle.md), [Lilith](lilith.md), and [Gregario](gregario-shaq.md). Both belong to the same person at different stages of her story and under different names or roles.

The Raven Queen mantle had not been properly used since [the Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md), because the previous Raven Queen was killed during the war with the gods at the end of the [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md). Alen's ascension reoccupied that mantle, but she distorted what it originally was.

## Planned Death and Ascension

Alen's death during [Lucius](lucius.md)'s assault was part of a deliberate strategy. Before the trial material in which the vampire queen's loss of control was judged, Alen was already an extremely powerful queen of vampires, close to divine power but still lacking a true mantle.

Alen knew Lucius intended the assassination strike. Rather than prevent her body's destruction, she allowed it to happen so other powers would stop looking for her. Through a deal with [Belle](belle.md), she had learned enough of the [Divine Soul Anchoring](../concepts/divine-soul-anchoring.md) method to let death become a hiding place and an ascension route rather than a final end.

The power released by multiple divine deaths during that era helped Alen begin moving toward godhood. This is one of the reasons her later Raven Queen identity should be read as an intentional, distorted claim on death authority rather than a clean inheritance.

## Family Position

Alen is not the biological mother of [Belle](belle.md). Her role in [Eve](eve.md) and [Lilith](lilith.md)'s origins is narrower and darker than simple parentage.

Alen helps arrange the cores and circumstances that allow [Experiment 69](experiment-69.md) and [Mummy](mummy.md) to grow at an accelerated rate. Her specific claim is over Lilith. When the time comes, Alen bites Mummy, transforms him into a vampire, and kills him. Belle then magically prepares and consumes Mummy's corpse as part of the birth working that creates Lilith.

That distinction matters because Eve and Lilith are twins in Belle's family, but their origin chains are not identical. Eve comes through Experiment 69. Lilith comes through Mummy's vampire-transformed line and is given to Alen after birth.

## Hidden Room

The cave shrine includes a protected portrait of a younger, softer Alen alongside several figures from her earlier life. The image feels out of place among the blood rites and serpent iconography, leaving its purpose unclear: anchor, memory, mockery, or warning.

## Legacy

The [Cobra Cult of Rax'al Karath](../factions/cobra-cult-of-raxal-karath.md) considers Alen a polluting presence on what they regard as holy ground. [Junvara](junvara.md) considers her old rooms too dangerous to disturb without preparation.

## Fifth Age Ambition

In the extracted [War of the Gods, Session 19](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-19.md), Alen appears much earlier in the timeline as an active power rather than a finished relic. There she describes herself as a former vampire queen who lost everything because she had not yet ascended to true godhood, and she frames her current agenda as the seizure of authority over death, afterlife, and undeath.

The same material also ties her to warnings about the [Divine Gate](../concepts/divine-gate.md), dungeon cores, and recombining worlds. Whether all of her claims are objectively correct is harder to prove, but the session makes clear that she was not merely a local vampire problem. She was thinking in setting-scale metaphysical terms long before later [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) fallout closed around her story.

In [War of the Gods, Session 21](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-21.md), Alen's manipulation continues in quieter form. She privately reassures Rose that her service is almost complete, praises [Gregario / Shaq](gregario-shaq.md) for sacrifices the rest of the party does not fully understand, and keeps framing herself as the one who appreciates what others dismiss. It is a textbook example of how she turns dependency, gratitude, and exhaustion into leverage.

In [War of the Gods, Session 22](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-22.md), that manipulation becomes much more direct. Rose dies while still tied to Alen's orbit, [Gregario](gregario-shaq.md) is urged to claim Rose's gear after the death, and Alen later shows him the newborn child she considers most precious and frames that child as his future charge or reward.

The same session also reveals that Alen remains one of the powers others still suspect of playing a much larger board than the party can see. Even when she is not visibly moving armies, she is still shaping loyalties, inheritances, and who gets used as part of the Gate crisis.

In [War of the Gods, Session 23](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-23.md), Alen finally enters the [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md) crisis directly. She manifests to confront [Lucius](lucius.md) during the first portal-wave siege, tries to stop him from capitalizing on the collapse around the death domain, and has her manifested body destroyed in the clash. Her fall immediately strips away much of the divine structure [Gregario](gregario-shaq.md) had still been using, even though the death also belonged to Alen's own contingency logic.

## Birth Assistance and Aftermath

Alen's assistance in the birth process is chiefly the preparation of Lilith's side of the bargain: arranging accelerated growth, claiming Mummy through vampiric transformation, and receiving Lilith after Belle carries and births her.

Later family material suggests that Belle helps arrange circumstances that lead to Alen's death at [Lucius](lucius.md)'s hands. After that, Belle reclaims Lilith and raises both girls until the later [Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md)-era [Divine Gate](../concepts/divine-gate.md) alterations pull them apart and send them back out into the worlds.

## End

[Annabelle](annabelle.md) removes the last active influence of Alen from [Gregario / Shaq](gregario-shaq.md). After this later [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) endpoint, Alen cannot return in any form. The Raven Queen mantle that had belonged to Alen now passes toward [Lilith](lilith.md) instead of consuming her.
