# Alistair Hooley

Alistair Hooley is Josh's harengon ranger / gloom stalker in the current [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md) party and a distant relative of the old Moonhare rabbit line of [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md).

## Bloody Fifth Origin

Session 1 notes place Alistair in Wave 3, Squad 12 as Josh's veteran character. That early placement links him to the same Bloody Fifth survival cluster as [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](sergeant-jefferson-stone.md), [Captain Sgt. Jose](jose.md), and [Bevar Lurton](bevar-lurton.md).

## Session 4 Notes

In Session 4, Alistair is the one who first senses that something is wrong in the party's barracks room after the Inquisition arrival. The search reveals that the room and lockers were thoroughly inspected and then put back together under a new lock signature.

Later, aboard the hidden ship, Alistair is hesitant about the nanite pods and the tubes, but eventually enters one voluntarily. The raw transcript records his enhancement as Dexterity +4 and Intelligence +1.

In Session 5, Alistair is also tied to the bridge and ship-tech discovery. He helps identify corridor pressure-plate danger, is present for the first coherent Kubix conversation, and is the party member most naturally pointed toward the advanced [Ship Sniper Rifle](../items/ship-sniper-rifle.md).

## Session 6 Notes

In Session 6, Alistair remains one Intelligence point short of fully attuning to the [Ship Sniper Rifle](../items/ship-sniper-rifle.md). He can use the weapon only in its base rifle form, without the advanced critical function or normal proficiency calculations.

He also has the [Kex](../places/kex.md) fabricate a compact metal quarterstaff from unused scimitars. The exact item name is unclear in the transcript and should be checked against the live character sheet before being treated as a finalized magic item.

On the route to [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md), Alistair is struck by the magical lightning storm and later uses truesight in the ruins to see spirits being drawn toward the [Music Box Anomaly](../items/music-box-anomaly.md). He also notices [Bevar Lurton](bevar-lurton.md) pocketing something from a rune mechanism.

In [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), that hidden item is revealed as a book. Alistair recognizes it as the object Bevar took from the Session 6 mechanism.

## Sunhollow Connection

Alistair is not a direct ruler or dynastic head, but his blood tie to the lost rabbit line is close enough to matter once Sunhollow's people recognize what he is connected to.

That connection has the potential to reopen questions the valley spent more than 120 years burying:

- whether the Moonhare line truly ended
- whether the rabbit folk were stripped of standing opportunistically
- whether the present council order still has the right to keep that decision settled

In [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), Alistair's arrival in [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md) immediately triggers genealogy questions inside [Burrowroot Warren](../places/burrowroot-warren.md) and private warnings from [Walter Wifflewick](walter-wifflewick.md), who believes Alistair's return is politically dangerous to the present balance.

The first proof of that connection comes at the end of Session 6: the party reaches the high pass, Alistair recognizes an old landmark from family stories, and the first Sunhollow patrol's hostility softens when they see him.

Session 7 sharpens the family problem. Rabbit elders suggest that Alistair's father's line is not the only relevant branch and that his mother's line may also matter. The missing old rabbit heir vanished with the last outward expedition, key responsibilities were never passed down, and the rabbit seat collapsed into Burrowroot afterward.

## Ssar'Velyn Temple

In [Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md), Alistair enters the [Dead Lands](../places/dead-lands.md) and [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md) with the party.

He recognizes the old elven/drow-spider language and fall narrative in the temple mosaics, connecting the site to the book [Bevar Lurton](bevar-lurton.md) took from the Session 6 ruins and revealed in Session 7. During the temple trials, Alistair reads enemy roles, identifies dangerous terrain, shoots a crystal hard enough to damage several enemies through backlash, and later uses the ether conductor blade to cut himself free from webbing.

Alistair also recognizes the guardian's blood-magic healing as matching the old temple magic described in Bevar's book. That recognition makes him one of the party members best positioned to understand how Ssar'Velyn's ancient law, blood magic, and spider rites connect.

In [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), Alistair helps turn the temple loot into usable information by identifying the recovered potions. His work clarifies the mana-restoration potions, the dangerous stat-boosting spider mutation potion, and the deadly green ingested poison, though final item-card naming and counts still need table confirmation before full item pages are created.
