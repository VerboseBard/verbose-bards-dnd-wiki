# Bartholomew Hildebrant

Bartholomew Hildebrant is Jack's dwarf barbarian champion in the [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md).

## Fourth-Age Survivor

Bartholomew enters the current party's story during [Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md), but he is not originally from the Seventh Age.

He wakes in a Fourth-Age temple assault, badly wounded and partially amnesiac, with a wood elf cleric named [Katrina](katrina.md) trying to keep him alive. The assault has already gone catastrophically wrong: spider eggs are shattered, drow attackers are dead, a dead orc wizard named [Oscar](oscar-fourth-age-wizard.md) is nearby, and the rest of the strike teams are probably gone.

After Katrina is killed by an invisible assassin, Bartholomew fights through the remaining defenders while poisoned and badly injured. His knees are cut apart, leaving him unable to walk, but he kills the assassin and drags himself into a stasis pod with his axe and crossbow rather than surrender.

Session 8 later reveals that his assault was part of a much larger Fourth-Age strike: roughly ten teams, about forty people total, including his cleric, wizard, and a small goblin. The assault nearly brought the temple down and left very few guardians behind.

## Session 8 Recovery

Centuries later, [Captain Sgt. Jose](jose.md) and [Alistair Hooley](alistair-hooley.md) find Bartholomew still preserved in the pod exactly as he entered it: poisoned, bleeding, full of broken arrows, and with ruined knees.

The party stabilizes him with a greater healing potion, but his knees remain unusable. Bartholomew joins the temple trials by riding on Jose's shoulders, turning his inability to walk into a brutal mounted-combat arrangement.

During the trials, Bartholomew proves immediately lethal despite his ruined legs. He beheads a monk, helps kill the assassin, shoots down a lightning sorcerer, and is thrown by Jose to kill a summoner mid-flight. His anger keeps him moving, but an insight moment shows that the void inside him now contains a distant candle or spark, suggesting more than revenge is pulling him forward.

## Hestia Link

Bartholomew carries [a medallion](../items/bartholomews-hearth-medallion.md) matching the amulet associated with [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](sergeant-jefferson-stone.md). The temple guardian notices the connection after the trials, suggesting that Bartholomew and Jefferson may both be tied to the same killed Fourth-Age divine power connected to [Hestia](hestia.md).

In [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), that link becomes personal rather than only symbolic. When Hestia's name is spoken, Bartholomew feels the soul-pain inside him fade for the first time he can remember. Near Jefferson's pendant and his own medallion, the divine presence feels like a campfire in the void rather than a distant candle.

Bartholomew later prays to Hestia and receives warmth, comfort, and a physical strengthening of his body. The same session also connects him to [Rurik Valdren](rurik-valdren.md), who recognizes him as an ancient dwarven soldier and starts pressing for his house, tattoos, and old military identity.
