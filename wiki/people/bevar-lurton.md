# Bevar Lurton

Bevar Lurton is an artificer-leaning party member in the [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md).

Table note: Bevar was originally played by Clayton. After Clayton left the table, Bevar became a character run by the DM.

## Bloody Fifth Origin

In the Session 1 command notes, Bevar appears as a veteran in Wave 3, Squad 12 alongside [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](sergeant-jefferson-stone.md), [Alistair Hooley](alistair-hooley.md), and [Captain Sgt. Jose](jose.md).

## Role

Bevar fills several of the party's technical and support gaps at once.

He appears as:

- a crafter and item-support specialist
- the party member most willing to experiment with odd biology and ship technology
- a character with strong alchemical or biochemical instincts

## Session 4 Notes

In Session 4, Bevar's support role shows up in the Fort Victory downtime and the hidden ship sequence. The party artificer crafts Cure Wounds scrolls before discharge, and aboard the crashed ship Bevar helps inspect the pod room and nanite systems after Jose is forcibly enhanced.

Bevar then becomes the first voluntary pod test after Jose. The raw transcript records his nanite result as a major Constitution increase to 28, reinforcing his later role as the party member most willing to treat strange ship biology as an experiment.

## Gwen and the Ship

In [Session 5](../sessions/session-5.md), Bevar finds the surviving alien spider specimen hidden in the [Crashed Ship](../places/crashed-ship.md)'s research storage. The spider is not named in that moment yet, but the transcript establishes her as a viable female specimen from the ship's spider/uplift research and gives Bevar the first rudimentary connection with her.

By [Session 6](../sessions/session-6.md), that bond has deepened enough that he:

- names the spider [Gwen](gwen.md)
- treats her like a companion rather than cargo
- introduces the rest of the party to her while teaching her tea etiquette
- comforts and protects her through the storm, cave rest, and ruin danger

## Ruins and Harvesting

Session 6 also shows Bevar's stranger side.

He:

- harvests neurotoxic material from the mutated displacer beast after the ravine fight
- works on the active rune-puzzle mechanisms inside the ruins
- appears to quietly pocket the result of at least one solved ruin mechanism without fully explaining it to the others

That combination makes him one of the campaign's more quietly dangerous knowledge-holders: the party member most likely to leave a scene carrying something powerful in his pocket.

In [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), the pocketed object is revealed as a book. Bevar produces it during the council discussion and says he is learning what the spider means.

Known contents include old elven or drow-associated magic, spider-summoning or spider-calling instructions, blood magic that transfers life force through mingled blood, and mirror-related spell work. The full contents remain unresolved.

In [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), his bond with [Gwen](gwen.md) becomes politically explosive when [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md)'s old law recognizes her as a valid emissary for entry into the forbidden blighted lands.

## Session 8 Emissary Separation

In [Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md), Bevar becomes increasingly distant from the party and increasingly focused on [Gwen](gwen.md). He is short with the others, appears to listen to Gwen's guidance, and insists that the party's job is not only to identify the corruption in [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md) but to fix it.

At the temple entrance, Bevar kneels before the ghost priestess and frames the party as servants of a mistress. Gwen is recognized as the lawful emissary, and Bevar follows her into the temple's emissary path while the rest of the party is forced into survival trials.

By the end of the session, Bevar and Gwen remain with or inside the temple for unresolved emissary work. His status after Session 8 is therefore separated from the main party pending the outcome of the temple sequence.

## Session 9 Status

In [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), the rest of the party is returned outside [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md), but Bevar is still missing.

The party discusses his increasingly strange behavior and treats his absence as dangerous. It remains unclear whether Bevar is acting under Gwen's influence, the temple's influence, his own choices, or some mixture of all three.
