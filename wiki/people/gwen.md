# Gwen

Gwen is the surviving alien spider specimen recovered from the [Crashed Ship](../places/crashed-ship.md) during the [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md).

## Description

Gwen is a small spider from the ship's preserved research stock, apparently from another world or dimensional background rather than normal [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) wildlife.

In the Session 5 transcript, she is recovered from stasis as a viable tarantula-sized female specimen with yellow, red, and black coloration. Kubix describes the ship's research as involving spiders, uplift, and a spinner collective or similar line. The name `Gwen` comes from later continuity; the first scene only establishes the surviving spider and her initial connection to [Bevar Lurton](bevar-lurton.md).

By the start of [Session 6](../sessions/session-6.md), she:

- has formed a strange connection with [Bevar Lurton](bevar-lurton.md)
- responds to handling and attention more like a familiar or companion than a lab specimen
- is already being folded into the party's daily routine
- has been named Gwen
- is being taught tea etiquette by Bevar

## Campaign Role

Gwen is one of the quieter but more revealing results of the [Kex](../places/kex.md) detour.

She proves that the ship was not only carrying weapons and nanite technology. It was also carrying live research stock, preserved organisms, and forms of life that do not belong naturally to the local world.

That makes Gwen both a character detail and a worldbuilding clue.

By [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), she becomes much more than a clue. [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md)'s old sacred law recognizes Gwen as a sentient spider emissary, which gives the party lawful authority to approach the blighted forbidden lands around [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md).

The council's reaction is divided. Some representatives are visibly unhappy about the spider, while the Mapach/raccoon representative is excited, the rabbit lore master is intrigued, and the Hadozee observer remains more neutral. Gwen's gestures before the council are treated as meaningful enough to affect old-law procedure.

In [Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md), Gwen's emissary status becomes active rather than theoretical. She appears to guide [Bevar Lurton](bevar-lurton.md) toward the temple, and the ghost priestess recognizes her as a valid emissary. Gwen and Bevar are separated from the rest of the party and remain behind for unresolved temple work after the others complete the trials.

This makes Gwen one of the central keys to [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md): not simply a spider companion, but an entity whose presence can satisfy ancient access law.

In [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), Bevar remains absent after the rest of the party leaves the temple. Gwen's exact status is still unknown, but her emissary role is now tied to one of the campaign's most dangerous unresolved questions: whether she is opening a way to fix the temple, enabling Lolth-linked work, or moving according to a logic the party does not yet understand.
