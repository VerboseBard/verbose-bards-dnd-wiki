# Hawk

Hawk was a party member in [Logan's Second Campaign](../campaign/logans-second-campaign.md), a [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) arc that begins roughly three years before [The Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md).

## Role

Hawk appears in the surviving notes as a practical frontline member of the group moving through Clipper Bay, Wilbrix, Salvation, and eventually [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md). He is less flamboyant than some of the other personalities around him, but he repeatedly shows up as the one who keeps functioning during chaos, tracks threats, protects allies, and keeps moving when stranger powers start tearing the campaign open.

## Blessing of Dione

Early in the campaign, after a lord is killed during fighting in Clipper Bay, the ground shakes, light descends, and Hawk is blessed with the knowledge of [Dione](dione.md), one of the powers later named in the [Council of Gods of the Fifth Age](../concepts/council-of-gods-of-the-fifth-age.md).

That tie matters for the rest of his arc. The notes repeatedly track "foresight" around Hawk's sessions, and later dream material makes it clearer that [Dione](dione.md) is not a one-time blessing. Hawk receives warnings, guidance, and prophetic pressure connected to the coming darkness.

In one later dream, [Dione](dione.md) warns Hawk that darkness and hard choices are coming, while also affirming that he has remained true to the totem of his people.

## Totem Path

Hawk spends long stretches of the campaign trying to turn into a bear or otherwise deepen his bond with his totem nature. The notes track these training hours directly, showing that this was not flavor text but an active long-term discipline in the campaign.

That same totem thread is reinforced by [Dione](dione.md)'s later dream-warning. Hawk is not simply a traveler with a weapon. He is someone being measured against an inherited spiritual standard while the world around him gets stranger and more violent.

## Second Campaign Arc

Across the surviving notes, Hawk is present for several major parts of the campaign's middle arc:

- the Wilbrix religious-political unrest tied to [Sedna](sedna.md)'s worship and anti-outsider pressure
- the expedition contract that pulls the group toward [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md)
- the Salvation dock fight where armored merfolk attack during the night
- the troll and slaad trouble in the swamps
- the naming of the party as "the revengers"
- the long overland and vehicle-based travel toward mines, deserts, and salt flats
- the growing instability around Ayden, [Aylinn](aylinn.md), [Wendell](wendell.md), and lunar or divine corruption
- the arrival in [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md), manor purchase, guild contact, and deeper political trouble

Hawk also shows a practical social side in these notes. He throws axes in taverns, socializes easily enough when off duty, plays with the party's panther companion, and can function in inns and cities without losing his grounding.

## Herbert's Death and Ayden's Manipulation

One of Hawk's more painful turning points comes when Herbert is killed in a conflict with merfolk while Ayden prevents Hawk from going to help.

Later notes make it clear Hawk begins to realize how much Ayden has manipulated him. That matters because the campaign increasingly turns on corruption, divided selves, divine pressure, and companions becoming dangerous. Hawk's arc is therefore not only about prophecy and totems, but about recognizing when trust inside the party has been poisoned.

## Silverbend and the Darker Turn

By the time the campaign reaches [Silverbend](../places/silver-bend.md), Hawk is caught in a much darker setting: moon rituals, curses, assassins, vampire pressure, guild politics, dragon fallout, and attacks tied to undead incursions and older powers.

Even there, Hawk remains one of the steadier figures in the notes. He fights off assassins in the hotel attack, tracks danger through enchanted objects, senses a connection between an evil dwarf staff and the undead incursion against the drow, and continues trying to make sense of the campaign while others around him slide further into spectacle or corruption.

## Possible Sixth-Age Links

The strongest continuity question in Hawk's notes is the repeated appearance of a figure named [Wendell](wendell.md).

Hawk's campaign places a magically capable [Wendell](wendell.md) in active party orbit years before the late-[Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) [War of the Gods / New Beginning Campaign](../campaign/war-of-the-gods.md), where [Wendell](wendell.md) becomes one of [Lucius](lucius.md)'s central agents. The name overlap, magical prominence, and setting importance make this a plausible continuity link, but the current notes are not strong enough to treat it as fully confirmed identity rather than a same-name precursor or unstable archival overlap.

## New Beginning Echo

Surviving notes from [Kestrel](kestrel.md) place a human named Hawk directly inside the late-[Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) New Beginning mine ritual. In that scene, Hawk appears with a dark leopard and a staff, speaks a word of power, and helps diminish [Aylinn](aylinn.md)'s local hold so awareness of other deities can return.

That does not solve every continuity issue around Hawk's lifespan or exact identity, but it does make him one of the clearest [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) player figures to echo forward into later [War of the Gods / New Beginning Campaign](../campaign/war-of-the-gods.md) material.
