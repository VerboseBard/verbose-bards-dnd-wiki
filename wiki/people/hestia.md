# Hestia

Hestia is a goddess of the [Fourth Age](../concepts/fourth-age.md), associated with the hearth, home, refuge, and remembered safety.

She was long thought to have been killed in the [Fourth Age](../concepts/fourth-age.md), which makes her manifestation in [Session 6](../sessions/session-6.md) of the [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md) extremely important.

## Divine Role

Hestia is tied to:

- hearthfire
- home and sanctuary
- bread, table, and kitchen
- maternal refuge
- the emotional memory of belonging somewhere safe

Her presence represents an older form of divine protection than the broken postwar world usually offers.

## Session 6 Manifestation

After the [Music Box Anomaly](../items/music-box-anomaly.md) reveals a deeply malevolent presence beyond its portal and begins drawing spirits into itself, [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](sergeant-jefferson-stone.md) bleeds onto an altar in the nearby ruins.

That blood activates the site and reveals one of Hestia's surviving hidden fragments.

She:

- appears as the emotional opposite of the force inside the box
- thanks Jefferson directly
- burns away the old runes binding or sealing the altar
- leaves him with a blessing tied to hearth, home, and sanctuary
- leaves a physical [Hestia's Hearth Pendant](../items/hestias-hearth-pendant.md) around Jefferson's throat

The pendant's visible symbol is an old kitchen with a fire, table, and bread. The Session 6 transcript calls the blessing the blessing of `Hest`, goddess of the hearth; the wiki currently treats Hest and Hestia as the same Fourth-Age figure unless later table notes split them.

Known blessing mechanics include turning a natural 1 into a 20 proficiency times per day and a backswing-as-bonus-action style benefit. The exact item text should still be checked against Jefferson's live character sheet.

## Significance

Hestia matters for two reasons at once.

First, she confirms that at least some powers believed lost in the [Fourth Age](../concepts/fourth-age.md) were not truly gone, even if what survives is fragmentary rather than whole.

Second, she gives the [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md) one of its clearest moments of ancient goodness: a surviving fragment of a goddess of refuge and home appearing in the middle of war trauma, ruin, and cosmic danger.

Third, her survival now points toward a deeper Fourth-Age divine war in which [Lolth](lolth.md)'s priestesses captured and hid rival fragments near their own temple sites.

## Session 8 Temple Evidence

[Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md) strengthens Hestia's connection to [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md). The [Dead Lands Ghost Druid](dead-lands-ghost-druid.md) recognizes the power tied to [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](sergeant-jefferson-stone.md)'s amulet as belonging to one of the killed divinities.

[Bartholomew Hildebrant](bartholomew-hildebrant.md), a Fourth-Age survivor from an older assault on the temple, carries [Bartholomew's Hearth Medallion](../items/bartholomews-hearth-medallion.md), which matches Jefferson's amulet. [The Ssar'Velyn Temple Guardian](ssar-velyn-temple-guardian.md) notices the match and treats it as significant, implying that Hestia's fragments, symbols, or surviving divine line may have been present in the old assault as well as in Jefferson's Session 6 altar encounter.

## Session 9 Recovery

[Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md) makes Hestia's survival more active. [Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](mistress-sila-vexry.md) claims that Hestia was not simply destroyed because hearth, home, motherhood, welcome, and hospitality still act as unconscious worship. In Vex'ryn's telling, [Lolth](lolth.md) tied that surviving hearth power to her own ascension system rather than wasting it.

The claim is suspect because it comes from Vex'ryn, but the party sees confirming signs. When Hestia's name is spoken, Bartholomew's inner pain fades, and the presence near his medallion and Jefferson's pendant feels like a campfire in the void. Bartholomew later prays and receives warmth, comfort, and bodily strengthening.
