# Julia / Julisia

Julia, also rendered Julisia in the session transcript and occasionally written as Julesia or Jellesia in later notes, is one fractured aspect of a rival devil whose soul was shattered by [Lucian](lucian.md).

Session canon uses `Julia / Julisia` as the clearest name for the power fragment. The alternate spellings are retained as note variants, not separate characters.

## Origin

Long ago, [Lucian](lucian.md) used a divine artifact to shatter a rival devil into six fractured aspects. Each aspect carried a piece of the original soul. Julia was one of those aspects.

[Lucian](lucian.md) made the mistake of doing an enemy a small harm rather than ending her completely. For a thousand years, Julia survived at the edge of his empire, watching, infiltrating, and waiting.

## Revenge

When [Lucian](lucian.md)'s resurrection neared, Julia triggered a sleeper cell in the [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md): the [Black Vipers](../factions/black-vipers.md). The Vipers believed they were acting for [Lucian](lucian.md), but Julia used them as a lure and distraction to draw the attention of [Lucian](lucian.md)'s enemies.

## Shift in Motivation

After [Belle](belle.md)'s attack on [Wendell](wendell.md)'s stronghold, the [Moon Stone Collectors](../factions/moon-stone-collectors.md) freed [Gillesa](gillesa.md), a sister-fragment Julia believed destroyed. Confronted with part of her original soul, Julia diverted resources and lost control of the larger game, allowing new possibilities to emerge.

The Conclave later reveals that Julia was the hidden player who set much of [Lucian](lucian.md)'s resurrection game in motion. Her goal appears to be forcing all of [Lucian](lucian.md)'s soul fragments into one destroyable form so the being who shattered her can be erased permanently.

## Elisia

In the reconstructed [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 11](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-11.md), Julisia appears through or over [Elisia](elisia.md) after the Deceiver's contingencies strike the school. The party finds [Elisia](elisia.md) with a second face superimposed over hers, standing over [Phlukk](phlukk.md)'s protected stasis pod.

This suggests that one of the six sister-fragments has rejoined or overtaken another. [Julara](julara.md) later frames the situation as [Elisia](elisia.md) becoming closer to the original whole, though not necessarily by consent.

## Sovereignty

In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md), several sister-fragments merge into [Sovereignty](sovereignty.md): Conquer, Justice, Love, and Lust. The merged being argues that the actions of one severed fragment should not define the whole.

In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 17](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-17.md), the remaining knowledge and wisdom fragments merge with [Sovereignty](sovereignty.md) in the [Summer Court](../places/summer-court.md). A later fragment list associates the power aspect with the name Julisia, while the completed [Sovereignty](sovereignty.md) is treated as a new whole rather than a simple return to one fragment's identity.

## Canon Note

If a planning note uses `Julesia`, treat it as a spelling variant for `Julisia`, not a separate sister. The session transcripts do not support `Alura` or `Julessa` as confirmed sister names.
