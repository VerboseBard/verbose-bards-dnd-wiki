# Lucian Coggeshall

Lucian Coggeshall is Constance's eldest brother, firstborn heir of the Coggeshall line, and one of the primary abusers of her childhood.

He is not [Lucius](lucius.md), the devil/demon god figure and architect of the gods' downfall. The similar names refer to completely separate people.

## Cruelty

Already an adult by the time Constance was born, Lucian Coggeshall saw her disability and age gap as a stain on the family. He beat her, locked her in the shed for days when he could get away with it, threw her down wells, and made a game of public humiliation.

He also spread the lie that Constance was a cursed changeling who had stolen his real sibling's body. One of his favorite forms of petty cruelty was throwing rotten fruit at her.

When Constance withdrew into tinkering, Lucian Coggeshall's abuse lessened mainly because he had less interest in her, not because he had changed. After taking on more family responsibility, he had less time to torment her directly, though he still viewed her as a shame.

In [War of the Gods, Session 22](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-22.md), Lucian Coggeshall proves that adulthood has not softened him. During the wartime confrontation in the family hall, he recites the hostile prophecy over Constance as evidence that she was always meant to betray and ruin her kin. Even after she brings reinforcements that help save the keep, he clings to the prophecy as proof that she is still the source of the disaster.
