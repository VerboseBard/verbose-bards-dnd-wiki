# Maeve, Queen of the Fae

Maeve was the Queen of the Fae, later the goddess of magic, the most powerful member of the old pantheon, and [Belle](belle.md)'s mother.

She shares her name with [Maeve, the Schoolmistress](mave.md), the half-elven paladin and school-running figure who later rose into [Bridget](bridget.md)'s place. They are separate people.

## Role

Maeve belongs to the older divine order preserved in the [Council of Gods of the Fifth Age](../concepts/council-of-gods-of-the-fifth-age.md), but her story begins in the [Feywild](../places/feywild.md).

Before becoming a goddess, Maeve achieved the extraordinary feat of ruling both the Summer Court and the Winter Court. Her court was powerful, beautiful, and brutal. Succession politics among her children, daughters, sisters, and rivals were built on assassination, manipulation, and lethal competition rather than clean inheritance.

## Family

Belle was one of Maeve's many children.

The violence of Maeve's succession politics eventually drove Belle out of the Fae realm and into mortal reality during the [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md). In the mortal world, the lower magic of reality drained Belle's power and intelligence. She was not merely weakened; she was starving, becoming less fully herself the longer she remained cut off from the Fae realm's abundance.

Belle was also hunted by dragonfly assassins from the Fae world. Her companions initially treated her warnings about them as ridiculous or exaggerated, but later events proved they were real killers who struck when the party was not present to protect her.

## Ascension

After [Dione](dione.md)'s ascension, Maeve worked with her and then chose to ascend herself, taking on the mantle of goddess of magic.

That ascent changed her title but not the need for precision. Depending on the era, she may be called Maeve, Queen of the Fae, or Maeve, Goddess of Magic.

## Divine-Law Commentary

Old divine-law commentary attributed to Maeve preserves an important distinction: killing someone, stopping someone from dying, and allowing someone to die are not treated as the same act.

That distinction matters most clearly in [Belle](belle.md)'s choice around [the Potato King](potato-king.md). Belle could have protected him during [Lucius](lucius.md)'s attack, but chose inaction and claimed his soul afterward. The act was morally damning to her enemies, but not identical to murder under the rules the gods were using.

## Place in the Pantheon

In the surviving council notes, Maeve is described as:

- goddess of magic
- formerly fae
- most powerful of the pantheon
