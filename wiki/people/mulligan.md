# Mulligan

Mulligan is the reborn identity of [The Commodore](../concepts/commodore-prophecy.md), the legendary Pirate King also remembered through the name [Molokan](molokan.md).

Most of the world remembers Mulligan as the betrayer of the [Hell's Bane Heroes](../factions/hells-bane-heroes.md). Very few people know that he was the Commodore reborn, or that the same legend that once saved [Opaal](../places/opaal.md) also fed his later return.

## Names and Identities

- Mulligan
- The Commodore
- The Pirate King
- Molokan
- The Commodore Reborn
- The Betrayer

Molokan is the correct spelling for this identity.

## Ancient Commodore Life

In his ancient life, the Commodore was a pirate king and naval warlord whose hatred for the merfolk began in childhood. Merfolk killed his parents, some of his siblings, and attempted to sacrifice him to their sea goddess. He survived and swore vengeance.

That vengeance made him a terror of the seas, especially to merfolk and sea elves. He was not a clean hero. He was a wounded and brutal man shaped by trauma, hatred, and the certainty that some enemies simply needed killing.

When the [Opaal Old One](../concepts/opaal-old-one.md) rose from the deep, the Commodore first considered letting it destroy the merfolk. Once he understood that the old one could destroy the entire world, he changed course and helped unite humans, merfolk, sea elves, and other peoples against it.

His relationship with [Aurora, Queen Beneath the Waves](aurora-queen-beneath-waves.md), began in violence and politics. The [Black Vipers](../factions/black-vipers.md) and vampire-linked enemies used her as leverage against him, and the meddling around that crisis helped create a blood binding between them. Over time, enemy, captive, ally, and queen became something deeper: lover, wife-figure, soul-bonded partner, and the mother of [Mira of Opaal](mira-of-opaal.md).

## Blood Binding with Aurora

Mulligan and [Aurora](aurora-queen-beneath-waves.md) share a life-force and soul bond. As long as one of them remains alive, the other can return after death. Their injuries and healing can echo across the bond: wounds suffered by one may manifest on the other, and healing one can help heal the other.

This blood binding is one reason the Commodore legend is more than reputation. His return is tied to prophecy, belief, Aurora's bloodline power, and the unfinished wound left by the old one.

## The Old One and the God-Killing Knife

In the final battle of his ancient life, the Commodore fought the deep old one now tracked in the wiki as the [Opaal Old One](../concepts/opaal-old-one.md). The plan to destroy it failed. Forced to improvise, the Commodore used a god-killing knife.

The strike did not happen cleanly. Bound by the creature and unable to reach it otherwise, he had to stab through himself to breach it. The knife cut him as well as the old one, mixing his blood, power, and legend with the monster's wound.

The old one was wounded and driven away, but not fully destroyed. That failure matters: when the old one stirs again, the prophecy and legend around the Commodore can generate the force needed to return him.

## Session 1 Return on Opaal

In [War of the Gods, Session 1](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-1.md), Mulligan returns too early.

He wakes on [Opaal](../places/opaal.md), washed ashore near a cliff edge as a corpse. Children help haul him out with ropes. A wind-like spirit or air elemental hovers near him, and Mulligan awakens with no memory of who he was or what happened before.

[Seeker Lindsey](seeker-lindsey.md) finds him and brings him to [Lady Penny Hathaway's School](../places/lady-penny-hathaways-school.md). Early notes identify this arrival through the Molokan name; the beach corpse is Mulligan, the Commodore reborn without memory.

## Straka Misunderstanding

Mulligan comes to believe that the wind-like spirit, later named [Straka](straka.md), brought him back from death.

That belief is wrong.

Straka began as an air elemental that had watched Mulligan in his previous life and recognized him when he returned. It did not resurrect him. Mulligan had been forced back prematurely by meddling around the pantheons and his enemies.

Because Mulligan believed Straka saved him, he began working to raise the air elemental into a minor god. He eventually bargained with [Bridget](bridget.md) to have Straka accepted into Bridget and [Belle](belle.md)'s pantheon structure. After [Bridget](bridget.md) was killed and [Belle](belle.md) became the effective head of that pantheon structure, Straka's later divine identity became part of the tangled aftermath.

## Maeve's Ascension

Before betrayal fully defined his legacy, Mulligan played a critical role in the ascension of [Maeve, the Schoolmistress](mave.md).

After [Bridget](bridget.md) was killed by [Lucius](lucius.md), Maeve was badly damaged. [Belle](belle.md) and Mulligan tried to keep her from dying, but Belle was pulled into direct battle with Lucius at the school. Mulligan carried Maeve to the school's temple shrine and used a dungeon core, soul coins, and the released divine magic from Bridget's death to stabilize her and help her ascend into Bridget's place as goddess of knowledge, art, beauty, and learning.

Maeve remained one of the few divine figures Mulligan genuinely trusted.

## Wendell, Lucius, and the Gate

Mulligan was manipulated and mind-controlled by [Wendell](wendell.md), but he was not only a puppet. He also agreed with part of Wendell and [Lucius](lucius.md)'s goal: the [Divine Gate](../concepts/divine-gate.md) would cut gods and other overwhelming powers off from direct rule over mortal reality.

Mulligan began playing factions against one another. His enemies from his Commodore life included [Alen](alen.md), the vampire queen who later claimed the [Raven Queen of Saharun](raven-queen-of-saharun.md) mantle; [Lucius](lucius.md); and [Sedna](sedna.md), the goddess of sea and sky tied to the sea-folk divine structure.

One of Mulligan's subtler victories was helping arrange circumstances so [Lucius](lucius.md) destroyed [Alen](alen.md)'s manifested Raven Queen form, fulfilling an old promise from his prior life.

## Fall at the Citadel

In [War of the Gods, Session 23](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-23.md), Mulligan's unstable situation finally detonates.

[Maeve](mave.md) confronts him, reveals more of his old sailor-king memories, and makes clear that the school cannot keep him on its grounds after his bargain with [Wendell](wendell.md). When the party follows him out, [Junvara](junvara.md) correctly identifies Wendell's crown as the immediate control mechanism distorting his choices.

The crown is removed, but too late to stop the contingency built into it. Infernal portals open around the group, Wendell's dragon appears, and Mulligan is driven into direct combat against his own companions. He is eventually killed in the fighting by his own teammates.

Publicly, this turns Mulligan into the Judas or Benedict Arnold of the war. Most people never learn that the betrayer was also the Commodore reborn.

## Maeve's Memory Gift

Before Mulligan's final War of the Gods death, [Maeve](mave.md) restores his memories. She tells him that as goddess of wisdom she can now return what was taken from him, and that he will keep those memories on his next return.

This is why his later return during [Moon Stone Collectors](../campaign/moonstone-collectors.md) and [The Hunt for Black Vipers](../campaign/hunt-for-black-vipers.md) is different. He is no longer an amnesiac tool available for pantheons, vampires, devils, or gods to shape.

## Later Return as the Commodore

When the Commodore appears again only a few years later, it means the original timing of the prophecy has finally arrived. He was supposed to rise when the seals began to fail and the old one stirred again.

This later incarnation is effectively his third life: ancient Commodore, amnesiac Mulligan, and now the returned Commodore with memory intact. He is not interested in being manipulated by anyone.

After the final phase of the [Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md), the [Divine Gate](../concepts/divine-gate.md) pushes [Belle](belle.md)'s daughters onto the mortal side of reality. [Lilith](lilith.md) ends up near [Gregario / Shaq](gregario-shaq.md), who had sworn himself to her at birth through the Raven Queen line. [Belle](belle.md) sends [Eve](eve.md), the daughter born through [Experiment 69](experiment-69.md), to the Commodore so he can protect and mentor her while the gods are cut off.
