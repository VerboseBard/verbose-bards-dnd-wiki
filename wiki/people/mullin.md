# Mullin

Mullin is a party member from the [War of the Gods / New Beginning Campaign](../campaign/war-of-the-gods.md) whose path shifts sharply from divine dependence toward self-forged survival.

## Entrance Into the Group

By [War of the Gods, Session 22](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-22.md), Mullin is already functioning as a volatile but important part of the party's later roster. The same session that ties him to [Wendell](wendell.md)'s oath also shows him participating in the dragon-hatchery crisis, the ley-line-nexus confrontation, and the increasingly open arguments over gods, power, and control.

## Ship Disaster Survival

In the extracted [War of the Gods, Session 20](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-20.md), Mullin is left aboard the damaged rescue ship when the front section blows apart after the dungeon-core crisis.

The Seeker and [Charlie](charlie.md) use him as the living conduit for an unstable portal-energy fix that keeps the ship from catastrophic destruction. Rather than feed that power into [Straka](straka.md) or any other god, Mullin tries to hold it himself and nearly dies in the process.

He survives, but only barely. The experience leaves him physically diminished, spiritually severed from the path he had been following, and much more committed to relying on himself than on higher powers.

## Break With the Gods

That same session makes his new position explicit. After recovering, Mullin throws [Straka](straka.md)'s symbol from the ship and rejects the divine leverage that had been wrapped around his second life.

[Charlie](charlie.md) immediately recognizes the shape of that choice and encourages it in her own rough way: if help from above never arrived honestly, then self-made strength may be more trustworthy than prayer.

## Wendell's Oath

In [War of the Gods, Session 22](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-22.md), Mullin's rejection of divine dependence evolves into something much stranger and riskier.

After forcing [Wendell](wendell.md) to obey the humiliating command to touch his own nose, Mullin secretly rides away on [Wendell](wendell.md)'s dragon and negotiates with him in private. [Wendell](wendell.md) gives him a crown that blocks mind-reading by gods and similar powers, then binds him with a ley-line oath: [Wendell](wendell.md) will help Mullin kill [Belle](belle.md), and Mullin will help [Wendell](wendell.md) against the [Divine Gate](../concepts/divine-gate.md).

That oath cannot be freely explained afterward, which leaves Mullin carrying critical knowledge he cannot fully share with the rest of the party.

## Commodore Legend

The same session also adds a major twist to Mullin's return from death. [Mave](mave.md) privately tells him that [Straka](straka.md) did not truly bring him back at all.

Instead, the belief and legend in the Commodore's return appear to be feeding him power directly. That would make Mullin less a restored servant of a god than a figure being sustained by myth itself, with the frightening implication that he may be rising toward something adjacent to godhood without meaning to.

## Fall at the Citadel

In [War of the Gods, Session 23](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-23.md), Mullin's unstable situation finally detonates.

[Mave](mave.md) confronts him, reveals more of his old sailor-king memories, and makes clear that the school cannot keep him on its grounds after his bargain with [Wendell](wendell.md). When the party follows him out, [Junara](junara.md) correctly identifies [Wendell](wendell.md)'s crown as the immediate control mechanism distorting his choices.

The crown is removed, but too late to stop the contingency built into it. Infernal portals open around the group, [Wendell](wendell.md)'s dragon appears, and Mullin is driven into direct combat against his own companions. He is eventually killed in the fighting, making Session 23 the first clear collapse of the "I can still walk this line safely" idea that had been haunting his arc since Session 20.

## Commodore Aftermath

At the start of [War of the Gods, Session 24](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-24.md), the party confirms that Mullin's death has not ended the deeper force sustaining him. An attempt to consume or redirect his soul fails, and the recap explicitly treats the Commodore prophecy as still active. In other words, Mullin may be dead for now, but the legend-fed reincarnation logic tied to his return has not been broken.
