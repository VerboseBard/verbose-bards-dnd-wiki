# Quill

Quill was an adventurer in [Logan's First Campaign](../campaign/logans-first-campaign.md), an earlier [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) campaign thread that began roughly three weeks before [Logan's Second Campaign](../campaign/logans-second-campaign.md).

## Party Context

The surviving notes place Quill alongside:

- Divok, a half-orc cleric
- Thane, an undead rogue
- Tamiel, an aasimar paladin

Quill appears to have been one of the party's arcane-capable members. He uses levitation, summons a sea hawk familiar, purchases spell-copying materials, and interacts comfortably with magical items, crafted upgrades, and libraries.

## Knife and Hourglass Incident

Quill's notes begin in the aftermath of a killing inside a cave, with Quill holding a box containing a knife and an hourglass.

An intense supernatural standoff follows:

- a celestial being with a prior relationship to Tamiel demands the artifacts
- a devil woman appears with her own offer
- Thane, already harmed by the celestial's presence, becomes desperate to bargain
- Quill quietly levitates the bag containing the artifacts toward the ceiling while the stronger personalities argue

The confrontation ends with the devil's death and the celestial destroying the artifacts.

That opening immediately places Quill in a campaign where divine and infernal powers are already competing over world-shaping objects, and where survival depends as much on staying calm in a metaphysical crisis as on fighting well.

## Wilbrix and Connor Lynch

After the cave incident, Quill's trail leads back toward Wilbrix and the mage Connor Lynch, who had previously contracted Quill's master.

The notes later confirm that Connor is hidden on the docks at Wilbrix behind protections that block magical and divine sight, which suggests he was not just a local enemy but someone operating with unusually serious concealment.

## Shipwreck and the Black Tower Island

One of the campaign's major middle arcs begins aboard a ship headed west toward Wilbrix while a pursuing fleet closes in.

That sequence escalates fast:

- the allied ship Scarlet buys the party time with a mage-powered chain-lightning cannon
- Divok's weather control and accidental merfolk contact complicate the escape
- a storm batters the ship
- the vessel and surrounding water are abruptly teleported to an island
- Quill and the others are left stranded with dead sailors, rubbery hand-mouthed creatures, and a black tower rising from a flat-topped cliff

This arc pushes Quill into one of the stranger corners of the [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) notes: broken geography, displacement magic, undead chambers, and a tower-island that behaves more like a sealed supernatural experiment than a normal ruin.

## Tower, Spectre, and Undead Halls

Inside the cave and tower complex, Quill's party encounters:

- an avatar or spectre tied to undeath and death-power
- rooms full of undead sorcerers
- dining chambers, storerooms, cells, gardens, and secret rooms
- enchanted objects, including a summoning glove and a magic rifle
- a void-linked passage opened by hidden controls
- signs that whatever was trapped there was weakening and looking for a way out

Quill's role in these notes remains steady and practical. He records the structure, the loot, the magical logic, and the escalating risk without losing track of the party's immediate needs.

## Dione and the Manor

By January 9, 2021, the party reaches the manor of [Dione](dione.md), halfling seer and oracle of Wilbrix.

This is important for Quill specifically. [Dione](dione.md) is one of the named powers preserved in the [Council of Gods of the Fifth Age](../concepts/council-of-gods-of-the-fifth-age.md), associated there with foresight, prophecy, and knowledge. At her manor:

- Quill is led through magically enlarged halls and antechambers
- he meets [Jinx](jinx.md) in her artificer role
- he acquires a massive chest on loan
- he buys materials to learn and copy spells

By this point, Quill's arc looks less like a random dungeon crawl and more like the path of a magically literate survivor being pulled into older prophetic and crafted-power networks.

## The Broker and the Game Room

Later notes place Quill in a protected game room full of anti-cheating magic, mixed currencies, and a broker named Mr. Sheppard.

That detail matters because it ties Quill's campaign not only to towers, undead, and prophecy, but also to a more worldly understructure of contracts, artificers, wealthy intermediaries, and magical commerce. His story sits at the meeting point of adventure, occult politics, and negotiated power.

## Later Fifth-Age Echo

In later [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) notes tied to [Vail](vail.md)'s campaign, Quill is mentioned again alongside [Jinx](jinx.md) as one of the people trying to build something that can contain or regulate dangerous magic using Ebodreth's research.

That does not yet tell us the full shape of Quill's later life, but it strongly suggests he survives his original campaign and remains active deep into the pre-[Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md) crisis.

## Fifth Age Position

Quill's campaign takes place three weeks before [Logan's Second Campaign](../campaign/logans-second-campaign.md), making it the earlier Fifth-Age party thread now cataloged as [Logan's First Campaign](../campaign/logans-first-campaign.md).

## Links to Sixth-Age Figures

Quill's strongest clean cross-age link is [Jinx](jinx.md).

In Quill's own notes, [Jinx](jinx.md) appears at [Dione](dione.md)'s manor in an artificer role, working around crafted upgrades, rare materials, and Lord of Blades components. In later [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) material, [Jinx](jinx.md) reappears as a much more openly extra-setting fixer and systems-level helper around the [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md).

The connection is strong enough that Quill currently serves as one of the clearest Fifth-Age witness points for a person who later becomes important again in Sixth-Age and Seventh-Age-adjacent records.
