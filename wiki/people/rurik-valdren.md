# Rurik Valdren

![Rurik Valdren](../assets/images/rurik-valdren.png)

Rurik Valdren, spelling provisional, is an extremely old dwarf associated with [Calvin's Curios](../places/calvins-curios.md).

## Background

Rurik helped develop the [Fog-Containment Artifacts](../items/fog-containment-artifacts.md) that pushed back [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md)'s fog. His work made him rich and indirectly enabled discovery of the [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) gate, though he says he warned others not to touch it.

## Goal

Rurik seeks [Sanctuary City](../concepts/sanctuary-city.md) or a key/path to it. He believes the answer lies somewhere in [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md).

## Deal with the Party

He wants dwarven artifacts and will pay better than the military. He offers protection and fine coverage for good-faith dwarven finds.

The party retains a communication stone or similar contact method tied to Rurik or one of his emissaries, originally meant for reporting dwarven artifact leads and arranging meetings.

## Power

Rurik is tied to the dwarven banking system and is not an easy man to attack politically.

He is calm, exacting, and difficult to impress. He treats value like a sacred language: not theatrical, not loud, but intensely aware of what things are worth and who deserves access to them.

In Session 4, [Colonel Core](colonel-core.md) treats Rurik as a dangerous political target because of that banking power. The same transcript keeps his interests narrower than the [Silk Parlor](../places/silk-parlor.md)'s broader corruption network: Rurik wants artifacts, leverage, and the path to [Sanctuary City](../concepts/sanctuary-city.md).

In Session 7, [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](sergeant-jefferson-stone.md) considers contacting Rurik as a possible way to create a legal-commercial shield around [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md) before the military, churches, or state powers seize the valley's ether wealth. This is only a plan at that point; the transcript says temple-related events must happen before Jefferson can send that message.

In [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), Jefferson follows through and contacts Rurik after leaving [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md). Rurik becomes actively involved in the Sunhollow crisis once he hears about the valley's ether wealth, the approaching [Inquisition](../factions/inquisition.md), the old blood oath, [Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](mistress-sila-vexry.md)'s temple activity, and the presence of [Bartholomew Hildebrant](bartholomew-hildebrant.md), an ancient dwarf who can still speak Dwarven.

Rurik warns that Vex'ryn's promise to `release` the people from the blood oath is dangerously ambiguous. In blood magic, release may mean freedom, but it may also mean death, severance, or fulfillment of the oath in a lethal form.
