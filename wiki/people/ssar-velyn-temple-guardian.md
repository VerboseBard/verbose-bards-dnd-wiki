# Ssar'Velyn Temple Guardian

The Ssar'Velyn Temple Guardian is the massive drider or spider-bodied guardian who runs the final trial inside [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md) in [Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md).

## Identity

The guardian's exact nature is unresolved. The transcript treats her as a large monstrosity rather than a fiend or undead, but it is not yet clear whether she is a drider, a specific surviving priestess, an avatar, or part of the temple's trial system.

She speaks with the same voice that has been running the trials, but the relationship between her, the temple voice, and the [Ssar'Velyn Ghost Priestess](ssar-velyn-ghost-priestess.md) is still open.

## Trial Role

The guardian descends from the ceiling for the final optional trial. She uses shield magic, ceiling movement, webbing, crushing drops, mace attacks, and shifting initiative to test the party.

The fight ends when the party proves itself rather than when she is destroyed. She declares that they have passed, returns [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](sergeant-jefferson-stone.md) wrapped in a cocoon, and heals the party through blood magic that [Alistair Hooley](alistair-hooley.md) recognizes from the old temple book.

## Ultimatum

After the trial, the guardian explains the moral problem beneath the blight:

- [Gwen](gwen.md), the emissary, still has work to do and remains behind with [Bevar Lurton](bevar-lurton.md).
- The valley's inhabitants are bound by ancestral blood oath to defend the temple.
- Many of those bound people may not know the full truth their elders inherited.
- Outsiders following the party will likely kill or exploit the valley for its [Ether Crystals](../items/ether-crystals.md).
- Destroying the temple could also doom thousands of bound innocents.
- Rabbitfolk are not all bound in the same way because their population outgrew the original oath structure.

She also notices the matching significance of [Bartholomew Hildebrant](bartholomew-hildebrant.md)'s [Hearth Medallion](../items/bartholomews-hearth-medallion.md) and Jefferson's amulet.
