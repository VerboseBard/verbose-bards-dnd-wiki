# Vail

Vail was a member of the Solovian rangers in [Logan's Third Campaign](../campaign/logans-third-campaign.md), a late [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) arc that begins roughly twenty-four weeks before [The Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md) and, by user chronology, ends with it.

## Role

The surviving notes present Vail as a disciplined investigator and field operative working inside ranger structures while the world slides toward collapse. He tracks evidence, survives magical and political betrayals, and keeps functioning as the campaign moves from ordinary criminal work into prophecy, undead war zones, and world-ending machinery.

He is initially tied to the Solovian rangers and later to Ranger Team 6.

## Blackthorn and Vernon Anatydes

Vail's notes begin with a pursuit of Blackthorn bandits and their tiefling leader Ashdan.

That investigation quickly reveals a larger structure:

- the bandits are running drugs
- Ashdan reports upward through a sending stone
- the real superior is V.A., later identified as Vernon Anatydes
- the Blackthorn hideout may have been bait, with the real pressure focused on a convoy

By April 9, 2022, Vail's group has defeated Vernon Anatydes and his ally, but the victory opens rather than closes the campaign. The drug trail leads onward into shipping, capitals, and hidden magical systems.

## Rochdale, Valarondir, and Riverbreak

The next stage of the campaign moves through Rochdale, Valarondir, and Riverbreak.

Here the investigation widens into civic and institutional corruption:

- McGinty shipping is moving illegal cargo
- different branches appear partially compromised without every worker understanding the scheme
- ranger leaders and city officials are pulled into the case
- Wilbur, the escaped gnome, and a dragon-associated half-elf operative push the conspiracy toward magical transit and hidden bosses

Vail's notes show a campaign that is no longer just about catching smugglers. It is about understanding how deeply the network has infiltrated trade, transport, and governance.

## Drowned Witch and the Butcher

At the Drowned Witch and the ranger offices, Vail's arc gets darker.

The campaign brings in:

- Captain Sophia Nikol..., whose identity is later revealed to be false
- Team 13 veterans at the inn
- blood magic used to track the butcher
- hidden holding cells and prison-interrogation scenes
- Alice the butcher, Dorothy, and a warlock-pact structure tied to a dragon-like master

The notes also tie the butcher to [Lilith](lilith.md), described in multiple forms and feared as well as revered. Vail is therefore operating in a story where criminal investigation and divine-family horror have already merged.

## Ancient Language and the Observatories

One of Vail's most important arcs involves ancient language, telescopes, and observatory sites.

He encounters:

- inscriptions that match pre-elvish symbols
- telescopes with linked or variant rune patterns
- observatories positioned across regions and across the sea
- a large ornate key
- a building tied to an artificial sun and world-ending magic
- dragon-head machinery, shields, levers, and colored crystal systems

This material suggests that by the time of Vail's campaign, the [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) is already full of dormant or half-understood infrastructure capable of catastrophic magical use if activated incorrectly.

## Leprechaun Bargain

While jailed, Vail is visited by a leprechaun-like man offering a fool's bargain.

The bargain gives Vail the power to read and understand all language. The same figure also presents a spyglass and implies that soon the inscriptions, the spyglasses, or even the knowledge itself may cease to matter.

That scene matters because it places Vail inside the same kind of deal-driven, fey-adjacent logic that shows up elsewhere in Fifth- and Sixth-Age notes. Vail is not just solving clues. He is being changed so he can keep up with the scale of the problem.

## The Lead-Up to the Breaking

By the later notes, the campaign has advanced eight months and the world is in visible collapse.

The party moves through:

- war-torn Clipper Bay
- Gostoke's devastation
- valleys full of undead armies fighting each other
- violently unstable weather
- magical anomalies that feel tied to beacon activation
- mercenary hunters trying to abduct [Belle](belle.md)
- ritual pressure around Astrawyck and the surrounding road network

The dead begin appearing as warnings on gallows, entire regions bloom or warp unnaturally, and the party is forced to think in terms of what should happen to a massive reservoir of dangerous magic rather than simply who to stab next.

## Toby, Rex, Belle, and the Fairground Power

Late in the notes, Vail's campaign centers on a magical power source around a fairground or sanctuary-city structure.

Several competing options emerge for what to do with that power:

- let Rex use it to manifest
- let [Belle](belle.md) use it to ascend
- channel it to an existing god
- give it to the Green Man

At the same time, Toby is seen carving symbols into walls, animated dolls are protecting children, and the region's authority structure is warping around beings calling themselves the Voice of Rex and the Hand of Justice.

This is where Vail's campaign most clearly touches the edge of [The Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md): not only armies and monsters, but reality-scale power choices with no clean answer.

## Links to Sixth-Age Figures

Vail's notes contain some of the strongest direct bridges between late [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) material and later [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) character pages.

The clearest is [Belle](belle.md). In Vail's notes, [Belle](belle.md) is already important enough to be hunted, destabilized, and considered as a candidate for ascension through the fairground power. That aligns cleanly with the far more explicit divine and political role [Belle](belle.md) plays later in the [War of the Gods / New Beginning Campaign](../campaign/war-of-the-gods.md).

The next strongest is [Jinx](jinx.md). [Quill](quill.md) and [Jinx](jinx.md) are said to be building a magical containment device from Ebodreth's research, and [Jinx](jinx.md) is already directly attested in [Quill](quill.md)'s earlier notes.

The name [Lilith](lilith.md) is more complicated. Vail's notes describe a feared and revered multi-formed [Lilith](lilith.md) with a draconic "true" form, which does not fit simply or cleanly with the later Sixth-Age child [Lilith](lilith.md) page as it currently stands. That overlap is important, but the safest current reading is that this may reflect a title, predecessor, mantle issue, mythic confusion, or another layered continuity problem rather than a simple one-to-one identity.

## Quill and Jinx Connection

One especially useful cross-link in the notes is that [Quill](quill.md) and [Jinx](jinx.md) are said to be building something meant to contain or regulate the dangerous magic, following Ebodreth's research.

That strongly links [Quill](quill.md)'s earlier Fifth-Age campaign thread and Vail's later one through [Jinx](jinx.md)'s ongoing artificer and containment work.
