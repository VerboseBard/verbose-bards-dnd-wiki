# Veil, Guardian of the Citadel

Veil was the long-standing guardian of the [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md), watching over its systems and people from within the crystal.

## Stewardship

Veil spent roughly a thousand years guiding, protecting, and subtly influencing the [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md). His role was less direct ruler than hidden steward, maintaining systems and nudging events from behind the scenes.

In the extracted [War of the Gods, Session 20](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-20.md), that hidden-steward role is shown much more directly in a late-[Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md) context. Veil receives [Junvara](junvara.md) and [Greg](greg-p-smith.md) inside the crystal's control center, reviews their movements, grants them future access, and openly frames himself as a non-divine but highly informed actor trying to keep the [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md) functional while stronger powers play dangerous games around it.

In [War of the Gods, Session 23](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-23.md), Veil is still operating in exactly that crisis-manager role. As the first infernal portals and mind-flayer pressures hit the [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md), he works the crystal's command systems directly, routes emergency responses, and deploys an off-world SB Solutions specialist toward [Greg](greg-p-smith.md)'s position as fast as the systems allow.

In [War of the Gods, Session 24](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-24.md), that stewardship is pushed to its hardest visible test. Veil refuses to leave the [Radiant Citadel Crystal](../places/radiant-citadel-crystal.md) even while [Lucius](lucius.md) attacks it directly, because abandoning the controls would mean losing the [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md) entirely. He sends the party onto the outside of the crystal to stop [Lucius](lucius.md), then receives them back after the kill and helps frame the next problem correctly: the seeded [dungeons](../concepts/dungeons.md) are not to be casually destroyed, because they now form part of the infrastructure needed to pull the worlds back together.

## Ascension

In the reconstructed events of [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 11](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-11.md), Veil reaches the end of that stewardship. Given the choice to remain, transfer into a new host, or leave, he chooses to ascend.

He turns away from the control panels, goes to a garden he loved, and releases the power within him. His light grows beyond the crystal, marking his departure from mortal-scale guardianship.
