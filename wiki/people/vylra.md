# Vylra

Vylra is a male firbolg, a protector tied to [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md), and one of the central members of the [Moon Stone Collectors](../campaign/moonstone-collectors.md).

## Background

Roster and player-note material describe Vylra as a very large firbolg, more than seven feet tall, gray-furred with black and green streaking, and well over a century old. He left [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md) about nine and a half years before the recorded campaign and spent that time traveling widely enough to become more comfortable with cities, ship travel, and improvisational adventuring than many of his relatives.

He is visually and thematically tied to spores, fieldcraft, and domestic labor. He carries cookware on the road, travels with a friendly hive of wasps sometimes referred to as Quint, and repeatedly tries to feed or stabilize people even when everything around him is collapsing.

## Role

At the canon Session 1 final roster, Vylra is a Druid 10 / Ranger 5.

He acts as nature controller, marksman, strategist, and one of the clearest consciences in the party. The notes repeatedly present him as someone who thinks tactically, feels protective responsibility strongly, and tries to keep the group from crossing moral lines just because the world is collapsing.

## Personality and Relationships

Vylra is the party member most consistently associated with care. He cooks for the group, worries about travel readiness, calms panicked animals, and is often the one who turns a tactical problem back into a moral one.

Several personal links reinforce that role:

- [Juan](juan.md) explicitly admires Vylra's calmer attitude and later trusts him enough to speak openly about fear, shame, and patron-driven manipulation.
- [Sorrel](sorrel.md) is protective of him for reasons that early roster notes treat as a party secret.
- [Elisia](elisia.md) is one of the clearest examples of his empathy in practice. He comforts her after her release from Hell, feeds her, and refuses to treat her suffering as proof that she has no future.
- His ties to [Denny](denny.md), [Volko Blackwood](volko-blackwood.md), and the larger Blackwood line make him one of the campaign's strongest bridges between the adventuring party and [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md)'s internal politics.

## Moon Stone Collectors

Vylra is present from the opening fake-emissary escort and becomes one of the campaign's strongest through-lines.

He helps manage the [Norvindr](../places/norvindr.md) ambush, helps save [Soda](soda.md) from infernal necrosis, tracks the truth around [Ebonwing Manor](../places/ebonwing-manor.md), comforts [Elisia](elisia.md) after her release from Hell, leads the party north toward [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md), and serves as one of the main interpreters of the grove's fungal, ancestral, and [Viscus](viscus.md)-linked systems.

In the [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md) phase, he is often the practical stabilizer:

- he accompanies [Juan](juan.md) to the Blackthorn trap and sees through the room's false faces
- he helps finance critical preparation, including fire-resistance support before the Hell assault
- he keeps watch on [Paul](paul.md)'s cursed-book instability and later tries to counter [Belle](../people/belle.md)'s mark on him

Once the party returns north, Vylra becomes even more central. He guides the group toward home, reunites with family, communes directly with the grove's symbiote systems, argues the case before the local council, and helps oversee [Phlukk](phlukk.md)'s restoration ritual.

His ties to [Denny](denny.md), the Blackwood family, and the grove make him one of the clearest bridges between the campaign's personal stakes and its world-scale consequences.

Late in the campaign, he gives one of the party's clearest rallying speeches before the hydra fight, brings fire and healing against both hydras, and then becomes one of the key people responsible for carrying, protecting, and installing the living [Viscus](viscus.md) cutting that lets the grove take control of the master goo network. In the final control-room struggle, he inserts the cutting, protects it under pressure, resists active contamination, and helps turn a looming goo apocalypse into something the grove can actually contain.
