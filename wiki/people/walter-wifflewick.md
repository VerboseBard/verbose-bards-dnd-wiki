# Walter Wifflewick

Walter Wifflewick is an elder Harengon of [Burrowroot](../factions/burrowroot.md), the rabbit lore master of [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md), and one of the most important surviving keepers of rabbit genealogy, old law, and Moonhare memory.

## Role

Walter is not the formal Burrowroot councilor. That seat belongs to [Tavero Bellan](tavero-bellan.md).

His authority comes from different ground:

- rabbit genealogy
- old Burrowroot records
- Moonhare and Hooley family memory
- Riddle, Puckle, and Sandwich family-name memory
- emissary law
- sacred-land access customs
- knowledge of the lost rabbit seat and how it was politically buried

When [the Sunhollow Council](../factions/sunhollow-council.md) debates the blight crisis in [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), Walter is the one who unrolls the relevant old law and argues that a party arriving with an emissary may legally approach the forbidden lands.

## Temperament

Walter is practical, suspicious, theatrical when it serves the law, and far too old to be impressed by formal authority alone. He is not a blind loyalist to the present council structure and appears to believe that the valley's official history has been cleaned up to hide older truths.

## Alistair and the Rabbit Question

Walter quickly understands that [Alistair Hooley](alistair-hooley.md)'s return is dangerous to the present political balance.

He knows that:

- the old rabbit heir likely vanished with the lost expedition
- key responsibilities were never passed down after that disappearance
- the rabbit seat was allowed to die for political reasons as much as ritual ones
- rabbit families may still hold legal leverage because they were never fully bound by the same blood-oath system as the other clans

He also warns that the valley's original masters were likely drow, not simply unnamed elves, and that speaking too freely with ancient ghosts may expose dangerous truths before the valley is ready to face them.

Walter also suggests that Alistair's mother's line may matter, not only his father's line. This keeps Alistair's exact relationship to the lost rabbit heir unresolved and politically dangerous.

Walter is likely to listen closely for Hooley, Riddle, Puckle, and Sandwich ancestry in Alistair's account.
