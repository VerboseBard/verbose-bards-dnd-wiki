# Zen

Zeniyrwynn Dothrynellion, usually called Zen, is a female elf, a diviner, arcane analyst, and one of the core members of the [Moon Stone Collectors](../campaign/moonstone-collectors.md) during the late [Sixth Age](../concepts/sixth-age.md).

Her surviving notes place her at the center of some of the campaign's most important investigative and ethical decisions: identifying infernal artifacts, reading portal traces, navigating church politics, protecting [Elisia](elisia.md), and helping the party survive the northern post-[Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md) wilderness.

## Role

Roster snapshot: Level 15 elf, Cleric / Wizard, School of Divination / Order Domain. Player: OncaObsidiana.

Zen serves as arcane tactician and divine scholar.

Zen repeatedly comes across in the notes as the party's clearest interpreter of magical systems and hidden structure.

Where other party members solve problems through force, luck, faith, or instinct, Zen tends to solve them through analysis:

- disguising herself as Laybrother Dominic during the opening decoy escort
- identifying cursed infernal objects and portal residue
- researching enemy organizations and hierarchy
- using divination and thought-reading to test motives and gaps
- keeping moral pressure on the group when demonic prisoners or compromised allies would be easier to treat as disposable

That combination makes her one of the main stabilizing minds in the campaign even when she is not the loudest person in the room.

## Norvindr Escort

Zen first enters the arc during the assassination-and-escort mission on [Norvindr](../places/norvindr.md), where the party works to protect Laybrother Dominic in the final weeks before the [Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md).

The real emissary and [Phlukk](phlukk.md) are moved ahead, while Zen disguises herself as the priest aboard the ice-ship to draw out the attackers. That makes her one of the key reasons the opening ambush becomes survivable intelligence rather than a clean kidnapping.

After the attack, she continues to work the problem from the analytical side. In [Ice Watch](../places/ice-watch.md) and [Frozenheart University](../places/frozenheart-university.md), she studies the residue of opened gates, identifies one-use portal objects, and helps establish that the enemy network is operating from the mortal side rather than merely invading from Hell.

## Demonic Artifacts and the Abyssal Hand

One of Zen's strongest threads in the notes is her work on infernal artifacts.

She identifies the dwarven gauntlet as a cursed command device tied to demonic and necrotic runes, with an outside controller and a disease effect that may require Greater Restoration or stronger magic to undo. She is also part of the research effort that uncovers the known faces of the [Abyssal Hand](../factions/abyssal-hand.md), especially [Zarvok the Torturer](zarvok-the-torturer.md), [Lyris the Deceiver](lyris-the-deceiver.md), [Morgrath the Enforcer](morgrath-the-enforcer.md), and [Vaelith the Whisperer](vaelith-the-whisperer.md).

That work is important because it frames Zen not as a passive scholar, but as the party member who keeps turning strange magical events into usable operational knowledge.

## Citadel and Elisia

Zen remains central once the campaign shifts to the [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md).

She helps navigate the move through [Arthur Saran](arthur-saran.md), [Lady Penny Hathaway](lady-penny-hathaway.md), and the school-side response to the collapsing divine order. During the dragon-egg rescue and Hell assault fallout, Zen is one of the characters most consistently focused on what the party is actually responsible for after the fighting stops.

That becomes clearest in the [Elisia](elisia.md) material.

After the party returns from Hell and [Cardinal Preceptor Thornvale](cardinal-preceptor-thornvale.md) pushes for a punitive response, Zen openly argues that [Elisia](elisia.md) is being treated unjustly and not even lawfully by [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md) standards. [Professor Hazard](professor-hazard.md) then gives Zen the controller to the [Collar of Demon Subjugation](../items/collar-of-demon-subjugation.md), making her the practical custodian of the compromise that saves [Elisia](elisia.md) from immediate execution.

This marks Zen as one of the campaign's clearest moral-pressure figures. She is not naive about danger, but she resists letting fear alone decide what counts as justice.

## Phlukk and Broken Minds

Zen is also deeply involved in the mystery around [Phlukk](phlukk.md).

She investigates his missing time, the cleaned-out room he leaves behind, the possibility that his abductors wiped or hollowed him out, and later the magical void that remains inside him even after his body is restored. Her thought-reading and arcane probing reveal that if [Phlukk](phlukk.md) is still fully present, he is buried very deeply.

That thread makes Zen one of the party members most associated with memory, hidden influence, and the problem of whether someone can be restored after being used by outside powers.

## Northern Expedition

After the [Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md) falls into wider crisis, Zen continues with the group's retreat and northern work on [Norvindr](../places/norvindr.md).

She helps organize travel toward [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md), studies the changing night sky, notices spelljamming movement between worlds, recovers and examines ancient dwarven light devices, and helps manage the practical dangers of the thawing north.

The later notes also show a quieter personal pattern: Zen has a strong attraction to systems, order, and hidden design. In moments of stress or intoxicated insight, she starts seeing structure in chaos and trying to understand how magic, craft, and reality fit together underneath the surface.
