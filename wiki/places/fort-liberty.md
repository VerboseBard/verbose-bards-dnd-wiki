# Fort Liberty

Fort Liberty is a major [Unity](unity.md) military base used during the late [Kreen War](../concepts/kreen-war.md) and postwar cleanup period.

## Description

Fort Liberty is better equipped than the exhausted front-line postings where many soldiers spend the war. It has training grounds, containment facilities, research space, and the logistical support needed for specialized anti-[Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) operations.

The base represents the polished side of the military machine: better command rooms, better equipment, and better paperwork. That does not make its work cleaner. Much of what happens there exists because the war has entered the phase where capture, experimentation, surrender doctrine, and political control matter as much as open battle.

## Ash's Hounds

In [Session 2](../sessions/session-2.md), survivors of the [Bloody Fifth](../factions/bloody-fifth.md) are transferred to Fort Liberty and reorganized into [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md) under [General Ash](../people/general-ash.md).

[Colonel Gandor](../people/colonel-gandor.md) becomes the Hounds' direct supervisor. The unit trains in small-pack tactics, anti-[Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) operations, and capture doctrine before being sent into missions like the [Dungeon Cemetery](dungeon-cemetery.md) and [Norinar](norinar.md).

## Research and Collars

Fort Liberty is also tied to the practical development of [Kreen Control Collars](../items/kreen-control-collars.md). The Hounds' captures and the research performed afterward help make mass [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) surrender politically and magically possible.

That achievement carries a moral wound. The same systems that prevent more slaughter also depend on captured thinking beings, coercion, experimentation, and public narratives that hide how ugly the transition from war to surrender really is.
