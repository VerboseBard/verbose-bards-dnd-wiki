# Kuran Grove

Kuran Grove is [Vylra](../people/vylra.md)'s homeland on [Norvindr](norvindr.md).

## Fifth Age

The grove lies in a warm, protected valley north of [Frostfire Keep](frostfire-keep.md), near mountains, glaciers, volcanic vents, and old giant or dwarven sites.

It is shaped by a vast chromate fungal network centered on an ancient tree called [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) or the Tree of Repose. The mycelium spreads through the grove and forms a symbiotic relationship with [Vylra](../people/vylra.md), his bees, and the local caretakers.

The grove looks both lush and unsettling to outsiders. Giant mushrooms grow like towers, bees and wasps fly like birds, Ironwood buildings are grown rather than cut, and undead laborers work beside living animals and farmers.

Visitors entering through the fields may see grove hunters on giant arctic foxes, enormous hyo-jaks and paints in the distance, undead workers tending crops, and families living inside hollowed or grown mushroom homes.

## Sixth Age

[Vylra](../people/vylra.md) describes his family as tied to the grove's leadership circles. His father and grandfather have served in circles connected to death, rebirth, and the caretaking of the grove.

The grove sends Seekers out into the world to search for other signs of chromate fungus and to bring back knowledge. The community includes several formal roles:

- Keepers maintain the grove and its daily functions.
- Hunters protect the valley and manage dangerous beasts.
- Leakers gather and spread information.
- Seekers search for other signs of chromate fungus.
- The Circles of Life, Death, and Rebirth guide the grove through [Viscus](../people/viscus.md).

The grove's current leadership is organized around three sacred circles:

- Circle of Life: [Sorkia Peck](../people/sorkia-peck.md)
- Circle of Death: [Vymra Blackwood](../people/vymra-blackwood.md)
- Circle of Rebirth: [Keck Heft](../people/keck-heft.md)

The Head Keeper of [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) completes the cardinal ritual circle and maintains the symbiosis between the grove and the mushroom tree.

Known grove figures include:

- [Serous Kentar](../people/serous-kentar.md)

In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 13](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-13.md), the grove performs the Ritual of Fated Remembrance for [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md). The ritual restores him to consciousness, though a fragment of [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) remains inside his skull.

In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 14](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-14.md), children playing near the edge of the grove fall into a collapsed hole that hides a mushroom circle. The circle transports the rescue party into the [Feywild](feywild.md), where the children have come under the protection and debt of the [Glade Mistress](../people/glade-mistress.md).

The party returns with the children after sixty-five hours have passed outside. [Paul](../people/paul.md) destroys the mushroom circle afterward.

## Seventh Age

In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 15](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-15.md), [Belle](../people/belle.md)'s [Fey](../concepts/fey.md) aspects reveal that the circle was a lifeline between the grove and the Spring Court. Its destruction wounds the grove's [Fey](../concepts/fey.md) balance and leaves [Paul](../people/paul.md) marked with the [Crown of Fools](../items/crown-of-fools.md).

In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md), the party petitions the [Summer Court](summer-court.md) to create a replacement link. Three weeks pass outside while the party is in the [Feywild](feywild.md), but the new link restores enough power to protect the grove from a dangerous cold front.

The thaw has released [Demonic Dwarven Goo](../concepts/demonic-dwarven-goo.md) from the old ice. Infected animals attack or go mad, and the grove's hunters are burning out trails of the substance.

An [Ancient Ice Hydra](../concepts/ancient-ice-hydra.md) awakens, kills an infected hyo-jak, and blocks one of the passes. In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 15](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-15.md), the party kills it and its ember hydra mate, clearing that immediate threat.

The larger threat remains the [Demonic Dwarven Goo](../concepts/demonic-dwarven-goo.md). In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 16](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-16.md), the grove prepares a living cutting of [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) so the party can attempt to convert or control the master goo in the old dwarven facility.

In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 17](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-17.md), that plan succeeds. [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) converts the master ooze beneath the [Gateway to the Empire](gateway-to-the-empire.md) and claims the wider goo network, including roughly 500,000 ancient undead soldiers preserved in frozen lakes and tunnels.

The grove is no longer merely a hidden northern community. With [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) connected to the old undead-control network, Kuran Grove becomes a major power that other nations and factions must account for. The transcript stresses that Viscus does not immediately want conquest, but the grove's status has still changed from local sanctuary to world-scale strategic actor.
