## Seventh Age

Norvindr is a world reshaped by collapse rather than conquest. Once locked beneath endless ice, it has been violently transformed by thaw, flood, and ruin.

The change did not come evenly. Some regions shattered and sank beneath rising seas, while others remained frozen, unyielding, and hostile.

The gods who once ruled here are gone. In their absence, Norvindr has turned to governance by election. Cities form councils. Districts elect governors. Authority is fragmented, negotiated, and fiercely protected, born from a collective refusal to ever return to divine rule.

The northern thaw zones are led by [Rimefall](rimefall.md), a city of indulgence, wealth, and excess built over drowned ruins. Gladiatorial arenas, treasure-diving expeditions, gambling houses, private collectors, and patron-backed raids all flourish there.

Farther south stands [Whiteridge](whiteridge.md), the capital and seat of restraint. The ice still holds there, monster populations have been forced inward, and hunting has become both profitable and necessary. Beneath the frozen ground lie sealed facilities and ancient technologies that are studied, guarded, and feared.

Norvindr's greatest transformation is economic. To rebuild quickly, the councils authorized mass undead labor. Necromantic manufacturing corps now handle construction, mining, salvage, agriculture, and hazardous recovery. That efficiency created wealth for necromancers, druids, and administrators, while displacing thousands of workers into crime, politics, or despair.

Norvindr is a world of extremes: idle elites, dispossessed laborers, wrecked cities, and old things waking beneath the thaw.

## Ten Years After

By this point the thaw has become a flood. Large portions of low-lying Norvindr are underwater, and the old map of the world no longer matches the surface.

- Some former cities are now submerged ruins and salvage zones.
- New settlements have been built higher up, on ridges, cliffs, and engineered platforms.
- Trade shifts toward flooded-port salvage, raised causeways, and adapted ship routes.
- [Rimefall](rimefall.md) remains a major center, but its relationship to the drowned districts changes from recovery to permanent expansion.
- [Whiteridge](whiteridge.md) survives as one of the last major cold anchors, but it is no longer the only obvious center of power.

The world no longer feels like a thawing ice realm. It feels like a reorganized water world that still remembers being frozen.

## Campaign Role

Norvindr is one of the major worlds in the setting brief. It shows that the linked worlds are not just battlefields in the [Kreen War](../concepts/kreen-war.md), but distinct civilizations reacting differently to the postwar order.

In [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 1](../sessions/moon-stone-collectors-part-1.md), the party escorts a disguised emissary toward [Ice Watch](ice-watch.md), the seat of religious power on Norvindr, and is ambushed by demons on an ice-ship route.

In the later [Moon Stone Collectors](../factions/moon-stone-collectors.md) arc, the party returns to Norvindr after fleeing the [Radiant Citadel](radiant-citadel.md). They travel through [Frostfire Keep](frostfire-keep.md), the [Chuckling Gecko](chuckling-gecko.md), thawing tundra, ancient dwarven outposts, and a vast frozen lake on the road toward the [Kuran Grove](kuran-grove.md).

The thaw also releases old dangers from before the [Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md), including [Demonic Dwarven Goo](../concepts/demonic-dwarven-goo.md), infected hyo-jaks, ancient undead under the ice, and an [Ancient Ice Hydra](../concepts/ancient-ice-hydra.md).

The world remembers who benefited from the thaw, and who was left behind.
