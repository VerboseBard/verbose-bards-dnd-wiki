# Saharun

Saharun is a desert world associated with death, sealed borders, dragonborn history, and [Alen](../people/alen.md) in her role as the [Raven Queen of Saharun](../people/raven-queen-of-saharun.md).

## Description

Saharun was never conquered. It was sealed. For most of its history, the world existed as a closed system - politically, religiously, and culturally isolated from the wider worlds.

That isolation ended with the war, not by invasion but by necessity. Saharun now stands at a crossroads, and the structures that once defined it - faith, lineage, and divine authority - are under immense strain.

The central truth known beyond Saharun's borders is that the desert world is in turmoil. What form that turmoil takes is difficult to discern from the outside, because information is tightly controlled and filtered through treaties, envoys, and deliberate silence.

During the war, Saharun introduced dragonborn as soldiers engineered for conflict, loyalty, and survival. After the fighting ended, it became impossible to deny that they were not constructs but children - and, worse, that they are capable of reproduction and dramatically accelerated maturation.

As those questions fractured institutions, the Queen ascended. Her transformation into both goddess and dragon cemented a new divine reality for the desert world, even as many questioned what that meant for the old order.

In response to that instability, Saharun has restricted nearly all external access. Trade is halted. Foreign diplomacy is minimal. Outside influence is unwelcome. Veterans of the war remain the one treaty exception, allowed passage upon completion of service.

Despite everything, Saharun has not collapsed. For now, order is maintained by faith, by force, and by those determined to hold the world together through transition rather than fracture.

## Campaign Role

Saharun is discussed in the wider world briefing and later becomes important through references to the Raven Queen, the crusading church, and the uncertain politics of the new age.

In the [Black Vipers](../factions/black-vipers.md) side arc, Saharun becomes the main refuge and recruiting ground for Viper remnants. [Silverbend](silver-bend.md) is attacked, [Big Tusk](big-tusk.md) becomes a casino lead, the [Shili Jungle](shili-jungle.md) becomes a cult-scouting target, and old serpent worshippers connected to [Rexel Kar](../people/rexel-kar.md) complicate the hunt.

[Soul Cage](../items/soul-cage.md) interrogation confirms that several assassins connected to [Charlie](../people/charlie.md) and [Jimbo](../people/jimbo.md)'s deaths remain on Saharun or are returning to it. The world also holds [Constance and Zuk's Dragon-Bonding School](constance-and-zuks-dragon-bonding-school.md), now a likely target because of its concentration of children, dragons, eggs, and bonded souls.

The old [Silverbend](silver-bend.md) mine loses its renewable green-crystal source when the [Possibility Core](../concepts/possibility-core.md) collapses, altering the balance between mortal secrecy and divine sight on Saharun.
