# The Silk Parlor

The Silk Parlor is a luxury establishment in [Sin](sin.md), run by [Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](../people/mistress-sila-vexry.md), previously transcribed as Mistress Sila Vex'Ry. It combines courtesan services, information gathering, black-market influence, and carefully controlled intimacy.

![The Women of the Silk Parlor](../assets/images/silk-parlor-women.jpeg)

## Description

The Parlor is the largest and most impressive building in town, and it dominates the center of [Sin](sin.md) both physically and socially. Wealth, secrets, favors, blackmail, pleasure, and quiet negotiations all pass through its doors.

The lower levels are where soldiers, merchants, gamblers, and opportunists mix. The upper spaces are more exclusive, more expensive, and far more dangerous. The Parlor is refined, expensive, and politically dangerous, and its workers are trained to extract information socially, magically, and psychologically.

Mistress Vex'ryn uses the Parlor as a power base. She offers the party payment to ignore her scavenging teams and hints at future employment if they prove useful.

## Women of the Silk Parlor

From left to right in the current visual reference:

- Velrith Nyx: chained woman with the goblet; dominant, unshakable, and likely one of Selyra's most trusted enforcers.
- Kaelis Varn: dark-haired watcher; a listener, reader of people, and information gatherer.
- Serenya Vael: pale, centered, and seated prominently; calm, refined, and suited to high-end clients.
- Draevira Sol: standing behind the others; composed, poised, and likely tied to internal security or oversight.

Other named Parlor figures include Kael Vex'ryn, Lethira Morn, Myrren Dusk, Zyraen Vex, and Thalira Noct.

## Campaign Role

The party's dinner at the Parlor reveals the true hierarchy of [Sin](sin.md). Vex'ryn makes clear that she has leverage over [Colonel Marrow Vance](../people/colonel-marrow-vance.md), controls most artifact flow through town, and wants drow-related artifacts from the fog.

The place is less a business than a machine for influence.
