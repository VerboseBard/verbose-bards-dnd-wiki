# Sin

Sin is not a grand city. It is a crowded, low-built frontier town pressed between danger and opportunity.

## Fifth Age

No [Fifth Age](../concepts/fifth-age.md) material has been added for Sin yet.

## Sixth Age

Sin begins as a rough frontier town feeding off military presence, salvage, and rumor near [Fort Victory](fort-victory.md) and the nearby trade routes.

## Seventh Age

Most of Sin is a maze of narrow streets, crooked rooftops, warm windowlight, and buildings packed so close together they seem to lean on one another for support. It has the feel of a place that grew too quickly and was never meant to matter this much. And yet it does.

What makes Sin important is not its beauty, but its position. It sits close enough to [Fort Victory](fort-victory.md) and Khaz-Tharol to feed off military presence, salvage, trade, rumor, and corruption. Soldiers come here to spend coin. Smugglers come here to make coin. Priests come here to keep order. Everyone else comes here because they have nowhere better to go.

At a glance, Sin looks shabby. At a second glance, it reveals its true nature: a town where power is concentrated in a few key locations, and where almost everyone answers to someone.

## Major Locations in Sin

### [The Silk Parlor](silk-parlor.md)

The largest and most impressive building in town, the [Silk Parlor](silk-parlor.md) dominates the center of Sin both physically and socially. It is a casino, fine dining hall, and brothel under one roof, but more than that, it is a machine for influence. Wealth, secrets, favors, blackmail, pleasure, and quiet negotiations all pass through its doors.

The lower levels are where soldiers, merchants, opportunists, and gamblers mix. The upper spaces are more exclusive, more expensive, and far more dangerous.

The Parlor's staff are as much part of the city's power structure as its patrons. Among the names spoken in town are [Selyra Vex'ryn](../people/mistress-selyra-vexryn.md), Kael Vex'ryn, Velrith Nyx, Kaelis Varn, Serenya Vael, Draevira Sol, Lethira Morn, Myrren Dusk, Zyraen Vex, and Thalira Noct.

### [Calvin's Curios](calvins-curios.md)

Despite the name, [Calvin's Curios](calvins-curios.md) is not a cluttered novelty shop. It is a high-end auction house and black-market exchange for the wealthy, the connected, and the desperate. Rare relics, military-approved surplus, strange salvage, restricted goods, and objects no one should openly own all pass through here if the buyer is acceptable.

The building itself feels curated rather than commercial. It is a place where value is performed as much as measured.

### [The Singing Sword](singing-sword.md)

[The Singing Sword](singing-sword.md) is the cheaper, louder, and more approachable heart of Sin. It is the town's working tavern, a place for soldiers, adventurers, laborers, drunks, gossip, and passing opportunity.

Its defining feature is the massive ancient sword embedded in stone within the building itself. The blade is enormous, far older than the tavern, and treated with the kind of casual reverence only frontier people can manage.

### [Church of the Gods](church-of-the-gods.md)

The church on the edge of town serves the pantheons recognized by the [Conclave of Churches](../factions/conclave-of-churches.md), rather than a single deity. It is not a grand cathedral, but it is an important stabilizing force in Sin.

It offers ritual, doctrine, counsel, and the public face of Conclave legitimacy in a town where legitimacy is always under pressure.

## Campaign Role

The party uses Sin cautiously, preferring to avoid long stays. They visit the [Singing Sword](singing-sword.md), meet local power brokers, and later avoid town once the [Inquisition](../factions/inquisition.md) begins tightening control.

Sin is the place where military presence, criminal opportunity, and spiritual authority all collide without ever fully controlling one another.
