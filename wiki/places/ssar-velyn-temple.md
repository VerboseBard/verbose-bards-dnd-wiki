# Ssar'Velyn Temple

Ssar'Velyn Temple is the ancient hidden temple complex at the southern forbidden edge of [Sunhollow Valley](sunhollow-valley.md), extending through mountain approaches and underground chambers preserved from the drow era.

## Description

The temple was entrusted to the valley's surviving lineages after the failed beastfolk slave uprising long before [the Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md). The clans were meant to keep its secret, guard its approaches, and prevent anyone from reclaiming what was sealed inside.

That charge was never lifted.

The temple contains a fragment of [Lolth](../people/lolth.md)'s divinity. It remained unclaimed for so long that the fragment's presence has swollen into an active corruption, poisoning the area around the temple and slowly worsening the valley's hidden danger.

[Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md) clarifies that the temple corruption is not only a simple fragment leak. It is an accumulated temple-and-forest corruption system tied to Lolth's old theft, a damaged sacred land, oily web-like blight, and living or surviving guardians who still enforce the old law.

## Fourth-Age Context

During the [Fourth Age](../concepts/fourth-age.md), many peoples warred against Lolth and her cult, with the dwarves carrying much of the burden. Part of that campaign grew out of Lolth's betrayal of the other gods and her role in the downfall of [Hestia](../people/hestia.md), goddess of hearth and refuge.

Lolth survived destruction through scattered fragments of spirit. Hestia survived in a similar fragmented form, but some of Hestia's fragments were later captured by Lolth's priestesses and hidden near Lolth-linked sites such as Ssar'Velyn Temple. If Lolth were ever restored fully, those fragments could be consumed to accelerate her return.

The Session 8 ghost-druid account adds that the valley began as a shared elven holy ground and treaty place before Lolth's corruption. In that version of the history, Lolth allied with the first dragon and [the Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md), killed a god, stole divine power, and turned the holy ground into a spider-corrupted temple system.

The same session introduces [Bartholomew Hildebrant](../people/bartholomew-hildebrant.md), a Fourth-Age dwarf survivor from one of roughly ten assault teams sent against the temple. That assault nearly destroyed the site and left very few active guardians behind.

## Sunhollow Role

Only the seated clan elders of [the Sunhollow Council](../factions/sunhollow-council.md) and their chosen heirs know the full truth of the temple duty.

That secrecy became unstable when the [Moonhare](../people/cedric-moonhare.md) rabbit heir vanished more than a century ago and his father died before the knowledge could be securely passed on. The council chose not to restore the lost rabbit seat, turning a sacred custodial failure into a long political wound.

Approach to the deeper sacred zone is also governed by old access law. By [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), the council states that lawful entry requires an emissary, traditionally an elf or someone accompanied by a sentient spider recognized under that role.

In [Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md), [Gwen](../people/gwen.md) is recognized by the [Ssar'Velyn Ghost Priestess](../people/ssar-velyn-ghost-priestess.md) as the active emissary. [Bevar Lurton](../people/bevar-lurton.md) follows Gwen into the emissary path, while [Captain Sgt. Jose](../people/jose.md), [Alistair Hooley](../people/alistair-hooley.md), [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](../people/sergeant-jefferson-stone.md), and Bartholomew are forced through temple trials.

The [Ssar'Velyn Temple Guardian](../people/ssar-velyn-temple-guardian.md) later explains that the valley's oath-bound inhabitants may be forced to defend the temple if outsiders come to seize its wealth. This creates the central moral problem of the Session 8 ending: destroying or exposing the temple may also doom thousands of people who inherited the oath without necessarily knowing its full truth.

## Session 9 Revelations

In [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), Jefferson's separated route exposes hidden temple infrastructure beyond the public trial spaces. He finds a shattered egg or crypt chamber, an archive or library, and a vat room full of thick purple demonic ichor.

Jefferson collects a [Demonic Ichor Vial](../items/demonic-ichor-vial.md) after the substance burns through leather but holds in treated glass. The vat system suggests that the temple's damaged defenses and processes were more extensive than the party saw during the trials.

[Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](../people/mistress-selyra-vexryn.md) appears to Jefferson in an office she calls her mother's office. She claims that the immediate source of the spreading corruption is something imprisoned in a cave south along the wall, not the temple itself. Because Jefferson can tell she is hiding motives and may be lying, this should be treated as a claim or partial truth rather than a replacement for the temple-fragment corruption model.

Vex'ryn also claims she needs one to two weeks to finish work connected to the temple and the valley's blood oath. If the [Inquisition](../factions/inquisition.md) threatens the temple before then, she warns that old oaths and old magics may kill many people.
