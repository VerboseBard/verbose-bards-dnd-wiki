# Sunhollow Valley

Sunhollow Valley is a real fog-hidden homeland basin on [Driftvale](driftvale.md), tied to beastfolk and fog-walker ancestry that had long survived only as rumor and family story.

## Description

The valley lies beyond normal patrol range in a basin where the fog parts dramatically rather than simply thinning. By the time the party reaches it in [Session 6](../sessions/session-6.md), they confirm several things that earlier scouts only hinted at:

- the valley is genuinely sunlit and far clearer than the surrounding foglands
- visibility opens up for miles instead of feet
- there are villages, smoke, open grass, water, and cultivated life below
- beastfolk patrols actively guard the approaches
- the first scout's marker lies near the final high-pass approach

It is not just a hidden camp. It is a defended living region.

The valley's known local name is Sunhollow Valley. The older drow name for the valley is `Ssar'Velyn`. The hidden temple the clans guard there is known as the `Ssar'Velyn Temple`.

The temple contains a fragment of Lolth's divinity. That fragment has remained unclaimed for so long that its power is beginning to poison the area around it, turning the temple from a buried responsibility into a growing threat.

By [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), the party confirms that the danger is no longer only hidden. A visible blighted quarter called the [Dead Lands](dead-lands.md) is spreading from the temple side of the valley.

Session 7 also confirms that the valley's overwhelming ether crystal wealth is not merely economic. [Lake of Glass](lake-of-glass.md)'s crystal growth is tied to ancient divine power connected to [Big Shad](../concepts/big-shad.md), not to normal ley-line growth.

## Peoples and Clans

Sunhollow is governed through five major clan groupings:

- [Ashmane](../factions/ashmane.md), the politically dominant cat clan, including lionfolk, catfolk, and lynx-like cat lines
- [Windcrest](../factions/windcrest.md), the broad bird coalition, including owlfolk, raven and crow folk, hawk and eagle folk, rooster and chicken folk, songbird folk, and other avian peoples
- [Briarstep](../factions/briarstep.md), the small-predator clan, including foxfolk, Mapach, and otter-, weasel-, ferret-, and badger-like folk
- [Burrowroot](../factions/burrowroot.md), the broad non-predatory bloc, including rabbitfolk, deerfolk, goatfolk, hedgehogfolk, mouse and jerboa smallfolk, and turtlefolk
- [Longbough](../factions/longbough.md), the Hadozee clan, made up of gliding apefolk with an old-duty role in the valley

These clans together form [the Sunhollow Council](../factions/sunhollow-council.md).

## Major Settlements

The current mapped settlements of Sunhollow include:

- [Hollowmere](hollowmere.md), the central lakeside council settlement
- [Windcrest Roost](windcrest-roost.md), the northwestern bird-clan stronghold
- [Burrowroot Warren](burrowroot-warren.md), the western Burrowroot stronghold
- [Briarstep Cross](briarstep-cross.md), the eastern Briarstep stronghold
- [Longbough Canopy](longbough-canopy.md), the southwestern Hadozee stronghold
- [Ashmane Hold](ashmane-hold.md), the southern cat-clan stronghold

The valley's central water is the [Lake of Glass](lake-of-glass.md).

The main blighted quarter of the valley is the [Dead Lands](dead-lands.md).

## Rabbit Question

The rabbit folk once held a distinct seat through the old Moonhare line. That standing collapsed more than a century ago when [Cedric Moonhare](../people/cedric-moonhare.md) vanished on a trade mission and [Edmund Moonhare](../people/edmund-moonhare.md) died before the inner temple burden could be passed on securely.

Rather than appoint a replacement, the council folded the rabbits into [Burrowroot](../factions/burrowroot.md), creating a wound that still shapes valley politics.

Older rabbit memory preserves another layer to that story: Harengon caravans once left the valley through the fog on a recurring trade cycle because the rabbits were never fully caught by the same blood-oath structure that bound the other clans.

[Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md) sharpens this point. The temple guardian says the rabbits are not all bound in the same way because the rabbit population grew too quickly and because the original oath structure did not catch the entire rabbit population. That makes the rabbit exception both political and magical.

## First Contact

At the end of [Session 6](../sessions/session-6.md), the party reaches the high pass and sees the valley for the first time: a wide fog-free basin with sunlight, trees, grass, water, villages, and smoke rising from settlements. A patrol with leonine, rabbit, Mapach, foxfolk, and ratfolk members approaches. Their first posture is hostile, then softens into wary curiosity when they see [Alistair Hooley](../people/alistair-hooley.md).

In [Session 7](../sessions/session-7.md), the valley's first formal contact with the party happens through a patrol led by [Captain Havlin](../people/captain-havlin.md). The meeting confirms three major problems at once:

- the valley's ether crystal wealth is vast enough to destabilize any outside response
- the party's marked route may already be compromised by followers, since a dead human scout is found with some of the party's marker discs
- the spreading blight in the [Dead Lands](dead-lands.md) has become severe enough that the council must consider outside help

The party's first stop inside the valley is [Burrowroot Warren](burrowroot-warren.md), where [Alistair Hooley](../people/alistair-hooley.md)'s family name begins reopening buried rabbit politics.

In [Session 8](../sessions/session-8.md), the party reaches [Ssar'Velyn Temple](ssar-velyn-temple.md) and learns the valley's deepest danger: many inhabitants may be bound by inherited blood oath to defend the temple even if they do not know the full truth. The outside forces following the party may kill or exploit the valley for its crystal wealth, but destroying the temple carelessly may also doom the bound locals.

In [Session 9](../sessions/session-9.md), the threat becomes immediate. The [Inquisition](../factions/inquisition.md) reports that it is about two days out from the party's last marker, while [Mistress Selyra Vex'ryn](../people/mistress-sila-vexry.md) claims she needs one to two weeks before the temple and oath work can be completed. [Rurik Valdren](../people/rurik-valdren.md) is brought into the crisis as a possible legal, commercial, or military counterweight to outside seizure.

By the end of the session, the party has returned to the old elven conclave site where the council meets. Jefferson recognizes that the surviving elven architecture may still be tied to old defensive systems, which matters because tens of thousands of animalfolk live in the valley and a large portion of the population appears to be children.

## Hidden Political Currents

Three currents matter most inside Sunhollow politics:

- [The Old Charge](../factions/old-charge.md), the secrecy-first isolationists
- [The Open Trail](../factions/open-trail.md), the cautious pragmatists
- [The Sixth Seat](../factions/sixth-seat.md), the quiet cause of rabbit restoration

## Campaign Role

[Colonel Core](../people/colonel-core.md) hires the party as contractors to scout a route to the valley. The mission requires pathfinding, safe camp markers, wagon-passable routes, and careful first contact.

The route takes roughly a month of scouting, dead ends, bridge checks, mountain passes, and return loops before the party finds a workable wagon path. Their arrival proves that the homeland stories behind the [Lost Homeland Mission](../concepts/lost-homeland-mission.md) are true and shifts the campaign from rumor-chasing into first-contact territory.
