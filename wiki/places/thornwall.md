# Thornwall

Thornwall is a hedge-walled village in Constance's homeland during the [War of the Gods](../concepts/war-of-the-gods.md).

## Description

Rather than stone fortifications, Thornwall relies on a massive defensive hedge: roughly thirty feet high, thick enough to slow people and animals, and threaded with druidic magic. One route through the hedge can be opened with a spoken trigger phrase known to local defenders.

The village includes farm districts, an inn or tavern district, and a nearby shrine complex hidden beyond an old bridge and overgrown ruins. That shrine becomes the main refuge for the survivors after the village falls, and the hidden hedge route becomes the difference between a survivable infiltration and a suicidal frontal assault.

## Fall During the War

During [War of the Gods, Session 16](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-16.md), survivors report that [Wendell](../people/wendell.md) arrived over Thornwall on an undead wyvern. A second figure, remembered as the "Deathbringer," jumped down and began casting death magic through the settlement.

People killed by the opening attack rose almost immediately, and the resulting plague spread faster than local defenders could contain it. The village church was later destroyed, fires spread through town, and the inn district became a temporary stronghold for occupying dork troops.

When the party returns through the hidden hedge route, Thornwall is no longer just "occupied." It is layered with undead spillover, fog-blighted vegetation, fire damage, and magical corruption. The attempt to reclaim the tavern becomes one of the arc's messiest fights, and the retreat back to the shrine makes clear that even small tactical gains in Thornwall are bought at serious cost.

By [War of the Gods, Session 17](../sessions/war-of-the-gods-session-17.md), the church site has become something worse than desecrated ground: a dungeon-like magical sink full of traps, treasure caches, alarms, and a deeper fire-fed shaft that appears to be draining or redirecting magical power.

That shift matters because Thornwall stops being merely a refugee tragedy and becomes evidence of [Wendell](../people/wendell.md)'s systems-level war. The ruined church is part fortress, part magical engine, and part prototype dungeon.

## Survivor Refuge

A hidden shrine outside Thornwall shelters the surviving children, farmers, and noncombatants. Refugees are protected there by a small group of defenders, including an old widow with a cursed anti-undead blade, a missionary cleric, a magically gifted musician sustaining the sanctuary's systems, and [Charlie](../people/charlie.md).

The same area later attracts outside anti-[Wendell](../people/wendell.md) forces, including scouts from a goblinoid aerial division that also treats the site as strategically significant. Their arrival reinforces that Thornwall is not a forgotten village on the edge of the war. It is part of a broader strategic contest over ley-line damage, undead spread, and who gets to define what the next age will look like.
