## Seventh Age

Unity is the central political and logistical hub of the known worlds after [the Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md). It links the surviving worlds, hosts major military infrastructure, and contains the [Radiant Citadel](radiant-citadel.md), the largest known nexus of travel, trade, and planar administration.

Unity is not merely a capital world. It is the only known world with true dungeons rather than only dungeon-like breaks or magical wounds. It also contains strange internal geography, including a hollow-world structure, the [Aether Sea](../concepts/aether-sea.md), and the inverted realm commonly called the [Underdark / Upside-Down](underdark-upside-down.md).

Unity is politically dense. The [Unified Armed Forces](../factions/unified-armed-forces.md), the [Conclave of Churches](../factions/conclave-of-churches.md), guilds, lords, and shipping interests all operate through it. Major military sites such as [Fort Liberty](fort-liberty.md) show the polished side of that system: training grounds, research facilities, and command structures built to turn wartime chaos into doctrine.

On Topside, the surface is badly scarred by magical war. Entire regions were warped, poisoned, erased, or made uninhabitable. In the aftermath, surviving powers have shifted into competition rather than peace.

The world also contains the political and logistical weight of the [Radiant Citadel](radiant-citadel.md), which stabilizes impossible amounts of magic at the [Nexus](../concepts/nexus.md) and controls sanctioned access into the Core.

Below the surface, the underground economy is harsher. The Guild still dominates the major trade route between Topside and the [Upside Down](underdark-upside-down.md), while drow, duergar, and other powers contest routes, tunnels, and old systems beyond it.

## Campaign Role

The party begins as soldiers moving between fronts under Unity's military order. Their early service, the formation of [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md), and later reassignment to [Driftvale](driftvale.md) all pass through Unity's military machine.

Unity is also where the larger postwar consequences settle: surrendered [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) become gray laborers, dungeons keep growing without enough staff to manage them, and the major worlds all react differently to the new order.
