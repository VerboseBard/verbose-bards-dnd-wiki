# Moon Stone Collectors, Part 13: The Kuran Grove

Part 13 brings the [Moon Stone Collectors](../factions/moon-stone-collectors.md) into the [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md). [Juan](../people/juan.md) purges a soul-intrusion, the party learns more about the demonic dwarven infection, [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md) is restored through a forbidden grove ritual, and the next threat is identified: an ancient ice hydra blocking one of the passes.

## Juan's Soul

Before the party reaches the grove, [Juan](../people/juan.md) remains unconscious after the events at [Ebonwing Manor](../places/ebonwing-manor.md). In a dreamlike soul-scape resembling the [Fey](../concepts/fey.md) realm, he walks a familiar path toward a lake and waterfall.

On either side of the path, he sees memories that may be true, false, stolen, or manipulated: his first encounter with Fate, his brother's apparent triumphs, and alternate visions of himself alongside the [Hell's Bane](../factions/hells-bane-heroes.md) heroes.

Taken together with the truth revealed in [Moon Stone Collectors, Part 9](moon-stone-collectors-part-9.md) and the later amulet-memory, this scene is one of the clearest indications that [The Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md) has touched [Juan](../people/juan.md)'s life directly. His dreams are not just symbolic. They are part of the space where fate, memory, and identity have been altered around him.

[Juan](../people/juan.md) realizes he is inside his own soul and that a dark presence has been shaping what he remembers. When he steps off the well-trodden path, the presence becomes afraid. [Juan](../people/juan.md) tears away its tendrils from his memories, recovering a lost memory of peaceful brotherhood before rivalry defined him.

His anger ignites as fire. He burns the foreign presence out of his soul, leaving a scorched valley of ash. The manipulation is gone, but his luck remains part of him.

## Awake Again

[Juan](../people/juan.md) wakes at the familiar inn on [Norvindr](../places/norvindr.md). The others explain what happened while he was out: [Elisia](../people/elisia.md) was consumed or overtaken by [Julia / Julisia](../people/julia-julisia.md), the party fled the [Radiant Citadel](../places/radiant-citadel.md), and they are taking [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md) to the [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md).

For the next part of the journey, [Juan](../people/juan.md) keeps mostly to himself, healing from the revelations about luck, fate, and accountability.

## The Goo Returns

At the tree-line camp, strange burn tunnels in the snow reveal that the slime dripping from the infected hyo-jak is moving toward the party's sled and [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md).

The substance is the same demonic dwarven infection first encountered in the early [Moon Stone Collectors](../factions/moon-stone-collectors.md) arc: necrotic, demonic, resistant to many forms of harm, and vulnerable to fire.

One trace of the infection gets into [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md). [Julara](../people/julara.md) notices the demonic presence, and greater restoration forces the black goo out through his eyes, nose, ears, and mouth with a psychic scream.

The goats are also infected. Two are restored, while the others are put down and later burned to prevent spread.

## Hunters from the Grove

As the party deals with the infection, hunters from the [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md) arrive riding giant arctic foxes and burning out the slime trails.

[Vylra](../people/vylra.md) recognizes [Kaithra Mally](../people/kaithra-mally.md), a skilled goblin tracker, and his uncle [Volko Blackwood](../people/volko-blackwood.md). A frost gnome named [Perrian Halfstar](../people/perrian-halfstar.md) is also present, helping handle the foxes.

They explain that the infection has been appearing for a couple of weeks. The thaw seems to have released something from the deep ice. Animals go mad quickly; people can resist for a while, but repeated exposure eventually takes them over.

The hunters warn that the infected hyo-jak is not the only problem. An ancient ice hydra has awakened, killed the bear, blocked one pass, and collapsed another.

## Into the Grove

The party enters the [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md), a warm valley surrounded by ice, glaciers, mountains, and giant fungal growth. Bees and wasps the size of birds fly among enormous mushrooms, Ironwood buildings, massive living animals, and undead laborers.

To outsiders, the grove looks like paradise and nightmare forced to share a body: lush fields, giant beasts, hippie-druid hospitality, skeletal workers, zombie labor, and strict resource cycling.

[Vylra](../people/vylra.md) explains the grove's structure:

- Keepers maintain the community.
- Hunters protect the valley and manage dangerous beasts.
- Leakers gather and spread information.
- Seekers leave the grove to search for other signs of chromate fungus.
- The three Circles of Life, Death, and Rebirth guide the grove through [Viscus](../people/viscus.md).

## Family Welcome

[Vylra](../people/vylra.md)'s family overwhelms the party with food, smoke, pipes, music, and welcome.

His mother [Vivra Blackwood](../people/vivra-blackwood.md) immediately decides everyone is too thin, especially [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md), and begins feeding him even though he is still unconscious. The party learns that [Vylra](../people/vylra.md)'s cooking is only a faint shadow of his mother's and grandmother's.

The Blackwoods live in a hollowed living mushroom, with walls that can regrow edible pieces. The food is powerful enough to function like a feast blessing for those who fully indulge.

## Viscus

[Vylra](../people/vylra.md)'s father takes him beneath the great mushroom-tree [Viscus](../people/viscus.md). Roots close around [Vylra](../people/vylra.md), and [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) receives everything he has experienced since leaving the grove.

In return, [Vylra](../people/vylra.md) sees glimpses of what happened during his absence: growth in the grove, hive joinings, the shock of [the Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md), ley lines activating, and the changing weather.

[Viscus](../people/viscus.md) agrees to help [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md).

## Ritual of Fated Remembrance

[Vylra](../people/vylra.md) asks the heads of the three circles for the Ritual of Fated Remembrance, a secret and nearly forgotten working used when a mind has been broken in an unnatural way.

The circle leaders hesitate because the ritual disrupts the normal cycle of death and rebirth, but an ancient undead called the Keeper has already agreed.

[Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md) is prepared in a hidden chamber. His feathers are plucked, ritual markings are drawn over his body, and the druids chant in an older language. The ritual struggles because [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md)'s memories are full of holes left by the infection, but the Keeper stabilizes it.

[Viscus](../people/viscus.md) drives a root into [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md)'s skull and leaves a piece inside. The marks ignite, burning into his body beneath his regrown feathers. [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md) wakes in a lower chamber, alive and himself, but missing the period between escorting the priest and his restoration.

## What the Dwarves Did

[Paul](../people/paul.md) continues studying the dwarven records from the old outpost. [Julara](../people/julara.md) finds corroborating demonic records.

The infection traces back to a false-name deal made between the dwarves and a lesser demon later known as [Zarvok the Torturer](../people/zarvok-the-torturer.md). The dwarves wanted controllable undead shock troops. The demonic substance was meant to animate and control dead or living bodies through command amulets.

A variant escaped control before the [Breaking](../concepts/the-breaking.md), causing an undead outbreak that forced the dwarves to withdraw and left the orc legions holding the line. The ice preserved the infection, which now reactivates as the thaw exposes old battlefields.

[Julara](../people/julara.md) also confirms that later dwarven blood-magic rituals with this demonic influence helped create the duergar-like descendants who survived by consuming those who did not accept the transformation.

## The Hydra Problem

The grove's hunters explain that an ancient ice hydra has awakened in the pass. It killed the infected hyo-jak, blocked the eastern route, and collapsed the northeastern pass.

The party considers diplomacy, but the grove's animal-handlers already judged the hydra too hostile to approach safely. They decide to prepare for a dangerous fight after a night's rest.

The session ends with [Juan](../people/juan.md) using the [Amulet of Lost Memories](../items/amulet-of-lost-memories.md) to seek a specific lost memory. He fails to catch that memory and is instead swept into the past, seeing the dwarven outpost before its fall. A dwarven mage receives orders to prevent knowledge of the escaped variant from returning home, reveals infected black blood, and spreads the goo to begin the catastrophe.
