# Moon Stone Collectors, Part 17: Viscus Takes the Master Goo

Part 17 closes the [Moon Stone Collectors](../factions/moon-stone-collectors.md)' arc. The party descends into the hidden dwarven facility, survives the control-room gauntlet, converts the master ooze through [Viscus](../people/viscus.md), and leaves [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md) as a new world power.

## Hidden Stair

After opening the moonlit cliff door at the old [Gateway to the Empire](../places/gateway-to-the-empire.md), the party descends into a deep dwarven facility. The door shuts behind them, though the three key discs are released back into their possession.

Magical braziers ignite as they move downward. The first chamber contains a purple ley-like magical flame, several dwarven statues, a seated figure with a lever, and sockets for the key discs.

Using the recovered poems, [Zen](../people/zen.md)'s reading magic, and several investigation checks, the group identifies the statues tied to craft, life and death, elemental work, and sight. They place the discs correctly and pull the lever, opening the way deeper.

## Trap Gauntlet

The next rooms are a dwarven trap gauntlet of skull doors, pressure plates, magical trip-lines, firebolts, lightning floors, acid bursts, blowgun walls, rotating levers, and portal controls.

[Denny](../people/denny.md) is sent ahead first and is badly shocked, burned, and stunned by the first trapped corridor. [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md)'s truesight lets him map many of the triggers, while [Soda](../people/soda.md) discovers that the skull-mouth door opens only while someone holds a central plate.

The party bypasses or brute-forces much of the complex with flight, crawling, jumping, mage hand, an immovable rod, a welding torch, shield cover, and enough stubbornness to make old dwarven safety engineers weep.

## Portals and Power

Beyond the lever rooms, the party reaches portal chambers tied to red, blue, and purple magical systems. Some doors are magically locked, and some routes must be reconfigured by changing the color-state of the system.

The facility also contains raw magical-energy channels and disintegration wells. Anyone magically sensitive recognizes that direct contact would be equivalent to a catastrophic disintegration effect.

[Vylra](../people/vylra.md) speaks with the strange mushrooms growing near one of the power wells and collects some to bring back to [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md). The mushrooms prove oddly conversational, though their sense of time and selfhood is very fungal.

## Giant Skeleton and Goo

In one chamber, the party finds a huge skeleton on a throne. [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md), still learning how his new magic works, attempts to cast reincarnate on it without understanding the limits or components involved.

The attempt stirs gray goo around the bones instead. [Vylra](../people/vylra.md) reacts by casting bone cage, tearing the bones into protective armor and accidentally exposing himself to the forming infection.

[p'Op](../people/p-op.md) strikes the goo with the holy-water flamethrower, fire is driven onto it, and [Luka](../people/luka.md) rages into the fight to keep the infection from reaching the living cutting of [Viscus](../people/viscus.md). [Vylra](../people/vylra.md) resists the infection, the [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) cutting survives, and the remaining goo is pulled into a disintegration well and destroyed.

## Control-Room Shortcut

Back in the portal puzzle, the party identifies which ritual circles are key sockets and which are teleport circles. Strong arcana, history, and luck checks let them bypass much of the remaining facility.

Instead of fighting through every defense room, they route the portal directly to the final control chamber.

## Master Goo

The final chamber contains the master ooze: a massive bound gray goo suspended in cables, chains, and control conduits. It can spawn infected servants, lash out psychically or physically, and use the room's defenses as lair actions.

The party's objective is to insert the living [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) cutting into the control apparatus, then maintain the panel long enough for [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) to take the network.

The fight becomes a defensive ritual:

- [Juan](../people/juan.md) and [Zen](../people/zen.md) work the control panel through luck, arcana, blessing, bardic support, and a borrowed luck ring.
- [Vylra](../people/vylra.md) inserts and protects the [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) cutting while throwing up fire, healing, spores, and battlefield control.
- [p'Op](../people/p-op.md), [Phlukk](../people/phlukk.md), and the others blast or disable waves of goo-servants with holy water, radiant shots, thunder, psychic magic, stinking cloud, faerie fire, and forced movement.
- [Luka](../people/luka.md), in a zealot rage, nearly kills the master goo outright before the party restrains and redirects him.

At one point, [Luka](../people/luka.md) is shoved into the goo and briefly infected. After [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) takes control, [Luka](../people/luka.md) survives and purges the infection through divine force.

## Viscus Claims the Network

The final control check succeeds. The color of the chamber shifts, power runs through the cables, and [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) converts the master ooze.

This does not simply destroy the [Demonic Dwarven Goo](../concepts/demonic-dwarven-goo.md). It places the ancient goo network under [Viscus](../people/viscus.md)'s control:

- The master ooze is converted.
- The wider infection is redirected.
- Roughly 500,000 undead soldiers in the frozen lakes and dwarven tunnels are claimed.
- The network of infected beasts, bodies, and goo nodes becomes part of [Viscus](../people/viscus.md)'s reach.

The immediate mass-infection threat to [Norvindr](../places/norvindr.md) and the world is neutralized, but the result makes [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md) a political and military power on the scale of nations. The transcript emphasizes that Viscus is not unreasonable and has no immediate need to conquer, but the grove has become a power that the world must reckon with.

## Sovereignty Made Whole

After the control-room victory, the scene shifts to the [Summer Court](../places/summer-court.md). The Green Man confirms that he has fulfilled an old bargain and kept [Wisdom](../people/wisdom.md) safe for roughly a thousand years.

[Wisdom](../people/wisdom.md), also known as Sophia, meets the demonic knowledge fragment [Julara](../people/julara.md). [Sovereignty](../people/sovereignty.md) thanks [Wisdom](../people/wisdom.md) for the insight that made this possible, and the remaining fragments merge.

[Sovereignty](../people/sovereignty.md) becomes a new whole: not merely Conquer, not merely Justice, and not simply any single sister restored. Because the merger occurs in the [Feywild](../places/feywild.md), she is too powerful to enter the mortal realm and does not ascend into the heavens.

## Ending

The [Moon Stone Collectors](../factions/moon-stone-collectors.md) are remembered not only for the Hell rescue, the [Concurrence](../concepts/the-concurrence.md)-era crises, and the sister-fragment decisions, but for changing the political map of the world.

By helping [Viscus](../people/viscus.md) take the master goo, they turn a civilization-ending undead infection into a strange, living power centered on [Kuran Grove](../places/kuran-grove.md). Whether that is a good ending, a dangerous ending, or both is left for history to decide.

The ending also explicitly frames the early [Seventh Age](../concepts/seventh-age.md), or [Age of Heroes](../concepts/age-of-heroes.md), as an age shaped by individuals taking paths different from what fate, duty, or outside control tried to dictate.
