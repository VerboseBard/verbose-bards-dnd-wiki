# Session 2: Ash's Hounds and Norinar

## Overview

Session 2 follows the survivors of the [Bloody Fifth](../factions/bloody-fifth.md) into [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md), a specialized unit formed to hunt and capture adapting [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) after the hive link is severed.

The session begins as a special-operations power shift and ends as a moral wound: the [Norinar](../places/norinar.md) operation exposes that some [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) may be communicating, surrendering, or forming bonds with civilians.

## Key Events

- The party is pulled from the devastated Fifth and transferred to a better-equipped base on [Unity](../places/unity.md).
- [General Ash](../people/general-ash.md) forms [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md), promoting survivors and organizing them into packs.
- The organization's spoken formal name is `Unified Counter-Kreen Suppression and Rogue Hive Special Operations Group`.
- [Colonel Gandor](../people/colonel-gandor.md), an orc officer whose exact spelling remains provisional, becomes the Hounds' direct supervisor.
- Ash explains that the [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) hive connection broke when the [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) gate closed, but rogue queens or alpha minds may still rebuild.
- The party trains in small-unit tactics, logistics, officer-level planning, and specialized anti-[Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) operations.
- The first hunt reveals dungeon-created [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) copies and strange ratlike dungeon creatures at the [Dungeon Cemetery](../places/dungeon-cemetery.md).
- A real [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) surrenders, and the party prevents [Lieutenant Rethiel Kaine](../people/lieutenant-rethiel-kaine.md) from executing it.
- The preserved surrender leads to capture doctrine.
- The party later witnesses a laboratory where captured [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) are dissected and experimented on.
- [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) telepathically beg for help when touched, proving communication is possible.
- Reports emerge of villages protecting [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md), possibly due to mind control, pheromones, loyalty, or genuine contact.
- The [Norinar](../places/norinar.md) mission attempts to subdue a village and its [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) without massacre.
- [Elder Tomas Halvek](../people/elder-tomas-halvek.md) says the village requested surrender/negotiation.
- A child dies protecting a surrendered [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md), [Lieutenant Rethiel Kaine](../people/lieutenant-rethiel-kaine.md) dies in the struggle, and [Sergeant Quill](../people/sergeant-quill.md) is implicated.
- The party helps cover up the event.
- Two [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) escape [Norinar](../places/norinar.md).
- [Ash's Hounds](../factions/ashs-hounds.md) are shut down, but their captures enable [Kreen Control Collars](../items/kreen-control-collars.md).

## Consequences

Session 2 transforms the campaign from war survival into postwar moral consequence. The party's capture work makes [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) surrender possible, but the lab, [Norinar](../places/norinar.md), and the cover-up create political and personal liabilities that continue into [Session 3](session-3.md).

## Important Conversations

- [General Ash](../people/general-ash.md) explains that the [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) hive-link broke when the [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) gate closed, turning the war into a hunt for rogue minds instead of a clean victory march.
- The captured [Kreen](../factions/kreen.md) surrender scene with Lieutenant Kaine becomes a turning point, because the party argues that surrender should mean something and that intelligence matters.
- [Elder Tomas Halvek](../people/elder-tomas-halvek.md)'s question about why the party attacked when the village had asked for surrender is one of the most important moral pivots in the whole arc.
- [Sergeant Quill](../people/sergeant-quill.md) and the party's cover-up conversation shows how quickly loyalty, guilt, and self-preservation start overriding official truth.
- The colonel's shutdown explanation ties the whole operation together: the Hounds mattered, the collars worked, and the party's actions changed how the war could end.
