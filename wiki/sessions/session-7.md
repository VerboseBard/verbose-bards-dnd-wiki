# Session 7: Sunhollow Valley First Contact

## Overview

Session 7 turns [the Lost Homeland Mission](../concepts/lost-homeland-mission.md) from pathfinding into first contact. The party enters [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md), meets the valley's beastfolk patrols, discovers the staggering scale of its [Ether Crystals](../items/ether-crystals.md), and learns that the route they marked through [Driftvale](../places/driftvale.md) may already be compromised by followers from outside. At the same time, the session reveals the valley's internal crisis: a spreading blight in the [Dead Lands](../places/dead-lands.md), a council divided over secrecy and survival, and an old sacred-access rule that unexpectedly allows the party to approach [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md).

## Key Events

- The party exits the fog through a northwestern pass and sees direct sunlight for the first time in many months, confirming that [Sunhollow Valley](../places/sunhollow-valley.md) is a real sunlit basin rather than a story.
- From the overlook, they see [Lake of Glass](../places/lake-of-glass.md), multiple settled villages, enormous ether crystal growths, and a dark blighted quarter in the southern valley.
- A first-contact patrol led by [Captain Havlin](../people/captain-havlin.md) challenges the party, initially ready for force but willing to shift once [Alistair Hooley](../people/alistair-hooley.md) is identified as rabbitfolk.
- The patrol includes [Penelope Puckle](../people/penelope-puckle.md), [Voss](../people/voss-kenku.md), [Reggie Norin](../people/reggie-norin.md), and [Talin Duren](../people/talin-duren.md), all of whom become early read-points on the valley's curiosity and suspicion.
- Language barriers complicate first contact until Jefferson tries to teach through [Jose](../people/jose.md), the party's goliath who speaks Elvish, and it becomes clear that Reggie understands more than he first admits through magic.
- The party confirms that the valley's people use ether crystals casually for lamps, ornament, and household beauty without understanding the outside world's likely military and economic response.
- A dead human scout is found with some of the party's [Fog Marker Tokens](../items/fog-marker-tokens.md), confirming that secrecy around the route may already be slipping.
- The party learns that [Lake of Glass](../places/lake-of-glass.md)'s crystal growth is tied to ancient divine power connected to [Big Shad](../concepts/big-shad.md), not to ordinary ley lines.
- The group reaches [Burrowroot Warren](../places/burrowroot-warren.md), where Alistair's family name draws immediate genealogical attention from rabbit elders and reopens buried questions around the old Moonhare line.
- [Walter Wifflewick](../people/walter-wifflewick.md), rabbit lore master and old-law authority, emerges as a major political figure distinct from [Tavero Bellan](../people/tavero-bellan.md)'s formal Burrowroot seat.
- The next morning, a mixed escort takes the party from Burrowroot Warren to [Hollowmere](../places/hollowmere.md), keeping the party under official control while multiple clans observe them.
- In [Hollowmere](../places/hollowmere.md), the [Sunhollow Council](../factions/sunhollow-council.md) convenes inside an older elven chamber where runic truth-binding and language comprehension shape the proceedings.
- The council confirms the valley's deeper history: failed beastfolk uprising, drow-era custodial oaths, bloodline restrictions, and the rabbit exception to total confinement.
- The party's spider companion [Gwen](../people/gwen.md) is recognized as a valid emissary under old sacred-access law, giving the group legal-religious authority to enter the blighted forbidden lands.
- The session ends with the party being sent toward the [Dead Lands](../places/dead-lands.md) to investigate the blight and approach the temple zone.

## Important Conversations

- The patrol's early questions establish that the valley assumes outsiders are dangerous by default, but that kinship still matters politically and emotionally.
- Jefferson's attempt to teach [Voss](../people/voss-kenku.md) language through [Jose](../people/jose.md) goes badly in exactly the wrong way, turning first-contact language learning into one of the session's comic highlights through profanity-laced bad instruction.
- The party explains enough of the outside world's valuation of ether to alarm Reggie and frame the valley as a potential target for coercion, chartered exploitation, or open conquest.
- Discussion of failed false trails confirms that the marked route cannot be easily hidden anymore if the pursuers are already close behind.
- The council's account of old slavery, failed revolt, resale to elves, and inherited oaths reframes the valley as both refuge and prison.
- Walter Wifflewick's invocation of old law establishes that sacred-land access depends on an emissary and that the party now qualifies because of [Gwen](../people/gwen.md).
- Walter privately warns [Alistair Hooley](../people/alistair-hooley.md) that his return is politically dangerous, that the official history is incomplete, and that the valley's original masters were likely drow rather than unnamed elves.
- [Bevar Lurton](../people/bevar-lurton.md) reveals that the hidden item from the Session 6 ruin mechanism is a book and says he is learning what [Gwen](../people/gwen.md) means.
- [Tavero Bellan](../people/tavero-bellan.md) and [Mireya Bellan](../people/mireya-bellan.md) make hospitality feel polite but controlled, while [Isaro Velen](../people/isaro-velen.md) handles the logistical side of guest quarters, guards, and communication.
- One proposed strategic response from the party is to contact the dwarven broker from [Sin](../places/sin.md) and try to create a legal-commercial shield around the valley before outside powers simply seize it.
