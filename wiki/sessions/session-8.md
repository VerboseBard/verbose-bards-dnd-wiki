# Session 8: Ssar'Velyn Temple and Bartholomew Hildebrant

## Overview

Session 8 takes the [After the War Campaign](../campaign/after-the-war-campaign.md) from [Hollowmere](../places/hollowmere.md) through the [Dead Lands](../places/dead-lands.md) and into [Ssar'Velyn Temple](../places/ssar-velyn-temple.md).

The party learns that the temple is not only a blight source but an old sacred and legal structure with guardians, trials, inherited blood-oath consequences, and a recognized claim on [Gwen](../people/gwen.md) as an emissary. The session also introduces [Bartholomew Hildebrant](../people/bartholomew-hildebrant.md), Jack's Fourth-Age dwarf survivor from an earlier temple assault.

By the end of the session, [Bevar Lurton](../people/bevar-lurton.md) and Gwen remain with or inside the temple for unresolved emissary work. [Captain Sgt. Jose](../people/jose.md), [Alistair Hooley](../people/alistair-hooley.md), Bartholomew, and [Sgt. Jefferson Stone](../people/sergeant-jefferson-stone.md) are expelled outside after passing the trials.

## Key Events

- The party leaves Hollowmere with [Captain Havlin](../people/captain-havlin.md), [Penelope Puckle](../people/penelope-puckle.md), [Voss](../people/voss-kenku.md), and [Reggie Norin](../people/reggie-norin.md) as escort.
- Bevar grows increasingly distant and focused on Gwen, while Gwen appears to be guiding him toward the temple.
- In the Dead Lands, the party meets the [Dead Lands Ghost Druid](../people/dead-lands-ghost-druid.md), who explains that the valley was once a shared elven holy ground before [Lolth](../people/lolth.md)'s corruption.
- The ghost links Lolth's rise to [the Weaver](../concepts/the-weaver.md), the first dragon, and older divine murders connected to [Hestia](../people/hestia.md).
- The temple mosaics show a fall narrative in which beauty, peace, and protection are rejected in favor of pain, spider transformation, and deeper power.
- The [Ssar'Velyn Ghost Priestess](../people/ssar-velyn-ghost-priestess.md) recognizes Gwen as an emissary and forces the rest of the party into a survival trial.
- Jefferson tries to intervene and is removed from the active party, later returned wrapped in a cocoon.
- Jose and Alistair discover old stasis cylinders and recover Bartholomew Hildebrant, still alive from a Fourth-Age assault.
- Bartholomew cannot walk because of ruined knees, so he joins the trials by riding on Jose's shoulders.
- The party clears multiple trial rounds against drow guardians, casters, summons, necromancy, and hazardous temple terrain.
- [The Ssar'Velyn Temple Guardian](../people/ssar-velyn-temple-guardian.md), a massive drider or spider-bodied figure, serves as the final trial and stops the fight once the party has proven itself.
- The guardian heals the party through blood magic, returns Jefferson, and presents the moral problem: destroying the temple may also doom thousands of valley inhabitants bound by ancestral oath.

## Important Conversations

- The ghost druid's account reframes the Dead Lands as a corrupted sacred-forest bond rather than merely an infected wilderness.
- The temple lore connects Lolth, the Weaver, the first dragon, Hestia, and the old elven treaty halls into one deeper Fourth-Age conflict.
- The guardian's ultimatum complicates the party's original mission: the valley's ether wealth will draw outside powers, but the oath-bound inhabitants may be forced to defend the temple even if they do not know the full truth.
- The rabbit exception is clarified: not all rabbitfolk are bound in the same way because their population outgrew the original oath structure.
